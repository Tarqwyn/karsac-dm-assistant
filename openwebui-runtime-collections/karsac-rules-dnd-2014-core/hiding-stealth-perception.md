# Hiding, Stealth, Perception

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/hiding-stealth-perception`

**Retrieval Summary:** Hiding uses Dexterity (Stealth) against active or passive Wisdom (Perception), but the DM decides whether the circumstances allow hiding in the first place.

## Rule Summary

You cannot hide from a creature that can see you clearly, and noise or exposure can give your position away.

## Procedure

1. The DM decides whether hiding is possible.
2. Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to hide.
3. Compare that result against active Wisdom (Perception) checks from creatures searching for you.
4. If no one is actively searching, compare against passive Wisdom (Perception).
5. If you remain hidden, attackers who cannot see you may suffer disadvantage, and you may gain advantage against creatures that cannot see you.

## At the Table

Use this for ambushes, sneaking, unseen attacks, scouting, and questions about whether someone notices a hidden creature.

## Common Questions

### What is passive Perception?

It is `10 + modifiers`, with `+5` for advantage and `-5` for disadvantage.

### Can an invisible creature hide automatically?

No. It can always try to hide, but it still has to avoid making noise and can still be noticed by tracks or other signs.
