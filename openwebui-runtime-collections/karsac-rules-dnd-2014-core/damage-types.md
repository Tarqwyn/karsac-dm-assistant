# Damage Types

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/damage-types`

**Retrieval Summary:** Damage types label the form of harm an effect deals and matter mainly because other rules refer to resistance, vulnerability, or immunity by type.

## Rule Summary

Damage types do not change damage on their own. They matter because creatures, objects, and features care about specific types.

## Procedure

1. Identify the damage type named by the attack, spell, or effect.
2. Roll or apply the damage.
3. Apply any other modifiers first.
4. Apply resistance or vulnerability to that damage type afterward.
5. Remember that multiple instances of resistance or vulnerability to the same type do not stack.

## At the Table

Use this when assigning damage for hazards, house effects, or improvised attacks, or when checking whether a creature's defenses apply.

## Common Questions

### Does a damage type have built-in extra rules?

No. The type mostly matters because other rules refer to it.

### In what order do modifiers apply?

Apply ordinary modifiers first, then resistance or vulnerability.
