# Concentration

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/concentration`

**Retrieval Summary:** A concentration spell lasts only while the caster maintains concentration, and damage or incapacitation can end it.

## Rule Summary

You can concentrate on only one spell at a time, and some events force a Constitution saving throw to keep it.

## Procedure

1. Check whether the spell's Duration entry says it requires concentration.
2. Start concentration when the spell takes effect.
3. End concentration if you cast another concentration spell.
4. When you take damage, make a Constitution saving throw with DC 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher.
5. Make a separate save for each damage source.
6. End concentration if you become incapacitated or die.

## At the Table

Use this whenever a caster is hit, starts another concentration spell, or tries to hold a readied spell.

## Common Questions

### Can I move and attack while concentrating?

Yes. Normal activity does not break concentration on its own.

### Can the environment force a concentration check?

Yes. The DM can call for a DC 10 Constitution save when environmental shocks would disrupt focus.
