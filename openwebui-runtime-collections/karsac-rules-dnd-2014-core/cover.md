# Cover

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/cover`

**Retrieval Summary:** Cover makes a target harder to hit or affect, with half cover giving +2, three-quarters cover giving +5, and total cover blocking direct targeting.

## Rule Summary

Only the most protective degree of cover applies, and cover matters when the attack or effect comes from the opposite side of the obstacle.

## Procedure

1. Check whether an obstacle lies between source and target.
2. Determine the best degree of cover the obstacle provides.
3. Apply the bonus or restriction:
4. Half cover: `+2 AC` and `+2` on Dexterity saving throws.
5. Three-quarters cover: `+5 AC` and `+5` on Dexterity saving throws.
6. Total cover: the target cannot be targeted directly by the attack or spell.

## At the Table

Use this for walls, trees, furniture, creatures in the way, arrow slits, and similar battlefield obstructions.

## Common Questions

### Do multiple cover sources stack?

No. Use only the strongest one.

### Can an area effect reach a target behind total cover?

Sometimes. Total cover blocks direct targeting, but an area effect can still include the space if its own targeting and origin rules allow it.
