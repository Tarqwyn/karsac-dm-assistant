# Vision and Light

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/vision-and-light`

**Retrieval Summary:** Light, darkness, and special senses determine what creatures can perceive and whether an area is lightly or heavily obscured.

## Rule Summary

Bright light allows normal sight, dim light creates a lightly obscured area, and darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Special senses can change that.

## Procedure

1. Determine the area's illumination: bright light, dim light, or darkness.
2. Determine whether the area is lightly or heavily obscured.
3. Apply sight penalties:
4. Lightly obscured: disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.
5. Heavily obscured: creatures effectively suffer the blinded condition when trying to see through it.
6. Check whether special senses such as blindsight, darkvision, tremorsense, or truesight change what the creature can perceive.

## At the Table

Use this for darkness, fog, foliage, underground scenes, invisible foes, and edge cases around special senses.

## Common Questions

### What does darkvision do?

It lets a creature see in darkness as if it were dim light within the listed range, but only in shades of gray.

### What does tremorsense need?

The creature and the vibration source must be in contact with the same ground or substance, and it does not detect flying or incorporeal creatures.
