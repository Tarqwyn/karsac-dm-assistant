# Death Saving Throws

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/death-saving-throws`

**Retrieval Summary:** A creature at 0 hit points that is not dead or stable makes a death saving throw at the start of each of its turns.

## Rule Summary

Death saves track whether a creature at 0 hit points dies, stabilizes, or returns to consciousness.

## Procedure

1. At the start of the turn, if the creature has 0 hit points and is not stable, roll a d20.
2. A result of 10 or higher is one success. A result below 10 is one failure.
3. Three successes make the creature stable.
4. Three failures kill the creature.
5. A natural 1 counts as two failures.
6. A natural 20 restores 1 hit point.

## At the Table

Use this for player characters and special NPCs that follow the full dying rules instead of dying immediately at 0 hit points.

## Common Questions

### What happens if the creature takes damage at 0 hit points?

It suffers one failed death save, or two if the hit was a critical hit. Damage equal to or greater than the creature's hit point maximum causes instant death.

### How do you stabilize someone without healing?

Use an action to attempt a DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine) check. A stable creature stays unconscious at 0 hit points and regains 1 hit point after `1d4` hours if not healed first.
