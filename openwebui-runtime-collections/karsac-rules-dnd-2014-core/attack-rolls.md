# Attack Rolls

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/attack-rolls`

**Retrieval Summary:** Attack rolls determine whether a weapon attack or spell attack hits a target's Armor Class.

## Rule Summary

An attack resolves in three steps: choose a target, determine modifiers, and roll against AC.

## Procedure

1. Choose a target within range: a creature, object, or location.
2. Determine modifiers, including cover and advantage or disadvantage.
3. Roll a d20 and add the relevant ability modifier.
4. Add proficiency if the attacker is proficient with the weapon or is making a spell attack.
5. If the total equals or exceeds AC, the attack hits.
6. On a hit, roll damage and apply any extra effects.

## At the Table

Use this for melee weapon attacks, ranged weapon attacks, unarmed strikes, and spells that call for an attack roll.

## Common Questions

### What ability modifier applies?

Melee weapon attacks usually use Strength, ranged weapon attacks usually use Dexterity, and spell attacks use the caster's spellcasting ability.

### What do a natural 1 and natural 20 do?

A natural 20 always hits and is a critical hit. A natural 1 always misses.
