# The Dark Captains

**Canon File ID:** `forces/the-captains`

> Old intelligences in long relationship with Maharuq; distinct from the Yantravaq; predate the Hiding; currently moving artefacts north toward the Yngondi.

## Overview

The Captains are not gods. They are not primordial forces. They are intelligences — old ones, older than living memory, older than most of Exandrian history — that have been in relationship with Maharuq since before Karsac was a continent. They are something like expressions: instruments that Maharuq's long pressure has shaped over millennia into forms that serve its direction without requiring its instruction.

They are distinct from the Yantravaq. The Yantravaq are three, named, and described in Appendix C. The Captains are a different entity class — older in their relationship to Maharuq, different in their method, and as yet unidentified by name or number.

This entity is not yet known to the players.

## Description

### Nature

The Captains are neither servants in the way lieutenants are servants, nor worshippers in the way a cleric worships. Something more like expressions — shaped by proximity into forms that serve Maharuq's direction without requiring instruction. They have been in Karsac since the continent formed. They were present when the gods looked at Karsac and flinched away.

### Knowledge of Exandria

The Captains know Exandria in detail that should not be possible given the Hiding. This is because they predate the Hiding — they had knowledge of and agents in what would become Exandria before Dhurvaq's arrangement took hold, and they have maintained those connections carefully, through channels too old and too subtle for the Hiding's mechanisms to identify and close. They are patient. They have had centuries to find the gaps.

### Current Activity

They are currently:
- Moving artefacts from the Stormwatch Mountains north toward the Yngondi Wastes.
- Positioning their lieutenants in key locations across Karsac and into Exandria.
- Working through the political structures of Lösweg — which intersects with, but is distinct from, Vishara's operation through Mathr.

Their preparation is for a world in which Maharuq has a *here* that is fully expressed rather than partially contained. They do not know exactly what that world will look like. They are ensuring that when it arrives, they are positioned to shape it.

## DM-Only

### Identity Withheld

The Captains' identity — who or what they are individually — is withheld in all available sources. The Sourcebook's author cannot fully answer what their relationship to Maharuq is. The inventory marks identity as withheld. This is not an ambiguity; it is the canonical state. Do not invent names or individual identities for the Captains without a future source document authorising it.

### Relationship to the Yantravaq

The two entity classes are distinct. The Sourcebook notes the Captains are something like expressions shaped by Maharuq's long pressure — analogous in mechanism to how the Yantravaq were shaped, but different in product. The Yantravaq have specific named principles (Dissipation, Irreversibility, Equilibrium). The Captains have no specified principles — they are intelligences in a different register. Historically, the Captains have found the Betrayer Gods as obstructive as any other force — which suggests they operate from a position of pure Maharuq-direction rather than any theological alignment.

### Discovery Path

Section VIII is suggested for Valweg discovery (Chapter 3). Players may never meet the Captains directly in Campaign 1. Their presence is felt through the operational evidence: the artefact movements, the Shadow-Walker network, the Exandrian reach. Named and framed only in Valweg.

## Ambiguities

- The number of Captains is not specified anywhere in the corpus.
- Their individual identities are withheld by design — this is not a gap to fill but a canonical limit.
- The relationship between the Captains' lieutenant network and the Shadow-Walkers is implied but not fully mapped.
