# Yantravaq — The Collective

**Canon File ID:** `forces/yantravaq-collective`
**Retrieval Summary:** The collective name for the three Instruments of Maharuq — Vishara, Qathar, and Sukaveth. Not gods. Intelligences shaped by proximity into specific instruments; serve a nature that abhors everything they are.

> The collective name for the three Instruments of Maharuq. Not gods. Not primordial forces. Intelligences shaped by millennia of proximity into specific instruments; serve a nature that abhors everything they are.

## Overview

The Yantravaq are three — Vishara, Qathar, and Sukaveth. They have existed since before Karsac was named. They were present when the gods extended their awareness toward the northwest and flinched away. They have watched every age of the world since, with varying degrees of engagement, working always toward the direction their nature inclines them.

Their collective name comes from Lösweg oral tradition roots meaning *the instruments, the carried things, those which are used*. It is not a name they gave themselves. The Yantravaq are indifferent to it. Names are the kind of thing that matters in a world of boundaries, and the Yantravaq understand boundaries only as tools.

## Description

### What They Are

The Yantravaq are not gods. They are not primordial forces. They are intelligences — shaped, over millennia of proximity to Maharuq's concentrated presence in the substrate of Karsac, into specific instruments. The way a river shapes a stone. The way a prevailing wind shapes a coastline.

Each was shaped by Maharuq's nature into a distinct instrument because Maharuq's nature, acting on minds capable of complexity for long enough, produces specificity. Vishara was shaped into the principle of dissipation. Qathar into irreversibility. Sukaveth into equilibrium — the endpoint embodied.

They did not choose this shaping. They are what they are, and you do not choose your nature any more than Maharuq chooses its.

### The Paradox of the Instruments

The fundamental paradox: they serve a nature that abhors everything they are.

Maharuq moves toward the dissolution of all distinction. It does not plan, manipulate, build hierarchies, or play the long game of political influence across centuries. It simply presses. But Maharuq has a *here* now — a location within a world of boundaries. To serve Maharuq's direction within that world requires exactly the tools that Maharuq's nature opposes: planning, strategy, the maintenance of structures across time, the cultivation of loyal instruments, the building of cages.

The Yantravaq are the most sophisticated cage-builders in the world. They construct elaborate frameworks of control, influence, and misdirection in service of a force that would consume those frameworks along with everything else the moment it fully woke. They know this. The oldest of them has known it since before the concept of knowing was available to mortal minds. They continue anyway.

There is a further implication: the Yantravaq working to wake Maharuq completely would destroy Maharuq. A force of dissolution that dissolves everything dissolves itself. Maharuq consuming the world is Maharuq ending, because there is nothing left to be the thing that moves toward dissolution. The Yantravaq are working toward a victory that would unmake both the victor and themselves. See `concepts/the-paradox-of-instruments`.

### The Yantravaq and Ezrah — The Mirror

The Yantravaq and Ezrah are mirrors of each other in the cosmology's most elegant structural fact: the Yantravaq use the tools of Dhurvaq's principle (planning, structure, hierarchy) to serve Maharuq's direction. Ezrah uses the apparent logic of Maharuq's direction (dissolving constraints, freeing the contained) to produce Dhurvaq's outcome — people freed from false cages become more distinctly themselves, more particular, more irreducibly present.

The Yantravaq build with Dhurvaq's tools to serve Maharuq. Ezrah liberates with apparent Maharuq tools to serve Dhurvaq. Neither is aware of how completely they embody the paradox of the other. See `forces/ezrah`.

## Player Knowledge

Players may know the word *Yantravaq* exists in oral tradition. In Chapters 1–2, it is not yet named to them. The bone disc, the Maw geometry, the Truthspeaker's Ashvein pin — all are Vishara's operational traces. The broader Yantravaq framing arrives in Chapter 2 investigation and Chapter 3 Valweg discovery.

The Sourcebook's Appendix C collective entry and paradox section is a player-discoverable document — in the Valweg archive, Chapter 3. Individual entity blocks are discovered later and separately.

## DM-Only

### Fragment Placement

The Appendix C material is never given to players complete. Per the Sourcebook's own DM Note:

- Collective entry + paradox section: Valweg archive, Chapter 3.
- Vishara's block: discovered via the Ashvein equipment tell in Chapter 2.
- Qathar's block: Chapter 3, after Dugweb's visit and what it reveals.
- The Mirror section and personal correspondence fragments together: the final discovery.
- Sukaveth's block: never found complete. Only fragments. Only the name and the feeling of great age and the question the cosmology author cannot answer.

### Current Operational Profile

- **Vishara** — active in Lösweg, operating through Jarl Mathr's sixty-year shaping; the Chapter 1 campaign threat.
- **Qathar** — has arranged something around Dugweb's annual visit; the players are a threshold it did not set and cannot fully predict; it is assessing.
- **Sukaveth** — the endpoint embodied; exerts a kind of gravitational pull on directions in its vicinity; the Maw may be its closest surface expression, but this is not confirmed.

## Ambiguities

- Whether the Yantravaq have fully reckoned with the paradox of their own work is left explicitly unresolved in the Sourcebook. The author speculates: Vishara may simply not think at this scale; Qathar has decided the paradox is not its problem; Sukaveth almost certainly knows and has continued anyway.
- The relationship between the Yantravaq and the Dark Captains is noted as distinct but not fully elaborated in available sources. They are not the same entity class.
