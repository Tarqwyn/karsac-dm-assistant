# Sukaveth

**Canon File ID:** `forces/sukaveth`
**Retrieval Summary:** Third Instrument of the Yantravaq; Principle of Equilibrium; the oldest; the endpoint embodied; players should finish Campaign 1 knowing it exists — not what it is.

> Third Instrument of the Yantravaq; Principle of Equilibrium; the oldest; the endpoint embodied.

## Overview

Sukaveth is the instrument of equilibrium — not balance in the dynamic sense, but the endpoint of entropy: the featureless sameness that is the destination of all dissipation and all irreversible change. The heat death. The long quiet. The state where everything is the same temperature, the same density, evenly distributed, nothing more concentrated than anything else anywhere. Not violent. Not dramatic. Simply complete.

It is the oldest of the three Instruments. It was present when the gods extended their awareness toward Karsac and felt Maharuq and flinched. It has been in the world since before the concept of the world fully applied.

This entity is not yet known to the players beyond fragments and a name.

## Description

### Nature

Sukaveth is closest in nature to Maharuq of the three Instruments — not because it is more powerful, but because it has been shaped by proximity into something that most completely embodies the destination. Where Vishara and Qathar are instruments in the active sense — things that do things — Sukaveth is an instrument in the passive sense. A demonstration. A proof that the destination is real, that the equilibrium is achievable, that the long quiet is not metaphor.

### Method

Sukaveth does not act in the way the other two act. Vishara moves. Qathar arranges. Sukaveth waits, and in waiting exerts a kind of gravitational pull — not on physical objects but on directions. Things in its presence tend to move toward stillness. Disputes resolve into stasis rather than resolution. Ambitions flatten. The particular becomes the general without anyone being able to say exactly how.

It is the most dangerous of the three not because of what it does but because of what it is. Being near Sukaveth is being near the destination.

### Appearance

The Sourcebook's author found no reliable account of Sukaveth's direct appearance. Every description that approaches it becomes vague at the crucial moment, as though the observer's attention, having reached the thing, found nothing specific to report. What is consistent across all accounts: an impression of great age; an impression of patience that has outlasted the concept of patience; an impression, afterward, that whatever you were worried about before the encounter matters somewhat less than it did.

### The Question the Author Cannot Answer

Sukaveth is the endpoint. It embodies the state that Maharuq's full waking would produce. It is, in a sense, already where everything is going. And it is still here, still distinct, still a thing that is not other things — still, in spite of everything, a self. The Sourcebook author has no explanation for this. The author suspects Sukaveth does not either, and that this is, for something that has existed as long as Sukaveth has, the closest thing it has to a question.

## Player Knowledge

Players should finish Campaign 1 knowing Sukaveth exists. They should not finish knowing what it is.

What they may encounter:

- The name, in the oldest oral traditions of Lösweg, Yngondi, and Zörsdkog alike — in contexts that suggest both reverence and terror.
- A quality in certain places — the stillness at the centre of the Maw, the silence the oldest sailor has kept since seeing the cargo — that may or may not be Sukaveth's presence.
- The fact, eventually, that the Yantravaq number three and the third is older than the other two.

They receive no entity block, no explanation of its method, no description of its appearance. Only fragments. Only the name and the feeling.

## DM-Only

### Relationship to the Maw

The inventory cross-reference edge notes that `places/the-maw` → `forces/sukaveth` is "possibly — Sourcebook App C is explicit that the link is suggestive only." The stillness at the Maw's centre is consistent with Sukaveth's gravitational pull on directions, but the Sourcebook does not confirm a direct relationship. The DM should treat the Maw as Sukaveth-adjacent without committing to Sukaveth's direct presence there. The ambiguity is the correct state.

### Discovery Path — Never Complete

Sukaveth's entity block is never found complete. Only fragments. Campaign 3 concerns Sukaveth, but even then the players should not finish knowing what it is. The Sourcebook's DM placement note: "Only fragments. Only the name and the feeling of great age and the question the author cannot answer."

### The Paradox

Sukaveth is the endpoint. It embodies the state Maharuq's full waking would produce. And it is still here, still distinct, still a self. This is the thing the cosmology author cannot explain. The DM should not explain it either. It is the final open question of the cosmology.

## Ambiguities

- The Maw / Sukaveth connection is explicitly marked as suggestive only in the Sourcebook. Do not treat it as confirmed.
- Sukaveth's entity block is deliberately incomplete even in DM-layer sources. The incompleteness is canonical.
