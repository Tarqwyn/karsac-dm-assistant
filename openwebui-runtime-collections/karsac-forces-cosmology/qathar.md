# Qathar

**Canon File ID:** `forces/qathar`

> Second Instrument of the Yantravaq; Principle of Irreversibility; works in centuries; arranging historical hinges; the players are a threshold it did not arrange and cannot fully predict.

## Overview

Qathar is the instrument of irreversibility — the principle that certain changes cannot be undone, that the past cannot be recovered, that time has a direction and some crossings change you permanently. Qathar does not change things. It arranges for the right things to be changed at the right moments, so that the world's own momentum carries everything forward and nothing back.

Qathar is primarily a Campaign 2 threat. In Campaign 1, it operates in the background — its sixty-year arrangement around Lösweg's political structure is visible in effects, but its identity and method are not yet revealed to players.

This entity is not yet known to the players.

## Description

### Nature and Method

Qathar works in centuries. It identifies the hinges of history — moments where a small application of pressure produces an irreversible change in the direction of everything downstream. It applies that pressure with precision, then waits. Extraordinarily patient even by the standards of the Yantravaq, because it has to be: the hinges of history are separated by long stretches of time in which nothing Qathar does matters. It has learned to find those stretches restful.

### Appearance

Qathar has the quality of a threshold. People who have met it describe an encounter that felt, at the time, like a decision point — as though they were being offered a choice. In retrospect they cannot identify what the choice was or which option they selected. Only that afterward something had changed that had not changed before, and the before was no longer accessible.

### Current Activity — The Sixty-Year Arrangement

Qathar has been arranging something in the political structure of Lösweg for the past sixty years. The specific nature of what it has arranged is not yet fully visible in the available sources — the Sourcebook author is deliberate about withholding it as a Campaign 2 reveal. What is clear:

- Jarl Mathr's sixty-year shaping by Vishara has been simultaneously running alongside a separate Qathar arrangement.
- King Dugweb's approaching annual visit represents a threshold Qathar has been preparing. Whichever way the visit falls, Qathar has worked to ensure the fall is permanent.
- Qathar has managed something around Dugweb's visits for sixty years. The mechanism of that management is unspecified in current sources.

### The Players as Threshold

The players are themselves a potential hinge that Qathar did not arrange and cannot fully predict. A threshold it did not set. This is the thing that most unsettles Qathar, insofar as Qathar can be unsettled. It is assessing.

This is the DM's key leverage: Qathar is active in Chapter 2, watching. Its presence near the south dock confrontation and near the Valweg events is felt as a quality — an encounter that feels like a decision point — but is not yet visible as a named entity.

## DM-Only

### Discovery Path

Qathar's entity block is a Chapter 3 discovery — after Dugweb's visit and what it reveals. The Yantravaq collective entry and paradox section arrives in Valweg archive (Chapter 3). Qathar is named and framed only then.

In Chapter 2, the DM may play Qathar's presence as an ambient quality: moments that feel like decision points. The south dock confrontation — with the Truthspeaker arriving bearing council authority, with Aldric Vane being escorted away — has the quality of a Qathar hinge. Not announced. Simply: the before is no longer accessible.

### The Paradox

Qathar works to ensure things cannot be walked back from. It is the architect of irreversibility. But a fully woken Maharuq would dissolve the distinction between before and after — dissolve the directionality of time itself, which is what gives irreversibility meaning. Qathar is arranging for a world in which its own principle becomes incoherent. Per the Sourcebook's author: Qathar has decided this is not its problem.

### Relationship to the Dugweb Visit

The storyarch specifies that Qathar has been arranging something around Dugweb's annual visit for sixty years and that all three Instruments have an interest in what the Air Ashari hold, guard, carry, or are. The connection between Qathar's arrangement and the Air Ashari thread is not specified in current sources — flag for Campaign 2 development.

## Ambiguities

- The precise nature of Qathar's sixty-year arrangement in Lösweg is deliberately withheld in the Sourcebook as a Campaign 2 reveal. The canon file preserves this withholding.
- Whether Qathar arranged the Mathr family appearance in 1263 or whether that was Vishara's work alone is not specified. Sixty years is Vishara's frame; 1263 is over 700 years ago. The two arrangements may be distinct operations at different scales.
