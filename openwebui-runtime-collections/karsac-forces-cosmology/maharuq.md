# Maharuq

**Canon File ID:** `forces/maharuq`
**Retrieval Summary:** The primordial principle of dissolution; the direction toward undifferentiation; not evil, not hungry — moves as entropy moves.

> The primordial principle of dissolution; the direction toward undifferentiation; not evil, not hungry — moves as entropy moves.

## Overview

Maharuq is one of two primordial conditions that predate the gods, consciousness, and the concept of time as living things experience it. It is the force that moves toward the dissolution of boundaries — the consuming of the distinction between one thing and another. The Lösweg oral traditions call it the Deep. The Sourcebook's cosmology author calls it the older feeling. Neither phrase is incorrect, and neither is complete.

It is not a being. It does not decide. It does not want. It moves as gravity moves, as entropy moves, as the tide moves toward the sea — not because it chose to, but because that is its nature.

## Description

### Nature

Maharuq is the direction toward undifferentiation. The Lösweg oral histories approach it through accumulated images rather than definitions: the deep water that has no bottom; the dark that exists before light has been conceived of; the patience of the thing that knows it will outlast everything that tries to hold it back, because it is not trying and does not need to.

Crucially, it is not evil. Evil requires intention — the making of a choice to do harm when another choice was available. Maharuq does not choose. The comparison to hunger is imprecise: hunger implies a state of wanting that could in principle be satisfied. Maharuq is not satisfied by consuming. It continues.

### Relationship to the World

Before the gods extended their awareness across what they were making, Maharuq was everywhere and nowhere — a property of the world's substrate rather than a location within it. When the gods' perception touched it, it was given a *here*. Land rose. The continent of Karsac formed — not because the gods willed a continent, but because the world had learned from the gods that where things are most concentrated is a place, and places have land.

This is the act that made Karsac. Not intention. Perception.

Since that moment, Maharuq has had a location. A place where its pressure is most concentrated. The Stormwatch Mountains above Törweg sit above the deepest part of that concentration.

### The Waking

Maharuq does not sleep. What the oral traditions call sleeping and waking are the difference between a pressure that is stable and a pressure that is increasing. The current age is one of increasing pressure — the Waking. The sealed location in the Stormwatch Mountains opened itself three months before the campaign's Chapter 2 events because the pressure from below had increased to the point where the seal could no longer hold. See `forces/the-waking`.

The Yantravaq are not causing the Waking. They are responding to it — positioning themselves for a world in which Maharuq is fully expressed.

### Relationship to Dhurvaq

Maharuq and Dhurvaq are not at war. The familiar framework of two opposing forces implies that one could win. Neither can. Neither can be destroyed. Maharuq without Dhurvaq is not a world. Dhurvaq without Maharuq has no purpose. The world is the tension between them.

## Method

Maharuq does not act. It presses. The agents who serve its direction — the Yantravaq — are the ones who act. Maharuq's method is the method of entropy itself: it requires no intelligence and no intention. Disordered states are more numerous than ordered ones. Things tend toward them. This is sufficient.

## Player Knowledge

Players in Chapter 2 have access to the following player-safe information about Maharuq:

- The name exists in the oldest stratum of Karsac oral tradition.
- The Lösweg people speak of something old beneath the land — something the gods looked at and turned away from.
- Floki, the crew chief of the Greyback, has said the bone disc symbol is "impossible" — and has not explained why.
- The Maw above Törweg has a stillness that is not natural stillness.

Players do not yet know Maharuq by name as a cosmological entity. The name arrives in Session 3 via the First Speaking fragment.

## DM-Only

### Full Cosmological Scope

The Sourcebook's Part IV is explicit that Maharuq predates the concept of old. It was present before the gods, before consciousness, before the idea of place applied to the world. Its current waking is not an event being caused by any agent — it is a process the agents are trying to position themselves relative to.

The Yantravaq serve Maharuq's nature — dissolution of all distinction — using the tools of Dhurvaq's principle: planning, hierarchy, structures built across time. This paradox is the deepest structural fact of the cosmology. A fully woken Maharuq would dissolve the Yantravaq along with everything else. See `concepts/the-paradox-of-instruments`.

### Maharuq and the Captains

The Dark Captains are intelligences that have been in relationship with Maharuq since before Karsac was a continent. They are not the Yantravaq. They predate the Hiding and have maintained connections to Exandria through channels too old and too subtle for the Hiding's mechanisms to identify. Their current activity — moving artefacts from the Stormwatch Mountains, positioning agents — is preparation. See `forces/the-captains`.

### Discovery Path

Maharuq as a named cosmological entity arrives for players via the First Speaking fragment in Session 3. Full understanding of its nature arrives in fragments across Sessions 4–6. The Appendix C Yantravaq entry is never found complete in player-facing material — only fragments, always through discovery.

## Ambiguities

- The Sourcebook's cosmology author notes that the "older feeling" claim — that Maharuq is the older of the two primordials — is the unanimous testimony of every tradition that has preserved knowledge of it, and acknowledges this is a claim difficult to verify. The canon treats it as true.
- Whether Maharuq registers the Yantravaq's work in any sense analogous to awareness is explicitly left unresolved in the Sourcebook. The canon follows suit.
