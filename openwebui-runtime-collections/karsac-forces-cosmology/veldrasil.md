# Veldrasil

## Overview

Veldrasil is the centre of the Löswegiann cosmological tradition. The word encompasses tree, spine, and axis simultaneously — a single term for the thing upon which all realms are hung as fruit upon branches. To the Löswegiann, Veldrasil is not metaphor or symbol. It is the literal structural fact of the universe.

## Description

### The World-Tree

Veldrasil's roots descend into three wells of tremendous significance: the Well of Origin, the Well of Fate, and the Well of Knowing. These wells are not decorative — they are the cosmological anchors of the Löswegiann understanding of existence.

The Well of Knowing is of particular theological importance because it is the site of the All-Father Vorath's defining sacrifice. Vorath surrendered one of his eyes to the keeper of the Well in exchange for a draught of its waters — seeking wisdom and paying for it permanently. The Löswegiann read in this story not tragedy but instruction: that true understanding requires the willingness to be permanently diminished in pursuit of it.

### What It Means

The Vushhiri faculty observes that the Löswegiann relationship to their cosmological tradition is not one of symbolic interpretation. The World-Tree is not a metaphor for interconnection or growth. It is the structural fact of the universe — a description of what existence literally is, not what it resembles.

This distinction shapes every aspect of how the Löswegiann engage with knowledge, sacrifice, and death. What happens in Ragnarholm will happen because the structure of the universe requires it, not because a story says so.

## Atmosphere & Themes

Veldrasil is what Vishara's dissipation threatens at the cosmological scale. The Löswegiann tradition that names the World-Tree correctly, that holds its structure in the specific verse, in the exact ritual form — that tradition is what keeps the tree real. A Lösweg where no skald can speak the old metres is a Lösweg where Veldrasil is less fully itself, not because the tree has changed but because the people who participate in its existence have lost the capacity to do so.

This is the deepest expression of the Karsac thematic: *to name something correctly is to participate in its existence*.
