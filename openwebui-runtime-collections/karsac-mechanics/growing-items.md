# Growing Magic Items — PC Artefacts

**Canon File ID:** `mechanics/growing-items`
**Retrieval Summary:** Design principles and growth tables for the four PC growing artefacts; level opens the door, story turns the key; Ravenwake, The Black Question, Caeli's Vigil, The Oath-Ring of Veyr.

> Level opens the door; story turns the key. Four items that reveal why they matter, not just what they do.

## Design Principles

- Items do not gain automatically on level-up — each awakening needs a story trigger.
- Early growth is sideways: names, senses, reactions, recognition before raw attack bonuses.
- +3 is the practical ceiling; a legendary item changes what is possible rather than just adding bigger numbers.
- Each item solves a specific story problem once per arc: not more damage, but permission to do something otherwise impossible.

## Growth Tables

Key awakening triggers (condensed):
- **Ravenwake Named (Level 5):** Rowan protects someone when vengeance would be easier.
- **The Black Question Named (Level 5):** Xyrrathh preserves evidence others would destroy.
- **Caeli's Vigil Named (Level 5):** Korvann protects someone from undead, shadow, or a false trail.
- **Oath-Ring Named (Level 5):** Ragnfrid uses verse to stop violence, reveal truth, or make someone keep faith.

## DM Running Notes

When an item awakens, describe perception first: warmth, pressure, sound, wind, grain, old verse, silence. Never announce: "your item has levelled up."

Let names feel discovered rather than awarded.

Do not give +2 before level 9–11. At 5 and 8–9, prefer narrative or once-per-rest utility.
