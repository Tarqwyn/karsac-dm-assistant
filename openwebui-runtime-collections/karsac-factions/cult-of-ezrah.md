# The Cult of Ezrah (Erik's Network)

**Canon File ID:** `factions/cult-of-ezrah`
**Retrieval Summary:** Erik Mathr's resistance network; clerics of Ezrah whose work is the naming of cages; operating inside Vishara's political structure from within.

> Erik's resistance network; clerics whose work is the naming of cages; operating inside Mathr's political structure to create the conditions for people to walk free.

## Overview

The "Cult of Ezrah" is the inventory's working name for a small, decentralised network of Ezrah clerics operating in Lösweg, centred on the figure known as Erik — a former companion who walked toward Vushhir five years ago and did not come back. They do not call themselves a cult. They operate through the theological discipline of Ezrah: seeing cages before anything else, finding the lock, making sure that when it opens the person inside chooses to walk through.

This entity is not yet known to the players.

## Description

### Mission and Method

A priest of Ezrah sees cages before they see anything else — the shape of the constraint, the fear that built it, the moment when the person inside stopped knowing it was a cage and started calling it home. Erik applied this capacity immediately to the political structure of Lösweg: the cage Vishara has built through Mathr is subtle, slow, and effective precisely because it does not feel like a cage from inside.

Erik's work, for five years, has been: identifying who can still be reached, creating conditions under which information can flow, keeping strategic threads alive for the moment when someone from outside the cage arrives. He has been patient with the patience of a man who understands that you cannot open a cage by pulling on the bars.

### Current Operations

**Pell Duvash** — kept in place in Törweg by a note: *"Stay, and tell the truth to whoever comes."* Duvash knows more than is safe. Erik kept him there specifically for the moment someone arrived asking the right questions.

**Erwing's message** — Erwing Smallfoot was given a coded message by a Cobalt monk (connected to Erik's network) who was discovered. The decoded text: *"Get word to the Cleric. I am discovered, trust no one, continue the work. It is now more important than ever."* Serris does not know who the Cleric is. The players may piece it together. See `items/the-coded-message`.

**Erik himself** — his location in Session 2 is not specified in available sources. He has not yet surfaced directly.

### Relationship to the House

Erik operates under the Mathr name as a strategic identity — the house is constructed, not a blood lineage, and he adopted the name to work within Mathr's network from inside. This gives him access that he uses against the network's purposes. He knows what the house is. He is one of perhaps three people alive who do.

## DM-Only

### The Dhurvaq Echo

Erik does not know that his god may be Dhurvaq's echo in the divine register. He does not know the cosmological architecture. He does what his theology requires of him, in the situation he has been given, as well as he possibly can. The Sourcebook's author finds this, in the end, the most moving thing in the entire account.

If the players eventually connect Erik's work to the Dhurvaq-Ezrah relationship, they will understand: a man trained as a cleric is doing the right thing, in the right place, at the right time, for reasons he would recognise without needing to understand all of this. Dhurvaq, if it has something like a feeling about anything, probably finds this almost pleasing.

### Canonical Status

The "Cult of Ezrah" label is the inventory's working designation — `canonical: speculative` because the network's full scope, membership beyond Erik and the Halvash monk, and internal structure are not specified in available sources. What is confirmed: Erik exists, the network exists, Pell Duvash is a node, the coded message is in motion.
