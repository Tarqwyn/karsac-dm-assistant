# The Shadow-Walkers

**Canon File ID:** `factions/shadow-walkers`
**Retrieval Summary:** Vishara's operational asset class in the field; carry Ashvein equipment; trace their deepest roots to the proto-Yngondi cultures; tied to the rune Korvann has followed for ten years.

> Vishara's operational asset class; carry Ashvein spikes and the oldest Vishara rune; killed the boy in the Halvash alley; two escaped with the Pryzi key.

## Overview

The Shadow-Walkers are the operational field asset class for Vishara's network — the figures in the field who do the work that requires direct action. They are not Vishara's willing servants in the theological sense (the Threshold-Caught). They are operatives. They carry standardised equipment: Ashvein throwing spikes, the black metal mined beneath Halvash. They carry Vishara's oldest symbol — though they do not know what it is at its deepest root. They carry it because their tradition carries it.

## Description

### Equipment Tell

The standardised kit — Ashvein spikes, the rune — is the Sourcebook's Appendix C discovery path for identifying Vishara's operational signature. The same metal as the Truthspeaker's pin. The same rune as the bone disc. Players who notice the connection between the Halvash alley equipment and the Truthspeaker's pin are making the key cross-reference: Vishara's operation and Lösweg's inner council are touching the same supply chain.

### The Halvash Alley Fight

Three Shadow-Walkers came to the Halvash archive. One died in the fight. Two escaped with the Pryzi key aboard a Lösweg fishing trawler sitting off the coast. The boy died. The bone disc was recovered. See `events/halvash-alley-fight`.

### The Yngondi Roots

The Shadow-Walkers trace their deepest roots to the proto-Yngondi cultures — the oldest stratum of Karsac's continental memory, the peoples from whom Vishara's oldest symbol emerged. Their tradition is not invented. It is genuinely ancient. They do not know what they carry. Their tradition carries it for them.

## Player Knowledge

Players have seen Shadow-Walkers directly: three in the Halvash alley, one dead. They know the Ashvein spikes. They know the symbol — it is on the bone disc they carry. They may not yet connect the Truthspeaker's pin to the same supply.

## DM-Only

Korvann has been following Vishara's operational signature — a corrupted copy of the bone disc markings — across Exandria for ten years without knowing what he was following. The Air Ashari operation he traced was Shadow-Walker work. The figure in old armour at the edge of sight was one of Vishara's willing servants, caught at the threshold, still at the task it was given.

When Vishara is banished in Session 6, the picture assembles for Korvann. He understands what his settlement was protecting. He does not yet know what the Air Ashari hold. That is Campaign 2.

The Shadow-Walkers are distinct from the Captains' lieutenant network, though both ultimately serve Maharuq's direction. The Shadow-Walkers are specifically Vishara's asset class; the Captains' lieutenants operate in a different register.

## Ambiguities

- The exact organisational structure of the Shadow-Walkers — whether they are a coherent organisation, a tradition-lineage, or a recruited network — is not specified in sources.
