# The Skalds

**Canon File ID:** `factions/skalds`
**Retrieval Summary:** Warrior-mystics of Lösweg; keepers of the oral tradition; speak with the dead; their silencing is a Vishara symptom; the tradition is kept alive by being spoken.

> Warrior-mystics of Lösweg; keepers of memory; practitioners of capacities the Vushhiri faculty cannot classify; the tradition is kept alive by being spoken.

## Overview

The Skalds occupy a role with no clean equivalent elsewhere in Karsac. They are warriors of significant capability — their training is as demanding physically as anything practised by the military orders of Zörsdkog. But the martial dimension is in some respects the least important aspect of what they are.

A Skald is a keeper of memory. The oral tradition of Lösweg — history, genealogy, theology, law, and the long mythological cycles — is carried entire by a fully trained Skald, capable of reciting lineages stretching to the age of the gods and improvising verse in the old metres with facility that suggests something beyond practice. The spoken word, in Lösweg theology, is not merely descriptive but constitutive: to name something correctly is to participate in its existence.

## Description

### The Three Dimensions

**As warriors:** physically trained to a demanding standard. Reported to enter battle-states that make them effectively impervious to pain. Cursing abilities that do not respond to conventional medicine.

**As memory-keepers:** carry the oral tradition entire. Law, genealogy, theology, prophecy — preserved in forms whose structure matters as much as content. To translate a skald's recitation into ordinary prose is already to wound it.

**As practitioners of the uncanny:** claim to speak with the dead. Several Vushhiri researchers report witnessing events consistent with this claim. Their weather-reading exceeds any meteorological system the faculty employs. The Vushhiri faculty vote on whether this constitutes arcane arts: seven in favour, four against, two abstentions — the abstentions with written statements that the question itself was malformed.

Those who encounter a Skald who has chosen to make their nature felt report a reproducible sensation: the feeling that you are standing before something that has seen the future and finds it neither surprising nor particularly troubling.

### Silenced Skalds — Vishara Symptom

The silenced skald is one of the clearest Vishara indicators in the storyarch. Skalds do not go silent. The tradition is kept alive by being spoken — if it is not spoken, it ends. When more than one report of a silenced skald arrives, the players understand they are running out of time. The struggling speaking — Skalds who cannot reach the dead clearly, presence without clarity, like speaking through water — is the intermediate stage. The Truthspeaker is an advanced stage: tradition intact in form, wrong in weight.

### Ragnfrid

Ragnfrid is a Skald travelling with the company — young, at the edge of a tradition responding to things it has never encountered. His Verse of the Sea in Session 1 landed on Xyrrathh at the moment of impact and something in Xyrrathh's presence answered the words themselves. His recognition of the Truthspeaker's wrong cadence at the south dock is the first moment in the campaign where a player character directly perceives Vishara's work. See `pcs/ragnfrid`.

### The Truthspeaker's Wrongness

The Truthspeaker is a real Skald — his tradition is intact in form, his formal cadence precise, his old metres correct. The weight underneath them is wrong. Ragnfrid hears it before he can articulate it. A Skald who carries the tradition and uses it in service of Mathr's political operations is not a Skald who has been destroyed. They are one who has been thinned. The form remains. The specific gravity has been removed.
