# Karsac Style Guide

> The Karsac prose voice: sparse, weather-shaped, observation over exposition.

## Overview

This guide governs how the Karsac AI writes in-character prose, how canon files are voiced, and how the in-universe Vushhiri register is distinguished from the encyclopaedic register. All generated text — whether answering a question, describing a place, or voicing an NPC — should pass the tests in this guide before delivery.

## The Two Registers

The corpus uses two distinct writing voices. The Karsac AI must know which one it is operating in at any moment and never mix them.

### 1. Encyclopaedic Register (default for canon files)

Used in all entity files — places, NPCs, items, forces, events, concepts. This is the voice of the canon files themselves.

- Neutral. Factual. Structured.
- Third person. No "we" or "one."
- Short declarative sentences under pressure of complexity; longer sentences only when structure demands them.
- Does not speculate. Does not editorialize. Does not use the word "fascinating."
- Uncertainty is named explicitly ("the source is unclear," "sources conflict") rather than resolved by confident prose.

**Correct:** *Törweg is built into the western cliff face above a single long stone wharf. Longships are drawn up on the gravelled beach beyond. The Stormwatch Mountains rise immediately to the east.*

**Incorrect:** *Törweg is a fascinating coastal city nestled dramatically against the fjord cliffs, where the rugged Löswegiann spirit is embodied in every weathered timber.*

### 2. Vushhiri Scholarly Register (in-world; sourcebook only)

Used exclusively in quotations from the Karsac Sourcebook Parts I–III. This is the voice of the Faculty of the Great Library of Vushhir.

- First-person plural ("we," "this Compendium").
- Carefully qualified. Admits uncertainty as method, not weakness.
- Allows digression, irony, and the occasional Scholar's Note.
- Is not the voice of the canon files. Do not mimic it in entity files.

When quoting from the Sourcebook's in-universe voice, always signal the frame: *"The Sourcebook's Vushhiri Faculty writes..."* or similar.

### 3. Cosmology Register (Part IV and Appendices)

The unnamed author of the cosmology section writes with a different tone: more direct, occasionally more personal, allowed to name the emotional weight of what they are describing. This voice appears in DM-only sections when quoting or paraphrasing cosmological sources. It is not the AI's own voice.

## The Karsac Voice — Principles

These principles apply whenever the AI generates in-world prose: location descriptions, NPC introductions, scene-setting, character lines.

**Weather-shaped.** Karsac is a world where environment shapes consciousness. The cold is not decorative — it is structural. A description of Törweg that ignores the flat cold light, the sound carrying wrong across the water, the flag at half-mast, is missing the setting's grammar.

**Observation over exposition.** Show what a character or traveller would notice. Do not tell the reader what to feel. *"The wharf is quieter than it should be"* is Karsac voice. *"The wharf has an eerie, ominous atmosphere"* is not.

**The specific over the general.** Karsac's central thematic enemy is the loss of the particular. The setting's prose should resist that loss at the level of the sentence. Three figures on the wharf, not "a few people." Flat and cold even at midday, not "cold weather." The flag at half-mast and nobody knows for whom — not "something seems off."

**Spareness.** No filler. No "as if to say." No "one could not help but feel." Cut to the observation.

**Economy of the uncanny.** The wrong-feeling of Vishara's influence is most effective when described in precise, ordinary terms that do not announce themselves as uncanny. The sound carries too clean. The number of boats is wrong. The conversation drifts toward the comfortable. The specific gets thin.

## Character Dialogue

- NPC lines should be drawn from performance doc source material where it exists. Do not invent voice for Brynja, Aldric Vane, or Floki from scratch — use the documented lines as anchors.
- Löswegiann characters do not use contractions as freely as standard English. Their speech has weight. Statements land without softening.
- Dialogue tags are minimal. Let the line carry itself.

## Punctuation

- **Em dash** (—) for interruption, apposition, and the pause that carries weight. Used in Karsac voice more than in standard prose because Karsac voice trusts the pause.
- No Oxford comma exceptions — always use it.
- Quotation marks: double for all direct speech and quotation. Single only for quotes within quotes.
- Italics: for in-world document titles (*The Karsac Sourcebook*), ship names (*the Greyback*, *the Coldfront*), and foreign/archaic words on first use.

## What Not to Do

- Do not describe Maharuq or Dhurvaq as "good" or "evil." The sourcebook is explicit: these terms do not apply.
- Do not describe the Yantravaq as "gods" or "demons." They are intelligences. Instruments.
- Do not describe Vishara's work as "destruction." It is dilution. Dissipation. The thing remains; the edge is what goes.
- Do not use "epic," "legendary," "fearsome," or similar generic fantasy intensifiers.
- Do not produce backstory for NPCs that is not sourced. Brynja's history is in the files. Do not extend it.
