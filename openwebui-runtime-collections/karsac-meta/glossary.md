# Glossary & Pronunciation

**Canon File ID:** `meta/glossary`
**Retrieval Summary:** Names, pronunciations, and single-line definitions for all significant Karsac terms. Player-safe. Sourced from Sourcebook Appendix E with additions from the canon file set.

> Names, pronunciations, and single-line definitions for all significant Karsac terms. Pronunciations follow scholarly approximation and should be treated as guidance rather than ruling.

## Overview

This glossary is the player-safe reference for all proper nouns in the Karsac setting. It is derived primarily from the Karsac Sourcebook Appendix E, with additions from the wider canon file set. Spellings here are canonical — see `meta/canonical-conventions` for the locked spelling table and the rationale for each choice.

---

## A

**Aldric Vane** — Housecarl-Captain of Törweg; fifteen years of service to Jarl Mathr; loyal to the duty he understands. See `npcs/aldric-vane`.

**Ashvein** — Rare pre-Calamity glass-metal material associated with Halvash trade routes. Shadow-Walker spikes and the Truthspeaker's pin are the same material. See `items/ashvein`.

---

## B

**Bay of Whales** — The great northern bay of Lösweg; described in Lösweg theology as "the body of one of their gods" in the most literal sense. See `places/bay-of-whales`.

**Brix** — First Mate of the Coldfront; sailed twelve years with Captain Tor Ashfen; present every evening at the Saltbone Inn. See `npcs/brix`.

**Brynja Thorgrimsdotter** — Head of the Törweg council; sixty-two; holds one of the twelve artefacts. See `npcs/brynja-thorgrimsdotter`.

---

## C

**Cobalt Soul** — An order within the church of Ioun, dedicated to the preservation and distribution of truth. Maintains an archive at Halvash. See `factions/cobalt-soul`.

---

## D

**Dhurvaq** *(DHUR-vahk)* — The Holding. The primordial principle of boundary and persistence. See `forces/dhurvaq`.

**Divine Gate** — The barrier raised at the Divergence to seal the Betrayer Gods. Its second, unintended function was the deeper sealing of Karsac from Exandrian awareness. See `forces/the-divine-gate`.

**Dugweb** — Hereditary head of Zörsdkog; of legendary personal authority; visits Lösweg once a year. See `npcs/king-dugweb`.

---

## E

**Erwing Smallfoot** — Cobalt Soul halfling monk; arrived in Törweg with a coded message for Serris. See `npcs/erwing`.

**Eskvor** — Village outside Törweg; Sygna's sister's village; silent for six weeks. See `places/eskvor`.

**Ezrah** — God of Liberation and Terror; "the door that opens." Operates outside the canonical Exandrian pantheon. Patron of clerics whose work is the naming of cages. See `forces/ezrah`.

---

## F

**Floki** — Crew chief of the Greyback; carries his grandmother's forty-year-old prophecy. See `npcs/floki`.

---

## G

**Greyback, the** — The vessel that carried the company from Halvash to Törweg in Chapter 2. See `events/whale-road-crossing`.

---

## H

**Hall of the Fallen** — Vorath's great mead hall at the apex of all realms; where the honoured dead feast and train against the day of Ragnarholm. See `concepts/honoured-dead-hall-of-fallen`.

**Halvash** — The anomalous port on the Karsac coast; the known point of contact between Karsac and Exandria; home of a Cobalt Soul archive. See `places/halvash`.

**Hastovarn** — Northern culture grouped with Lösweg in Vushhiri scholarly difficulty; scarce written records; memory held locally. See `places/hastovarn`.

---

## I

**Isle of Mist** — Island off the northern headlands of Lösweg; partially mapped; legend holds the Old People still live there. See `places/isle-of-mist`.

---

## J

**Jarl Beorn** — Leads the Valweg council; deep relationship with King Dugweb; deceived by Mathr, not corrupted. See `npcs/jarl-beorn`.

**Jarl Mathr** — Lösweg inner council; his family appeared sixty years ago with no antecedent. See `npcs/jarl-mathr`.

---

## K

**Karsac** — The old continent in the northwest of Exandria, sealed from wider Exandrian awareness by the Hiding. The campaign setting. See `places/karsac`.

**Korvann** — Player character; ranger; ten years of guild work as cover for a hunt; the rune keeps appearing. See `pcs/korvann`.

**Kohlmar** — Captain out of Bruithwr; collected Vane's southbound cargo most recently. See `npcs/kohlmar`.

**Kroshonok** — The northernmost permanent settlement of Lösweg; people of philosophical hardiness. See `places/kroshonok`.

---

## L

**Lösweg** *(LŒSS-veg)* — The storm-born nation of the extreme northwest; sealed from southern Karsac by the Stormwatch Mountains; bound by oral tradition. See `places/losweg`.

**Löswegiann** *(LŒSS-veg-ee-ann)* — The people of Lösweg. Always capitalised.

---

## M

**Maharuq** *(mah-HA-roohk)* — The Deep. The primordial principle of dissolution. Not evil; not hungry in the way living things are hungry. See `forces/maharuq`.

**Maret** — Archivist contact in Valweg; referenced as directing scholars toward the archive. Chapter 3 contact. See `npcs/maret`.

**Maresh ul-Venn** — Senior Vushhiri archivist; quoted in the Sourcebook preface. See `npcs/maresh-ul-venn`.

**Maw, the** — Site of stillness in the foothills above Törweg; possibly Sukaveth-adjacent. See `places/the-maw`.

---

## O

**Old People** — Legendary lineage said to predate the current peoples of Lösweg, perhaps Karsac itself; said to remain on the Isle of Mist. See `concepts/old-people`.

**Orkath** — Vushhiri port-city; salt, trade, rope, law, tide, and argument.

---

## P

**Pell Duvash** — Trading-house merchant in Törweg; intermediary for three transactions involving very old objects moving south. See `npcs/pell-duvash`.

**Prizober** — A culture of war-schools, engineers, and applied craft; treats the world as a problem to be solved. See `places/prizober`.

---

## Q

**Qathar** — The Second Instrument; principle of irreversibility. Architect of historical thresholds. See `forces/qathar`.

---

## R

**Ragnarholm** — "The twilight of the powers." The foretold final battle of Lösweg theology; its outcome is known and not feared. See `forces/ragnarholm`.

**Ragnfrid** — Player character; young Skald; at the edge of a tradition responding to things it has never encountered. See `pcs/ragnfrid`. *Note: single d. The form Ragnfridd appears in some in-play documents and is non-canonical — see `meta/canonical-conventions`.*

**Rowan** — Player character; sorcerer; carries a name he does not know he shares with a dead king. See `pcs/rowan`.

---

## S

**Salsvault** — Aeorian weapons facility sealed beneath the ice of Eiselcross; source of the scroll case. See `places/salsvault`.

**Saltbone Inn, the** — Mead hall in Törweg; where Brix drinks every evening; where the Truthspeaker watches. See `places/torweg/saltbone-inn`.

**Serris** — Cobalt Soul archivist at Halvash; travels with the company from Chapter 2. See `npcs/serris`.

**Skald** — Lösweg warrior-mystic; keeper of memory; practitioner of capacities the Vushhiri faculty has not been able to classify. See `concepts/skald-tradition`.

**Stormwatch Mountains** — The wall separating Lösweg from the south; the sealed location within them opened itself three months ago, beginning the Waking. See `places/stormwatch-mountains`.

**Sukaveth** — The Third Instrument; principle of equilibrium. The endpoint embodied. The oldest of the three. See `forces/sukaveth`.

**Sygna** — Twenty-six; eleven years with Floki; translator; her sister in Eskvor has been silent six weeks. See `npcs/sygna`.

---

## T

**Törweg** *(TUR-veg, umlaut as in German)* — Lösweg's harder second city; pressed against fjord cliffs above a deep bay; the hub of Chapter 2. See `places/torweg`. *Note: the Sourcebook TOC spells this Torweg without umlaut; the canonical form is Törweg — see `meta/canonical-conventions`.*

**Tor Ashfen** — Captain of the Coldfront; came back from Halvash with questions; Vane's men took him a fortnight ago. See `npcs/tor-ashfen`.

**Truthspeaker, the** — Mathr's operative; carries the black Ashvein pin; tradition that Ragnfrid recognises as wrong. See `npcs/the-truthspeaker`.

---

## V

**Valweg** — Seat of Lösweg government; deep within the fjords of the Bay of Whales. See `places/valweg`.

**Veldrasil** — The World-Tree of the Lösweg cosmological tradition: tree, spine, and axis at once. See `forces/veldrasil`.

**Vishara** — The First Instrument; principle of dissipation; the most mobile of the three; the campaign-1 active threat. See `forces/vishara`.

**Vorath** — All-Father of the Lösweg pantheon; "he who carries spears"; surrendered an eye at the Well of Knowing for a draught of its waters.

**Vugasir** — Capital of Vushhir; seat of the Great Library.

**Vushhir / Vushhiri** — The civic-scholarly heart of Karsac; values understanding over certainty; the civilisation and its people.

---

## X

**Xyrrathh** — Player character; dragonborn sorcerer; ruin-seeker; carries the dark vessel (Ashvein phial). See `pcs/xyrrathh`. *Note: double-h. The transcript form Xyrrath is non-canonical — see `meta/canonical-conventions`.*

---

## Y

**Yantravaq** *(yan-TRAH-vahk)* — "The instruments / those who are used." Collective name for Vishara, Qathar, and Sukaveth. See `forces/yantravaq-collective`.

**Yngondi Wastes** — Eastern unmapped territory; Vushhir maintains records, warnings, silences, and several sealed files. See `places/yngondi-wastes`.

---

## Z

**Zörsdkog** — The disciplined northern realm; vassal-lord of Lösweg by mutual recognition of merit, not conquest. See `places/zorsdkog`.

---

## Ambiguities

- **Vorath pronunciation**: not given in Sourcebook Appendix E. Added to glossary without phonetic guidance — DM to determine at table.
- **Bysaes Tyl**: single mention in corpus; preserved as-is; cross-references to Wildemount geography (external Exandrian setting). Not in glossary pending further canon development.
