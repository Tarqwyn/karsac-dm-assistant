# Ragnfrid

> Skald; Floki's nephew; at the edge of a tradition responding to things it has never encountered.

## Overview

Ragnfrid is a Lösweg-born Skald — a keeper of names, a poet, a witness, and an oath-witness. He did not become a sailor like his uncle Floki. He became something older, sharper, and in some ways more dangerous. By adulthood he had earned his place at more than one fire. He has spoken warriors into courage, shamed liars into honesty, calmed rooms before blades came out, and left more than one hall colder than he found it because a poem made silence where shouting had failed.

Floki asked him aboard the Greyback not because he needed another sailor but because something about the cargo's marks reminded Floki of an old verse that Ragnfrid's family knew — one that carried pieces older than anyone currently alive.

## Background

His work is to remember what others simplify, bury, or reshape. In Lösweg, that makes him useful, respected, and sometimes dangerous. He knows which families carry old feuds, which victories are exaggerated, which deaths are lied about, and which oaths were broken quietly.

When the Stormwatch Mountains opened and the cargo came down, Floki understood that what was on the Greyback was not only sailor's business. A skald knows when an old story has stopped being a story.

*Languages: Lösweg, Veyr.*

## Voice & Presence

Ragnfrid reads environments the way his tradition trained him — in cadence, posture, and what is not said. On the western dock at Törweg, he heard three things in the first minutes that nobody else heard: that the welcoming party was wrong (two armed men Floki is not clan-tied to, at a welcome that does not include them); that the half-mast flag flies only for council or close-kin dead; that Brynja's address to Floki used council cadence — she was being watched, and was telling him so.

He is the first player character to directly perceive Vishara's work. At the south dock, he hears the Truthspeaker's formal cadence and feels the weight underneath it is wrong. The form is intact. Something else is gone. He cannot name it. He simply knows.

## The Verse of the Sea

In Session 1, his Verse of the Sea landed on Xyrrathh at the moment of impact during the alley fight, compressed the damage, and something in Xyrrathh's presence answered in near-affirmation of the words themselves. Neither of them has spoken about that yet.

## DM-Only

### The Tradition Responding

The storyarch describes Ragnfrid as *"at the edge of a tradition responding to things it has never encountered."* This is the most precise description of his DM-layer situation: his training has given him instruments for detecting Vishara's work — the wrong cadence, the thinned weight beneath the form — but the tradition was built before Vishara was working this way at this scale. He is using tools that were not designed for this exact task and finding that they still work, partially, at the edges.

### Session 6 Victory

The campaign's Session 6 victory is stated in his terms: he speaks with the dead and they answer clearly for the first time in the campaign. The tradition functions as it should. The specific dead who waits is still themselves when called. That is what they were defending.

The Truthspeaker's wrong weight — which Ragnfrid heard and could not name — is Vishara's work. When Vishara is banished, whatever the Truthspeaker was before his twenty years of service to Mathr may, or may not, be recoverable. That is DM discretion.
