# The Spiral Carver's Whittlings

**Canon File ID:** `items/the-spiral-carvers-whittlings`
**Retrieval Summary:** Scraps of wood carved by the Carver every afternoon; the spiral matches the bone disc geometry fragment; he does not know why he carves.

> Scraps of wood; the Carver makes the same spiral every afternoon; the geometry matches the bone disc.

## Overview

The Carver at the Cooperage produces spirals on scraps of wood every afternoon — the same one, compulsively, without being able to say why. The geometry is structurally identical to a fragment of the bone disc pattern.

Players who examine the whittlings and have seen the bone disc, or who have Serris with them, can recognise the geometry. Ragnfrid's tradition hears what the Carver is doing before he can articulate it.

## DM-Only

The whittlings are Vishara's oldest geometry propagating through someone who was present at the threshold of Hjalmar's transformation — the Carver does not carry the pattern deliberately; it leaked into his hands through proximity to the Maw's event. The specific mechanism is not specified; the result is observable.
