# The Scroll Case

**Canon File ID:** `items/the-scroll-case`
**Retrieval Summary:** The scroll case is both key and map — the case opens the binding, the map inside leads to the binding's location; found in Salsvault for fifteen gold; the players have been carrying the answer since Session 1.

> Both key and map; the players have been carrying the answer since Session 1.

## Overview

The scroll case is not what started the journey. It is the journey. Found in Salsvault — a pre-Calamity Aeorian bioweapons laboratory under the ice of Eiselcross — priced at fifteen gold among ordinary stock. No intelligence arranged this. No plan produced it. It is the purest accident in a chain of events otherwise shaped, at every point, by someone's intention.

The players carry the answer since Session 1. They do not know this until Session 6.

## Description

### Physical

A scroll case of unusual material — the exact description is not given in available sources, but its properties are consistent with Ashvein: dark, neither quite glass nor quite metal, warm to the touch (the company began noticing this warmth on the Greyback crossing; nobody has mentioned it aloud yet). On the Greyback, the case opened once on its own at the south dock — not all the way, just enough for the writing inside to be briefly visible in an orientation it had not been in before.

### The Map

The map inside runs northwest, with partial coastline, star chart annotations, and one line in archaic Draconic: *where the gods did not reach, it was already old.* This is not navigation in any ordinary sense. It is the binding described in the language of the thing that opens it. The scroll case and the binding are the same object expressed in two registers. The case is the key. The map is the instruction.

### The Pattern

Serris found markings on the bronze disc cargo (the artefacts from the Stormwatch vault) that match the bone disc carving and continue past it. The scroll case is a third face of the same pattern. There are more. The pattern is the binding — together, these fragments describe what holds Vishara to material existence on this plane.

## DM-Only

### The Full Secret

Someone, long ago, understood both the scroll case and the binding simultaneously — the key and the lock — and separated them deliberately. The case was placed in Salsvault not as a hiding place exactly but as the best available approximation of invisibility: stored as ordinary stock, filed as ordinary inventory. The binding was moved out of Karsac so quietly that not even the Yantravaq noticed. Not even Vishara itself.

**Session 1:** scroll case found.  
**Session 5:** binding seen for the first time.  
**Session 6:** connection made. Case used to open binding. Vishara is banished.

The Aeorian note from the Maw — *"It is not a weapon. It is a condition. Available to be woken or to be held."* — describes the binding. The same hand that wrote the Maw's note wrote the scroll case margin. After two thousand years.

### Who Placed It

The identity of whoever placed the case in Salsvault is not specified in available sources. This is the question the players leave Session 6 carrying. That is the thread into what comes next.

## Ambiguities

- The scroll case's material (whether Ashvein or another material) is not explicitly confirmed in sources — its warmth and behaviour are consistent with Ashvein but not identified by name in the corpus.
- The identity of whoever separated the case from the binding is deliberately withheld.
