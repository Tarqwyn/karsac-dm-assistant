# Ashvein

**Canon File ID:** `items/ashvein`
**Retrieval Summary:** Rare pre-Calamity glass-metal; mined only beneath Halvash; the Shadow-Walkers' throwing spikes, the Truthspeaker's pin, and Xyrrathh's dark vessel are all the same material.

> Rare glass-metal mined only beneath Halvash; the Shadow-Walkers' spikes, the Truthspeaker's pin, and Xyrrathh's vessel share a supply chain.

## Overview

Ashvein is a rare material — volcanic in colour, dark, neither quite glass nor quite metal, cold to the touch. It is associated with Halvash and pre-Calamity trade routes. The specific claim in sources: mined only beneath Halvash.

## The Cross-Reference

Three items in the Chapter 2 corpus share this material:

**The Shadow-Walkers' throwing spikes** — the weapons used in the Halvash alley fight; one spike embedded in Xyrrathh during the encounter.

**The Truthspeaker's pin** — the small black pin at his throat; visible on the western dock, at the Saltbone Inn, on the south dock. Brix noticed the same material on the man who came with Vane's man to take Ashfen.

**Xyrrathh's dark vessel** — the phial he has been carrying since before the campaign; Ashvein, though he does not know this name.

These three items share a material that can only be sourced from beneath Halvash. The Shadow-Walker network, Mathr's council voice, and Xyrrathh's most personal possession are all Halvash-adjacent. This is the Ashvein cross-reference — the tell that connects disparate operational threads to a single supply point.

## Player Knowledge

The connection is available but not announced. Korvann's passive Investigation at the south dock (DC 12) is the easiest gate. Xyrrathh recognises the material immediately if he sees the Truthspeaker's pin — it is what his vessel is made of. He may or may not say so aloud.

## DM-Only

The implication of a shared Halvash supply chain: Mathr's network and the Shadow-Walker network are supplied from the same source, or connected through the same pre-Calamity trade route. What this implies about Mathr's connections to the Yngondi Wastes — where the Captains are moving artefacts — is not specified in current sources. Plant the cross-reference.
