# The Stormwatch Vault Opening

**Canon File ID:** `events/stormwatch-vault-opening`
**Retrieval Summary:** Three months before Chapter 2: a sealed location in the Stormwatch Mountains opened itself; twelve objects were recovered; Vane began moving them south on Mathr's orders.

> Three months before the campaign's Chapter 2 events; the sealed location opened itself; twelve objects recovered; Vane's orders began.

## Overview

Two days before the autumn equinox of the current year, a sealed location in the Stormwatch Mountains above Törweg opened itself. Not forced — the pressure from below had increased to the point where the seal could no longer hold. Twelve objects were recovered from inside.

Jarl Mathr was informed immediately. He instructed Aldric Vane to begin moving the most significant objects south to Bruithwr. Vane has not asked why. He has been moving cargo for three months.

## Significance

This is the threshold event that marks the Waking's current phase — the moment the Waking crossed from stable pressure into pressure that could no longer be contained. It is also what set Vishara's operational acceleration in motion: Mathr moving the artefacts south is an attempt to disperse the binding components and prevent the scroll case's map from being usable.

## Player Knowledge

Players learn the timeline from Brynja: three months ago, the Maw opened; the objects came down; Vane began moving cargo. They do not yet know the objects are binding components.

## DM-Only

The vault opening was not an act of human agency — it was the Waking. The seal failed because the cosmological pressure failed. Mathr's response (disperse the artefacts) is Vishara's direction at work: keep the binding inaccessible. This is why the timing matters: the players arriving with the scroll case's map at the same moment the artefacts are being dispersed is the race that drives the campaign.
