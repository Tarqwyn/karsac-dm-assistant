# The Threshold-Bound Echo

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/threshold-bound-echo`
**Retrieval Summary:** The Guardian at the Maw fracture; tester, not finisher; does not pursue beyond the fracture; CR 4; connected to Hjalmar's fate.

> Guardian at the Maw fracture; tester, not finisher; does not pursue beyond the fracture.

## Overview

The Threshold-Bound Echo is the encounter at the bottom of the Maw's tilted shaft — what guards the Aeorian fragment and the bone disc geometry continuation. It tests, withdraws, and tests again. It does not pursue beyond the fracture. It is not a threat that wants to kill the players; it is a threshold that wants to know what they are.

This entity is not yet known to the players.

## Description

**CR 4 (Large aberration, neutral territorial)**

The walls in the fracture score with patterns where it has moved. The cold air begins moving sideways — through itself. Where the shaft turns, the wall is not where it was. Something has chosen to be there instead of where it was.

Dry smell. Faintly mineral. The same smell the Shadow-Walkers carried in the Halvash alley.

**Key traits:**
- **Threshold-Bound.** Cannot move more than 60 ft. from the fracture. If forced beyond this limit, teleports back silently at the start of its next turn.
- **Wrong Geometry.** Each time hit by melee, attacker makes DC 13 Wisdom save. On failure, disadvantage on next attack against it.
- **Recoil.** If it takes 20+ damage in a single turn, it disengages and retreats 30 ft. into the fracture for 1 minute.

**Multiattack:** two slams (+6, 10 ft., 14 bludgeoning). On hit, target makes DC 14 Strength save or is moved 10 ft. toward the fracture or away from the exit.

## Tactical Notes

Tester, not finisher. It moves at them, withdraws, moves at them again. Fight intelligently — it disengages within three rounds. Fight stupidly — it kills someone. Once they leave the Maw it does not pursue.

The DM note in the performance doc specifies: its behaviour around Xyrrathh should be different from its behaviour around the others. It tests him more, and withdraws less. Something in what he carries is recognised.

## DM-Only

### The Hjalmar Connection

Hjalmar — the Carver's brother — went into the Maw three months ago and came out wrong. The Echo's relationship to Hjalmar is not fully specified in sources. Whether Hjalmar became the Echo, was absorbed into its function, or simply encountered something that changed him fundamentally is not canon-confirmed. The available evidence: Hjalmar came out changed; the Carver cannot describe how; the Echo is present in the Maw; the Carver's spiral matches the Echo's geometry. The connection is the inference. The mechanism is not specified.

### Relationship to Sukaveth

The Maw is possibly Sukaveth-adjacent — the Sourcebook's Appendix C is explicit that this link is suggestive only. The Echo's nature (threshold-bound, testing, not destroying) is consistent with Sukaveth's presence as gravitational pull on directions rather than active force. But this is inference, not confirmed canon.
