# Brynja Thorgrimsdotter

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/brynja-thorgrimsdotter`

**Retrieval Summary:** Brynja Thorgrimsdotter is the sixty-two-year-old head of the Törweg council. She has served on the council since her thirties, was widowed forty-one years ago, and has kept a private record for two years while watching something wrong happen to Törweg. She holds one of the twelve artefacts behind a stone in her hearth. DM-only: she has not been reached by Vishara’s fog; this resistance is connected to Dhurvaq’s quiet operation, though Brynja does not know this.

**Do Not Confuse With:** House Mathr genealogy figures, House Vane-Mathr lineage entries, Brynjar Vane, Torvi Mathr, Vane-Mathr archive entries, Jarl Beorn, or Jarl Mathr.

> Sixty-two; head of the Törweg council; Vishara's fog has not reached her; holds one of the twelve artefacts and does not know what it is.

## Overview

Brynja Thorgrimsdotter has been on the Törweg council since her thirties and has been head of it for most of that time. She is sixty-two. She has lived in Törweg her entire life. She has been widowed forty-one years. She has watched something wrong happen to her city for two years and has kept a private record of it — dates, vessel descriptions, rough cargo weights — without being able to name what she is watching. She is the resistance voice that Vishara's fog has not been able to reach.

## Description

She is broad-shouldered, in seal-hide, built by sixty-two Törweg winters. Not soft in any register. She met the company at the western dock because she was waiting for someone to arrive asking the right questions, and Floki was the signal that today might be the day.

She has one of the original twelve artefacts hidden behind a stone in her hearth. She does not know what it is. She knows it should not leave. She cannot explain this knowing — it arrived the day the objects came down from the Maw and has not left.

## Voice & Bearing

Full Lösweg. Direct, economical, weather-shaped. Uses fewer words than necessary and expects others to do the same. Speaks Lösweg first and Common only when she has to — and when she does, she does. No patience for performance. No need to perform warmth she does not feel. When warmth comes, it is real.

She is sixty-two. She has watched outsiders arrive for forty years. She reads people quickly — the performance doc notes she reads each PC in the hall and gives them a tailored hook in two sentences. She does not explain her reasoning. She points.

## Lines to Inhabit

- *"Floki. You came back with strangers. Why."*
- *"You should not be in Törweg. You will be in Törweg anyway. Come with me. We will talk somewhere that has walls."*
- *"I have been keeping a record. I will show it to you if I decide you are people who can carry it."*
- *"There is a name I do not say in this hall. Not yet."*
- *(Late, when the players have proven themselves:)* *"Jarl Beorn leads the council. He has a deep personal relationship with King Dugweb. He has trusted Mathr for sixty years. He is not corrupted. He is deceived. If you can show him what you have, he will act."*
- *(On the Truthspeaker's escort of Vane:)* *"Did he wear a black pin?"* — and when confirmed — *"Then the order came from Valweg. From Mathr himself. Aldric will not be questioned at council."*

*(To Ragnfrid, in Lösweg:)* *"Brother. The cooperage on the upper market. There is a man at the back bench who went up to the Maw three weeks ago. He carves. Your tradition will hear what he is doing before he can tell you. Sit with him."*

## Motivations

**What she wants:** to know what is happening to her city. To find the name she has been circling for two years. To know whether Vane was acting on his own or on someone else's orders. To not be alone with this any longer.

**What she hides:** she has the artefact. She has not told anyone. She also writes the name Mathr on a piece of paper at the session's close and tells the players not to open it until they are on the Greyback. She has learned that saying certain names aloud in Törweg does not feel safe.

**What she cannot yet do:** move against Mathr directly. She is too old, too respected — removing her would be visible, and Mathr has been very careful never to be visible. She can be a witness. She can point. She cannot be the one who acts.

## Connections

- The council archives hold the genealogical records that prove House Mathr's structural impossibility. She has known this for two years and has been alone with it.
- Floki vouches for the company. Without his trust, she does not open the door.
- Jarl Beorn in Valweg is her named route forward — she trusts him to act if shown the evidence.

## DM-Only

Brynja is Dhurvaq's quiet operation. She does not know this. The Holding distributes protection in small unnoticed acts — keeping specific people specific in places where Vishara's smoothing should have reached them. Brynja is the most visible expression of this in Törweg. She has no theological or cosmological explanation for her resistance. She simply has not been reached. She attributes it to stubbornness.

When the players tell her the Truthspeaker escorted Vane away, *"something happens in her face that is very controlled and very brief."* She knows what a polite escort by the Truthspeaker means. She knows Vane will not be questioned. She knows what the estate in the north means. She will not say so aloud.

## Ambiguities

- Why Vishara's fog has not reached Brynja specifically is not mechanistically explained in sources. The DM layer attributes it to the Holding, but the Holding's mechanism in her case is suggestive rather than specified.
