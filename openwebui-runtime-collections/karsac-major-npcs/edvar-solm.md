# Edvar Solm

> Halvash harbourmaster, nineteen years; threshold-keeper, not road-walker; stayed behind.

## Overview

Edvar Solm has been the harbourmaster of Halvash for nineteen years. He does not leave. His place is the threshold, not the road — which is why Serris goes and Edvar stays. He gave the company a working Lösweg vocabulary and a letter of introduction in his own hand before they sailed. Serris's Common is rougher; some things will be lost.

## Description

He came to the Meridian the night after the alley fight. He did not knock. He told them what he knew — two Shadow-Walkers on a launch to a Lösweg fishing trawler sitting off the eastern coast, three miles out; the trawler went dark as soon as they reached it. He asked:

*"I have been the harbourmaster of Halvash for nineteen years. I have never had to think about the Yngondi Wastes. I am asking you to find out what is wrong in Lösweg. I am asking because I do not know who else to ask."*

Then he went back to his post. He raised a hand when the company passed him at dawn. He did not stop them.

## Voice & Bearing

Precise. Economical. The voice of someone who has been the threshold-keeper long enough to know exactly what the threshold costs and exactly what it costs not to cross it. He does not sleep well after the alley fight. He asks because he does not know who else to ask — that sentence carries weight because it is true.
