# The Carver

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/the-carver`
**Retrieval Summary:** Cooperage carver; his brother Hjalmar went into the Maw and came out wrong; carves the same spiral every afternoon and does not know why.

> Cooperage carver; his brother went into the Maw and came out wrong; carves the same spiral every afternoon and does not know why.

## Overview

The Carver works at the Cooperage in Törweg's upper market, carving barrel staves by trade. Every afternoon, in addition to his work, he produces spirals — the same one, repeated, on scraps of wood. He has been doing this since his brother Hjalmar went into the Maw three months ago and came back different. He does not know why he carves. He simply does.

His name is not given in available sources. He is referred to throughout as the Carver.

## Description

He is slow. He looks at his hands often. He does not start new conversations. He continues old ones when they are continued for him. The carving is not agitated — it is compulsive in a specific way, the way a body does something its mind has stopped supervising.

*"We went up. Two of us. Hjalmar went in. I waited at the slot. He came out—he came out wrong. He looked the same. He walked the same. He did not—"*

He forgets the sentence. He begins again on the spiral.

## Voice & Bearing

Slow. Looking at his hands. The voice of a man who has been trying to tell a story for three months and cannot find the shape of it because the shape is wrong. He is not distressed in a dramatic way. He is quietly, specifically stuck. The spiral helps somehow, or he thinks it does.

## Motivations

**What he wants:** to finish the sentence. He has been trying for three months. The sentence is: "he did not come back the same." He can say the words but they do not complete the thought because the thought requires admitting that whoever came back from the Maw was not Hjalmar.

**What he hides:** nothing consciously. The compulsive carving is the thing that has replaced whatever he was hiding from himself.
