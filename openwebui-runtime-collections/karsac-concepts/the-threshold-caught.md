# The Threshold-Caught

**Canon File ID:** `concepts/the-threshold-caught`
**Retrieval Summary:** Those who served Vishara willingly in life; the dissipation so thorough at death that the specific self cannot make the passage; they remain at the threshold, bearing the rune, still at the task.

> Those who served Vishara willingly; too dissipated to cross at death; bearing the rune; still at the task they were given.

## Overview

Vishara extends its reach to the boundary between life and death — which is, in the end, a boundary like any other. Those who serve Vishara willingly in life find that at death, the dissipation has worked so thoroughly on them that the specific particular self — the thing that would cross — has already been partially dissolved. There is not enough of them left to make the passage.

They remain caught at the threshold, bearing the rune, marked by what they served.

## Observable Examples

**The Sea Hag's Thralls** — Vishara's willing servants, drowned, still at work on the Whale-Road. One wears Lösweg clothing in patterns Floki's father knew. He fights the company without knowing who Floki is.

**The Truthspeaker** — a Skald whose tradition has been thinned over twenty years of service to Mathr's shaped purposes. Not yet Threshold-Caught — he is a living, advanced-stage symptom. If Vishara is banished, whether the thinning is reversible for him is DM discretion.

**The Air Ashari Dead** — Korvann followed a Threshold-Caught figure in old armour at the edge of sight for ten years. Still at the task it was given.

## DM-Only

The Threshold-Caught bear the rune — Vishara's oldest symbol, the same geometry as the bone disc. The company has been carrying Vishara's oldest mark since Session 1. They have been surrounded by the Threshold-Caught's traces — the rune, the corrupted geometry — the entire campaign. When Vishara is banished, the Threshold-Caught cross. The dead who were held are released. Ragnfrid speaks with them and they answer clearly.
