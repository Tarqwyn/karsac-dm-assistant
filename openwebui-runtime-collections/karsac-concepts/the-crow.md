# The Crow

> The crow at thresholds; appeared on the Council Archives beam in Törweg; a promise, not surveillance.

## Overview

A crow has appeared to Rowan at thresholds throughout his life. Never close enough to be certain. Never often enough to be sure it is the same bird. His first instinct has always been resentment — the assumption that House Thornevale's familiars are reporting back to Aldric. Over years the sightings grew infrequent enough that he stopped being sure what to think.

In Törweg, stepping off the gangplank: a flicker of black motion at the edge of his vision, high and to the east, on a wooden beam protruding from the upper terrace of the eastern cliff face. The Council Archives. It held for two seconds. Then it was gone without the beat of wings you would expect.

## DM-Only

The Crow is a promise, not surveillance. It is not Thornevale family magic. It is something older, attached to the bloodline — possibly to the name itself (Rowahn, the dead king of Karsac whose name Rowan shares without knowing it). It has been pointing toward discovery since before Rowan had language for what it was tracking. It chose the Council Archives because the archives contain the document where Rowan's name appears in Karsac's history.

The Crow does not explain itself. It chooses a direction. Rowan either goes that way or he doesn't.
