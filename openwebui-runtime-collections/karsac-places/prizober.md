# Prizober

**Canon File ID:** `places/prizober`

> Culture of applied craft, war-schools, and engineering; treats the world as a problem to be solved.

## Overview

Prizober is, by the assessment of every faculty that has examined it, Karsac's pre-eminent culture of applied craft. Its habit is to look upon difficulty and ask what tools, what arrangement of stone, what disciplined column of people might resolve it. Where Vushhir studies as though the world is a conversation to be entered, Prizober builds as though the world is a problem to be solved. The Sourcebook's Chapter 4 acknowledges the strengths and the costs of that disposition without yet detailing either.

## Description

The Sourcebook's Chapter 4 is marked *forthcoming* with outline sections only. What is currently known:

- Prizober houses war-schools, engineering houses, and a political culture shaped by craft and applied knowledge.
- Relations with Vushhir involve shared interest in structure; the two cultures differ sharply in temperament.
- Described briefly in Vushhir's relations section as a culture of feudal houses, dynasties, courts, and border lords — archives complicated by victorious families erasing defeats, usurpers rewriting succession, and chroniclers rewarded for loyalty.
