# Pell Duvash's Trading House, Törweg

**Canon File ID:** `places/torweg/pell-duvashs-trading-house`
**Retrieval Summary:** Trading house and warehouse; back room where Duvash sits with the choices he has made; site of the assassins' attack.

> Trading house and warehouse; back room where Duvash sits with the choices he has made; site of the assassins' attack.

## Overview

Pell Duvash's trading house is the location for Thread C. Duvash has been the intermediary for three transactions of very old objects moving south. He did not know what he was handling at first. He does now. A note in a place only he would look told him: *"Stay, and tell the truth to whoever comes."* He has been waiting.

## Description

Mid-fifties. Soft hands. Quiet eyes. Past the point of softening.

*"Sit. Please. I have been waiting for someone who would actually listen."*

He tells them: three transactions. The first two letters were in Aldric's writing. The third was not — the seal was right, the signature was right, the instruction was too direct. Vane has been careful for fifteen years. The last instruction was not careful. Someone else wrote it. The captain collecting was a Bruithwr man called Kohlmar.

### The Assassins

Ten to fifteen minutes into the conversation: two figures appear at the back of the building. They did not come through the front. Duvash goes very still. He is not surprised. Initiative is called.

On the happy path, after the fight: *"Get me out of Törweg. I have given you what I have. I will go wherever you go next. I will not stay here another night."*

## Player Knowledge

Players learn: three cargo transactions; the third letter's handwriting mismatch; Kohlmar as the Bruithwr recipient. The assassins confirm someone wants the conversation stopped.

## DM-Only

The note that kept Duvash in place was left by Erik Mathr — the cleric working inside Mathr's network. Erik saw Duvash as someone the cage had almost swallowed, who could still be useful if someone arrived in time. The assassins were sent by whoever noticed Duvash had not yet left Törweg.
