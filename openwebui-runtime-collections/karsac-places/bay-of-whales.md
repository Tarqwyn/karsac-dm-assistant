# Bay of Whales

> The great northern bay of Lösweg; "the body of one of their gods" in the most literal theological sense.

## Overview

The Bay of Whales dominates the northern coastline of Lösweg — the grey waters from which the region derives its lifeblood: fish, trade, and the profound spiritual relationship with the sea that underpins virtually every aspect of Lösweg culture. It is not merely an economic resource. In the most literal theological sense, it is the body of one of their gods.

## Description

The Bay's currents would swallow lesser sailors without thought. Its fjords run deep inland, providing the protected anchorages that Valweg and Törweg occupy. The Whale-Road — the open sea between Halvash and the Bay's mouth — is the two-day crossing the Greyback makes in Chapter 2.

The theological dimension is not metaphorical. The Löswegiann relationship to the Bay is more intimate than environmental dependency. The Bay features in the Lösweg cosmological tradition as literally divine — part of the god's body as present and material fact, not as symbol or simile.

### Deepwhales

The Chapter 2 BETA references a Deepwhale on the Whale-Road crossing — something old, acknowledging what the Greyback carries. The Deepwhale does not attack. It registers. Floki recognises the bone disc's significance and says: "impossible." The flat-hand gesture the crew make is specifically for the Deepwhale; it means something different than the gesture made for the sea hag's thralls.

## Atmosphere & Themes

The Bay of Whales is not decorative geography. It is the region's theological centre, its economic lifeblood, and the sea-road that brings the company from Halvash to Törweg. When the company arrives at dawn on day three of the crossing and the Stormwatch Mountains resolve from pre-dawn darkness and Törweg sits in a deep bay with smoke rising from too-few chimneys, that bay is the Bay of Whales. The flag at half-mast is visible before the voices on the wharf are audible. Both facts reach the deck at the same time.
