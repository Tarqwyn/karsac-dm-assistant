# The Saltbone Inn, Törweg

**Canon File ID:** `places/torweg/saltbone-inn`
**Retrieval Summary:** The mead hall in Törweg; where Brix works through a bottle every evening; where the Truthspeaker watched without approaching.

> The mead hall; Brix drinks here every evening; where the Truthspeaker is watching.

## Overview

The Saltbone Inn is where Thread A (Brix and the Coldfront) runs. It is a public space — the mead hall locals use — and accordingly the clock advances by one for being here openly. Players who listen carefully and spend time can learn what there is to know about Aldric Vane, the south dock, and what happened to Tor Ashfen.

## Description

The Saltbone is the kind of inn that knows its regulars. Brix has been at a far table every evening since the Coldfront came back without Ashfen, working through a bottle. He is mid-forties, short, deliberate, weathered — the kind of man who needs patience rather than pressure.

*"He came back asking different questions. Interested in different things. He was gentler than he had been before. That was what frightened me most."*

The man in the dark coat with the black pin is also here — at a far table, drinking nothing, watching everything. He does not approach. If a player approaches him directly he is polite, brief, gives a name that is not his, and excuses himself before the conversation can develop. The clock advances by one for this exchange.

### What the Inn Knows (ambient)

Players who listen carefully can learn: Aldric Vane has been Housecarl-Captain for fifteen years, his father before him; Vane's people are loading a coastal vessel at the south dock that started work before dawn; the captain of that vessel is not from Törweg; locals are wary of asking Vane questions but not afraid of him. He is one of theirs. He drinks here when he drinks. He remembers names. He pays his round.
