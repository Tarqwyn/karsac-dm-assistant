# Törweg

> Lösweg's harder second city; pressed against fjord cliffs above a deep bay; the hub of Chapter 2.

## Overview

Törweg occupies a more exposed position than Valweg, further along the Lösweg fjords. It is the colder, harder of the two Lösweg cities, home to those Löswegiann who regard even Valweg as somewhat soft. The settlement is built into the western cliff face above a single long stone wharf; longships are drawn up on the gravelled beach beyond. The Stormwatch Mountains rise immediately to the east, the Maw an hour's climb up the foothill path.

## Description

The town presses against the rock above the waterline. Timber-and-stone buildings climb the cliff in tiered terraces. Smoke from chimneys rises in thin columns; the light is flat and cold even at midday. The harbour is in a deep bay at the mouth of a fjord, protected from the open Whale-Road by the geography.

In normal conditions the bay would be full of fishing vessels and the wharf full of working figures. By Chapter 2, neither is true — three boats out instead of twenty, three figures on the wharf instead of the expected crowd, the flag at the harbourmaster's tower at half-mast. Sound carries strangely: voices on the wharf reach inbound vessels before they should. This is the Thinning of the Particular at city scale.

Törweg's authority structure is unusual. The Housecarl-Captain holds formal power and is currently Aldric Vane — fifteen years served, sworn to Jarl Mathr in Valweg. Beneath him sits an advisory council, normally weak. In Törweg specifically, the council has accumulated unusual weight over the last two years because Vane has been distracted carrying out orders from Valweg that he has not questioned. Brynja Thorgrimsdotter has held the city in that gap.

## Atmosphere & Themes

Törweg is what Vishara's method looks like at the political scale: a community that should be loud, working, contentious — and is instead quiet, sparse, and uncomfortably agreeable. The wrong-feeling is environmental before it is political. Sound carries clean. The number of people is off. The flag is at half-mast and nobody on the inbound vessel knows for whom. Players should feel something is wrong before they have a name for it.

## Player Knowledge

Players arrive at Törweg in Chapter 2 aboard the Greyback. They know it as Floki's home port and the destination of his cargo. During their stay they learn:

- Aldric Vane has been moving cargo south to Bruithwr for eight weeks on Jarl Mathr's orders.
- Brynja has been keeping a private record for two years.
- One of the twelve artefacts is hidden in Brynja's house.
- The Maw above the town has had something taken from it recently.
- House Mathr appears in Lösweg records sixty years ago with no antecedent.

## DM-Only

Törweg is Vishara's most-worked town in Lösweg — the Thinning is measurable here in ways it is not yet in Valweg. Vishara's methodology operates through Jarl Mathr but lands hardest on Vane because Vane carries the operational weight. Brynja has not been reached by the fog. This is Dhurvaq's quiet operation — the Holding distributing protection in small unnoticed acts. Brynja does not know this is what she is.

The Truthspeaker was in Törweg the day before the company arrived. His arrival is not coincidental — someone in Mathr's network was warned the Greyback was coming back with strangers.
