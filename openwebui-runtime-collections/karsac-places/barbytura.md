## Overview

Barbytura lies east of Iambarsir, beyond the last familiar roads. Its peoples are among the most generous sources of material in all Karsac and among the most challenging to verify. Their hospitality is often real, their memory formidable, and their sense of historical scale expansive. The difficulty lies not in getting them to speak — it lies in determining where history ends, poetry begins, and whether the distinction matters to them at all.

## Description

The Sourcebook gives Barbytura more character than most stub entries: a culture of great kings, impossible riders, sky-burials, ancestral bargains, battles fought beneath red moons, cities carried on wagons, horses that knew the names of stars, and warriors who rode for nine days without sleeping in order to deliver insults of diplomatic importance.

A Barbyturan tale may be inaccurate in date, impossible in detail, and entirely true in meaning. This is, by the Sourcebook's admission, inconvenient for archivists and excellent for understanding Barbytura.

When a Barbyturan elder, asked whether a particular king had truly wrestled a storm into submission, replied *"Not with his hands"* — the Vushhiri interviewer recorded this as clarification. It has clarified nothing.

What is stable: the great riding houses; a pastoral or semi-pastoral culture; eastern metals and trade goods flowing through Iambarsir; oral traditions of tremendous scope.

## Ambiguities

- Barbytura's geographic extent, political structure, and relationship to Zörsdkog or the Yngondi Wastes are not specified in available sources.
