# Maw-Changed Creatures

## Adversary Summary

Creatures that have been altered by proximity to or emergence from the Aeorian-era sealed location in the Stormwatch Mountains above Törweg. They are not directed agents. They are the environmental consequence of a sealed place having opened and something having been taken from it. The most important detail about them is visual: the geometry of the bone disc pattern appears in their form.

## Campaign Purpose

Maw-changed creatures give the Maw physical consequence without explaining it. They reinforce the bone disc pattern recognition thread — a player who has seen the bone disc, the wall carvings in the Maw, and the geometry of a changed creature has seen the same pattern three times in three contexts. That accumulation is the design intent. These creatures also establish the Stormwatch foothills as dangerous terrain for Chapter 3.

## What They Are

Animals, beasts, or things that should not exist in this form, altered by whatever was sealed in the Maw and by the opening of it. They are territorial and unpredictable. They are not intelligent. They carry the geometry of the Maw in their bodies.

## What They Know

Territorial behaviour and immediate compulsion. Nothing of the campaign's cosmological structure.

## What They Do Not Know

Everything above the level of animal behaviour.

## Tactics

Environmental and territorial. They do not pursue far beyond the Maw's area. Their attack patterns may be distorted by the geometry — moving in ways a normal beast would not, or responding to the bone disc symbol in ways that suggest it registers to them even if they cannot articulate why.

## Escalation Levels

**Low** — Something moves in the Maw that has no business moving. Peripheral. Sounds. A shape.

**Medium** — A creature in the Maw's approach or interior that is visibly wrong — proportions off, a pattern in the scarring.

**High** — Active territorial encounter inside the Maw. Multiple changed creatures. The wall geometry and the creature geometry are recognisably the same.

## Mechanical Base

No canon stat block. The DM should select an SRD beast chassis appropriate to the environment and CR needed, then apply the following modification notes:

- Add a description note: the creature's scarring, growth patterns, or movement traces the bone disc geometry
- The creature may be drawn to or repelled by objects carrying the bone disc symbol (DM's choice, consistent within the encounter)
- The creature does not pursue beyond the Maw's immediate area

Suitable SRD bases: any beast CR 1/2 to CR 3 appropriate to mountain/cave environment. If the DM wants something more threatening, undead chassis (ghoul, wight) with beast flavour reskin.

**Mark all Maw-changed creature stat blocks as provisional homebrew. Do not treat them as canon until Chapter 3 material or later explicitly establishes this creature type.**

## Karsac Adaptation Notes

The visual hook is the design requirement. Whatever chassis is used, the bone disc geometry must be present in the creature's appearance. This is not explained in the scene — the players see it and either recognise the pattern or do not. Do not draw attention to the connection unless a player makes the check or makes the observation.

## Player-Safe Use

Players may perceive: wrongness of proportion, a pattern in the scarring that resembles something they have seen before, behaviour that suggests something other than ordinary predation, the sense that the creature has been changed by a place rather than born into what it is. Players must not be told what the Maw is at the cosmological level or what was taken from it.

## Canon Status

**Provisional.** The Maw's opening and the removal of something from it are canon (Ch2 closing). Maw-changed creatures as a type are an extrapolation — the canon establishes the conditions without specifying what remains inside or whether changed creatures exist.
