# Adversary: The Weaver

## Design Intent
This adversary is designed to be a subtle infiltrator within Karsac cities and towns, operating independently of Mathr and Vishara, but potentially aligned with broader Yngondi interests. They are primarily social manipulators and information gatherers, capable of limited combat but preferring to avoid direct confrontation. This is a social-led threat with a hybrid combat capability, intended to create tension and paranoia within settlements.

## Mechanical Base
Base: monsters/srd-2014/spy
Reason: The Spy’s skillset – stealth, deception, and a baseline of combat ability – perfectly embodies the Weaver’s need to blend in and react to exposure.

## Adaptation Summary
- Kept from base: Languages, ability scores
- Changed from base: Finalised around the repaired stat block, faction doctrine, and settlement-cover behaviour.
- Added: Prepared Cover, Mapped Exits, No Last Stand, Information First
- Removed: Draft-only or unsupported claims not present in the final stat block.
- Mechanical risk: Normalized after final deterministic repair.

## Tactical Notes  
The Weaver’s restraint is not mercy—it is *discipline*. They use violence only to:  
- Preserve the mission (e.g., destroying evidence).  
- Escape (e.g., using **Dagger** to disable pursuers).  
- Prevent exposure (e.g., eliminating witnesses).  

They will **never** engage in combat for the sake of pride or ideology. Their tools are deception, misdirection, and the urban environment itself. If cornered, they will *disappear*—leaving behind only confusion and unanswered questions.

## Stat Block

**The Weaver**
*Medium, Humanoid, Neutral*

**Armour Class** 13 (Leather Armor)
**Hit Points** 33 (6d8 + 6)
**Speed** 30 ft.

**Saving Throws** Int +4, Wis +3
**Skills** Deception +7, Insight +3, Investigation +4, Perception +3, Persuasion +7, Stealth +4
**Senses** Passive Perception 13
**Languages** Common, Trade Tongue (local), Shadow Walker Sign
**Challenge** 3 (700 XP) · Proficiency Bonus +2

### Traits
**Local Reputation:** The Weaver has a carefully cultivated reputation within the community, granting advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks when interacting with locals who recognize them.
**Subtle Influence:** The Weaver can subtly influence the opinions of those around them. Once per long rest, they can grant advantage on a single Charisma check made by an ally within 30 feet.
**False Identity:** The Weaver maintains a false identity, complete with fabricated history and contacts. They can spend 1 minute to change their appearance and backstory, gaining advantage on Deception checks related to their assumed persona.
**Prepared Cover.** The Weaver has advantage on Charisma (Deception) checks made to maintain a prepared civilian identity or pass as an unremarkable worker, clerk, or passerby in the current settlement.
**Mapped Exits.** If the Weaver has spent at least 10 minutes in a settlement location before combat begins, it has advantage on initiative. During the first round of combat, it can move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
**No Last Stand.** The Weaver does not fight to the death. If reduced below half its hit points, exposed by name, or separated from its cover identity, it attempts to flee, surrender under a false identity, or create a public misdirection.
**Information First.** When the Weaver is reduced below half its hit points, captured, or forced to reveal information, it can conceal, destroy, or pass on one carried note, cipher strip, ledger scrap, or marked bone tally.

### Actions
**Dagger:** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, range 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing damage.
**False Lead:** *Action:* One creature the Weaver can speak to within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom (Insight) check or pursue a planted false lead until the end of its next turn.
**Persuasion:** *Action:* The Weaver attempts to persuade a target. They add their proficiency bonus to the check.

### Bonus Actions
None

### Reactions
**Evasive Maneuver:** When a creature misses the Weaver with an attack, the Weaver can move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
**Crowd Break.** When the Weaver is hit or missed by an attack while at least two non-hostile creatures or significant cover are within 30 feet, it can move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

## Variant Options
**Choose 2 traits from:**
* **Network Contact:** The Weaver has a contact within a local organization (e.g., merchant guild, temple, guard). They can use this contact to gather information or request assistance.
* **Keen Observer:** The Weaver has advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks to detect lies and hidden motives.
* **Resourceful:** The Weaver can scavenge supplies and information from their surroundings, allowing them to regain 1d4 hit points or gain advantage on an ability check related to gathering information.
* **Silver Tongue:** The Weaver gains advantage on Persuasion checks against creatures who are easily flattered or manipulated.

**Choose 1 signature action from:**
* **Distraction:** *Action:* The Weaver creates a minor distraction (e.g., a dropped item, a staged argument) to divert attention. Creatures within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 11 Wisdom saving throw or have disadvantage on their next attack roll.
* **Misdirection:** *Action:* The Weaver attempts to mislead a creature, causing it to believe something false. The target must succeed on a DC 13 Intelligence (Investigation) check or believe the Weaver's false statement.

**Choose 1 reaction from:**
* **Swift Retreat:** When the Weaver takes damage, they can use their reaction to Disengage and move up to half their speed.

## Tactics
The Weaver prioritizes observation and information gathering. They avoid direct confrontation, preferring to manipulate situations and people to achieve their goals. If confronted, they attempt to use deception and misdirection to escape or turn the situation to their advantage. They will attempt to create diversions and exploit weaknesses in their opponents' defenses. If exposed, they will flee and attempt to disappear into the crowd.

## Doctrine Under Pressure  
On round one of combat, the Weaver’s priority is **immediate evasion** and **information preservation**. They will:  
- Use **Mapped Exits** to move toward pre-familiar routes, turning the battlefield into a maze of escape options.  
- Activate **Crowd Break** to leverage nearby civilians or environmental obstacles, creating movement lanes through the chaos.  
- Deploy **False Identity** or **Social Misdirection** to confuse attackers, framing bystanders or fabricating alibis.  
- If cornered, they will use **Information First** to discard or conceal mission-critical data before attempting negotiation or surrender.  
- **Never** reveal faction ties, mission details, or the bone disc’s significance—these are classified even under duress.  

Their goal is to *disappear into the city* rather than engage in prolonged combat.

## Social / Investigation Use
The Weaver’s deception pattern is subtle and layered. They ask seemingly innocuous questions to gather information about individuals and organizations. They notice inconsistencies in people’s stories and behavior. They reveal information accidentally, often through carefully crafted lies designed to elicit a specific response. They use ledgers, cargo lists, and harbor records to create the illusion of legitimacy.

## Doctrine-Expressive Mechanics
- **Cover Identity / Unremarkable Presence.** The Weaver stays legible as ordinary city life first, using concealment or a prepared civilian identity to remain forgettable until pressure closes in.
- **Mapped Exits.** Pre-read routes and room geometry let The Weaver turn round-one pressure into movement instead of paralysis.
- **No Last Stand.** Controlled withdrawal matters more than pride; once exposed or bloodied, The Weaver chooses escape, false surrender, or public misdirection over dying in place.
- **Crowd Break.** The city itself becomes cover under pressure, turning contact with the party into fresh movement lanes through bodies, stalls, and obstructed sightlines.
- **Information First.** Messages, tallies, and scraps are preserved or denied before retaliation; the mission survives even if the operative does not keep control of the scene.
- **Social Misdirection.** The Weaver solves pressure socially where possible, using false certainty, harmless pretexts, and shifting public attention before steel becomes the only answer.

## Player-Safe Description  
A seemingly unremarkable individual, perhaps a merchant’s assistant or a dockworker, blends seamlessly into the background. Their clothing is plain but well-maintained, and their demeanor is polite, unassuming, and keenly observant. They may carry a small ledger or tally stick, which they inspect with quiet intensity. Subtle cues—like a faint scar hidden beneath their collar or a peculiar habit of adjusting their gloves—hint at a life lived in the shadows, but nothing confirms it.

## DM-Only Notes  
The Weaver is gathering information on a specific target or location for an unknown purpose, likely tied to a Yngondi directive. They are **not** directly controlled by Mathr or Vishara but operate under the guidance of a local Shadow Walker cell. They believe the bone disc symbol is a mark of prestige, unaware of its true connection to Vishara.  

- **Motivation**: Their mission may involve sabotage, espionage, or protecting a Yngondi asset.  
- **Vulnerabilities**: Compromise of their false identity (e.g., recognizing a prepared persona) or exposure of their ledger/tally.  
- **Lore Hook**: The Weaver may have a personal stake in the mission—e.g., a family member tied to the target or a debt to their handler.  
- **Escalation**: If cornered, they may trigger a **Network Contact** to summon allies or use **Disguise Self** (if scaled) to vanish entirely.  

This adversary is a Shadow Walker operative working within a local cell, operating under the radar of both Yngondi and their rivals.

## Scaling Options

- Stronger version: Increase hit points to 54, grant advantage on all saving throws, add the *Disguise Self* spell.
- Non-combat version: Remove the Dagger action, increase Intelligence and Wisdom scores, grant proficiency in Investigation and History.

## Doctrine  
The Weaver’s doctrine is rooted in *survivability through anonymity* and *mission preservation over individual sacrifice*. They operate as a ghost in the machine of city life, leveraging prepared identities, environmental cover, and social manipulation to remain legible as ordinary citizens until pressure forces them into action. Key principles include:  
- **Cover Identity / Unremarkable Presence**: The Weaver’s primary role is to stay unnoticed, using prepared personas and environmental blending to avoid detection.  
- **Mapped Exits**: Pre-familiarity with urban layouts allows them to turn combat initiation into immediate movement, avoiding paralysis under pressure.  
- **No Last Stand**: Controlled withdrawal is paramount; once compromised, they prioritize escape, false surrender, or public misdirection over dying in place.  
- **Crowd Break**: Urban density becomes a tactical asset, using bystanders, obstacles, and sightline obstructions to create movement lanes and evade pursuit.  
- **Information First**: Messages, ledgers, or tallies are preserved or destroyed before retaliation, ensuring the mission’s survival even if the operative is captured.  
- **Social Misdirection**: They solve pressure through social engineering, using false certainty, harmless pretexts, or shifting public attention to avoid escalation.  

Their actions are guided by a cold calculus: *the mission survives, the operative may not*.  

---

## Behavioural Stages  
1. **Observation Phase (Pre-Combat)**: The Weaver blends into the environment, asking innocuous questions, noting inconsistencies, and gathering intel through subtle social probing. They avoid direct confrontation, using **Social Misdirection** to deflect suspicion.  
2. **Engagement Phase (Round 1–3)**: If pressured into combat, they use **False Lead**, **Dagger**, or **Distraction** to create diversions. They prioritize **Crowd Break** and **Evasive Maneuver** to maintain mobility.  
3. **Controlled Withdrawal (Round 4+)**: Once compromised or bloodied, they initiate **No Last Stand** protocols—fleeing, surrendering with false terms, or detonating **Information First** to erase mission traces. They may use **Network Contact** or **Resourceful** traits to regroup or manipulate allies.
