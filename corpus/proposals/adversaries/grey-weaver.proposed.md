# Adversary: Grey Weaver

## Design Intent
This adversary is a Shadow Walker operative designed to infiltrate and operate within Karsac cities and towns, gathering information and subtly influencing events. They are not directly linked to Mathr or Vishara, operating as a self-contained cell within the larger Shadow Walker organization. Their role is primarily social and intelligence-gathering, with combat as a last resort. Threat level is moderate – a significant obstacle to player investigation and social maneuvering, but not a direct combat threat unless provoked. Use case: creating social pressure, extracting information, subtly manipulating local events, and providing a recurring, unsettling presence.

## Mechanical Base
Base: monsters/srd-2014/spy
Reason: The Spy's skillset – stealth, deception, and information gathering – provides a perfect foundation for a covert urban operative. It also establishes a baseline for a character who is more comfortable with social interaction than direct confrontation.

## Adaptation Summary
- Kept from base: Languages, ability scores
- Changed from base: Finalised around the repaired stat block, faction doctrine, and settlement-cover behaviour.
- Added: Urban Camouflage, Contacts, Prepared Cover, Mapped Exits
- Removed: Draft-only or unsupported claims not present in the final stat block.
- Mechanical risk: Normalized after final deterministic repair.

## Tactical Notes  
Their restraint is not mercy—it is a calculated discipline to preserve the mission. They use violence only to escape, protect their contacts, or prevent exposure of Shadow Walkers’ operations. Social manipulation and environmental exploitation (e.g., *Crowd Break*, *Mapped Exits*) are prioritized over direct combat. If forced into a fight, they aim to wound rather than kill, ensuring they can retreat and regroup.

### Traits
**Urban Camouflage:** The Grey Weaver has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks made to hide in urban environments. They can spend 1 minute to subtly alter their appearance to better blend in with the local population.
**Contacts:** The Grey Weaver has a network of informants and contacts within the city. They can use these contacts to gather information or create diversions (DM’s discretion).
**Prepared Cover.** Grey Weaver has advantage on Charisma (Deception) checks made to maintain a prepared civilian identity or pass as an unremarkable worker, clerk, or passerby in the current settlement.
**Mapped Exits.** If Grey Weaver has spent at least 10 minutes in a settlement location before combat begins, it has advantage on initiative. During the first round of combat, it can move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
**No Last Stand.** Grey Weaver does not fight to the death. If reduced below half its hit points, exposed by name, or separated from its cover identity, it attempts to flee, surrender under a false identity, or create a public misdirection.
**Information First.** When Grey Weaver is reduced below half its hit points, captured, or forced to reveal information, it can conceal, destroy, or pass on one carried note, cipher strip, ledger scrap, or marked bone tally.

### Actions
**Dagger:** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing damage.
**Throwing Spike.** Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, range 20/60 ft., one target. Hit: 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution saving throw or its speed is reduced by 10 feet until the end of its next turn.
**Subtle Influence (Action):** The Grey Weaver attempts to subtly influence a creature within 30 feet. The target must succeed on a DC 13 Wisdom (Insight) check or be charmed for 1 round. While charmed, the target is more receptive to the Grey Weaver's suggestions and is at disadvantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks against them.  (This does not grant the Grey Weaver control, only increased persuasiveness.)

### Bonus Actions
None

### Reactions
**Swift Dodge:** When an attacker moves to attack the Grey Weaver, they can use their reaction to move up to half their speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
**Crowd Break.** When Grey Weaver is hit or missed by an attack while at least two non-hostile creatures or significant cover are within 30 feet, it can move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

## Tactics
The Grey Weaver’s opening move is observation and blending. They prioritize gathering information and identifying potential targets before taking action. They prefer to operate from the shadows, using their Urban Camouflage to remain unnoticed. If confronted, they attempt to use Subtle Influence to de-escalate the situation and gather more information. Escape is prioritized over engagement. They will not engage in prolonged combat unless cornered.

## Doctrine Under Pressure  
On round one of combat, the Grey Weaver immediately seeks to disengage using *Swift Dodge* or *Crowd Break* to exploit environmental cover and crowd density. They will use *Mapped Exits* to navigate toward pre-identified escape routes, prioritizing movement over confrontation. If escape is unfeasible, they deploy *Information First* to discard or conceal critical data before retreating, ensuring the mission’s survival. They will never reveal Shadow Walkers’ operations, their cell’s existence, or the coded message’s contents. Even under duress, they maintain a focus on controlled withdrawal, using *No Last Stand* to choose between false surrender, public misdirection, or vanishing into the city’s anonymity.

## Social / Investigation Use
The Grey Weaver uses subtle questioning and observation to extract information. They ask seemingly innocuous questions about local events, trade routes, and prominent figures. They are adept at noticing inconsistencies in people's stories and identifying potential vulnerabilities. They reveal information accidentally only when attempting to mislead investigators. They use ledgers, cargo manifests, and harbor records to appear legitimate.

## Doctrine-Expressive Mechanics
- **Cover Identity / Unremarkable Presence.** Grey Weaver stays legible as ordinary city life first, using concealment or a prepared civilian identity to remain forgettable until pressure closes in.
- **Mapped Exits.** Pre-read routes and room geometry let Grey Weaver turn round-one pressure into movement instead of paralysis.
- **No Last Stand.** Controlled withdrawal matters more than pride; once exposed or bloodied, Grey Weaver chooses escape, false surrender, or public misdirection over dying in place.
- **Crowd Break.** The city itself becomes cover under pressure, turning contact with the party into fresh movement lanes through bodies, stalls, and obstructed sightlines.
- **Information First.** Messages, tallies, and scraps are preserved or denied before retaliation; the mission survives even if the operative does not keep control of the scene.
- **Social Misdirection.** Grey Weaver solves pressure socially where possible, using false certainty, harmless pretexts, and shifting public attention before steel becomes the only answer.

## Player-Safe Description  
This individual moves through the city with the quiet confidence of someone who knows every alley and backstreet. They dress plainly, speak softly, and observe everything with an unassuming intensity. Their presence is almost imperceptible—until they’re gone, leaving only the faintest impression of someone who was never meant to be noticed.

## DM-Only Notes  
This Grey Weaver is part of a small, independent Shadow Walkers’ cell operating outside the main organization’s direct oversight. They are focused on gathering intelligence on local power structures and trade routes, unaware of Vishara’s influence in Karsac. Their coded message contains critical information for a contact in a neighboring city, making them a high-priority target for factions seeking to intercept Shadow Walkers’ communications. They are deeply paranoid about exposure, carrying no weapons or tools that could link them to the Shadow Walkers. If captured, they will refuse to cooperate, preferring to die or disappear rather than reveal their affiliations.

## Doctrine  
The Grey Weaver operates under the principle of *survival through obscurity*. Their primary objective is to gather intelligence on local political, economic, and social developments while maintaining anonymity. They use prepared civilian identities, urban camouflage, and social misdirection to remain unremarkable until pressure forces them into action. If confronted, they prioritize de-escalation through *Subtle Influence* or creating distractions to escape. Their mission survives even if the operative is exposed or forced to retreat—coded messages, ledgers, or false leads are preserved or discarded to protect the Shadow Walkers’ larger objectives. Violence is a last resort, used only to preserve the mission, escape, or prevent exposure of their affiliations.

## Behavioural Stages  
1. **Observation and Blending** (Pre-Engagement): The Grey Weaver moves through the city with deliberate casualness, observing trade routes, public interactions, and prominent figures. They ask innocuous questions to gather intel and test the environment for threats.  
2. **De-escalation and Manipulation** (Early Engagement): If approached, they attempt *Subtle Influence* to charm or misdirect targets, using social persuasion to avoid confrontation. They may plant false leads or use *Social Misdirection* to shift attention away from themselves.  
3. **Distraction and Escape** (Mid-Engagement): If cornered, they create diversions (e.g., throwing spikes, triggering *Misdirection* or *False Lead*) to buy time for retreat. They use *Crowd Break* to navigate through obstacles or bystanders.  
4. **Controlled Withdrawal** (Late Engagement): If forced into combat, they prioritize disengagement over killing. They may leave behind a coded message, ledger scrap, or marked tally to ensure the mission’s continuity, even if they are captured or killed.
