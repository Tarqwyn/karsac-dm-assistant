# Encounter: The Silent Toll

## Encounter Type
Social Obstruction / Information Extraction

## Campaign Purpose
To demonstrate Mathr's subtle influence along the fjord road, create a social dilemma for the party regarding the Mathr token, and subtly hint at the approaching threat of Dugweb's visit. It also connects to Brynja's backstory and the artefact.

## Cast
* **Tollkeeper (Karsac: Commoner):** Collects the road toll. Wants to complete his duties without incident. Knows the road is under unusual scrutiny. Must not know about Mathr's involvement or the party’s mission. (Mechanical Base: Commoner)
* **Watcher (Karsac: Spy):** Observes the party and the Tollkeeper. Wants to assess the party's reaction to the toll situation. Knows the party possesses a Mathr token. Must not know about Brynja's artefact or the bone disc. (Mechanical Base: Spy)
* **Cart Driver (Karsac: Commoner):** A local farmer, visibly nervous. Wants to get his goods to market quickly. Knows the road is becoming increasingly unsafe. Must not know about the party’s possessions or the Watcher’s presence. (Mechanical Base: Commoner)

## Opening Beat
The party arrives at a stone toll booth built into a narrow section of the fjord road, the last settlement behind them fading into the twilight. A lone Tollkeeper collects the passage fee, while a seemingly innocuous Cart Driver waits nearby. An unnervingly quiet Watcher stands a short distance away, partially obscured by the shadows of the surrounding cliffs, observing the scene with an intensity that feels out of place.

## What the Opposition Wants
The Tollkeeper wants to collect the toll and maintain order. The Watcher wants to gauge the party’s reaction to the toll and assess the value of the Mathr token they possess. He is looking for signs of defiance, willingness to bribe, or attempts to conceal information. The Cart Driver wants to simply be on his way.

## What the Players Can Notice
* **DC 10:** The Tollkeeper is overly meticulous, checking the party's belongings with unusual thoroughness.
* **DC 12:** The Watcher’s gaze is fixed on the party, and he seems to be subtly directing the Tollkeeper's actions.
* **DC 15:** The seal on the Toll receipt bears the Mathr sigil, though it's slightly smudged, as if hastily applied.
* **DC 18+:** The Watcher is using subtle hand signals to communicate with someone out of sight.

## Pressure Ladder
Low: The Tollkeeper demands a standard toll payment and conducts a cursory search.
Medium: The Tollkeeper demands additional fees or information, citing vague "new regulations." The Watcher becomes more overt in his observation.
High: The Tollkeeper accuses the party of smuggling and threatens to detain them until a patrol arrives. The Cart Driver attempts to flee.

## Checks and Mechanics
* **Persuasion (DC 13):** Success – The Tollkeeper accepts a reasonable explanation and lets the party pass. Partial Success – The Tollkeeper demands a slightly higher toll. Failure – The Tollkeeper becomes suspicious and demands further scrutiny.
* **Deception (DC 15):** Success – The party successfully hides the Mathr token or their true intentions. Partial Success – The Tollkeeper notices something is amiss but doesn't press the issue. Failure – The Tollkeeper becomes suspicious and demands to see what they're hiding.
* **Insight (DC 12):** Success – The player correctly identifies the Watcher's role and his connection to the Tollkeeper. Partial Success – The player senses something is wrong but can't pinpoint the source. Failure – The player dismisses the situation as normal.
* **Investigation (DC 15):** Success – The player finds evidence of the Watcher’s communication (a faint trail leading away from the road). Partial Success – The player notices the smudged Mathr sigil. Failure – The player finds nothing of interest.
* **Stealth (DC 14):** Success – The party can discreetly attempt to pass the toll without attracting attention. Partial Success – The party can avoid direct eye contact with the Watcher. Failure – The Watcher notices their attempt at concealment.

## Player Choices
* **Comply:** Pay the toll and move on quickly.
* **Challenge:** Confront the Tollkeeper and demand an explanation for the unusual scrutiny.
* **Bribe:** Attempt to pay off the Tollkeeper to expedite their passage.
* **Split Group:** Have one character distract the Tollkeeper while others attempt to slip past.
* **Distraction:** Create a diversion to draw attention away from the party.
* **Follow:** Secretly track the Watcher after passing the toll.
* **Expose:** Publicly accuse the Tollkeeper of corruption.

## Outcomes
* **Clear Success:** The party pays the toll, avoids conflict, and notes the Watcher’s presence. They gain a sense of unease but continue their journey.
* **Partial Success:** The party resolves the situation through negotiation or bribery but attracts unwanted attention. The Watcher reports their actions to someone unseen.
* **Costly Success:** The party exposes the Watcher, but the Tollkeeper calls for reinforcements, delaying their progress significantly.
* **Failure:** The party is detained, and their possessions are searched. The Mathr token is discovered, leading to a more serious confrontation.
* **Fail-Forward:** The party successfully passes, but the Watcher’s report triggers a later, more significant encounter.

## Combat Fallback
If the party attempts to fight, the Tollkeeper is a non-combatant. The Watcher (Spy) is agile and evasive, prioritizing escape over confrontation. The Cart Driver flees in terror.

## State Updates
* **Party:** Increased suspicion of Mathr’s influence. Possible loss of time. Potential for a future encounter with the Watcher.
* **Campaign:** Mathr’s reach is subtly demonstrated. The Watcher’s report adds another layer to the ongoing investigation.

## Follow-up Hooks
1. The Watcher reappears later on the road, indicating that someone is actively tracking the party.
2. Brynja remembers a similar incident from her past, revealing a connection to Mathr’s network.
3. The Cart Driver, if spared, becomes a potential informant, offering information about the Watcher’s movements.
4. The Mathr token proves to be more valuable than initially thought, attracting the attention of other factions.

## NPC Specific Details
**Tollkeeper (Karsac: Commoner):**
* **What he wants:** To perform his duties and go home.
* **What he knows:** The road is under increased scrutiny, and he's been given vague instructions to be observant.
* **What he must not know:** He is being used as a pawn by Mathr.

**Watcher (Karsac: Spy):**
* **What he wants:** To assess the party’s reaction and report back.
* **What he knows:** The party has a Mathr token and is travelling north.
* **What he must not know:** The full purpose of the party’s journey or the nature of Brynja’s artefact.

**Cart Driver (Karsac: Commoner):**
* **What he wants:** To deliver his goods and return home safely.
* **What he knows:** The road is becoming increasingly dangerous.
* **What he must not know:** The party’s possessions or the Watcher’s presence.

## Story Beat  
As the party approaches the toll booth, the air grows unnervingly still, as if the fjord itself holds its breath. The Tollkeeper’s movements are precise but jittery, his voice monotone as he recites the toll. The Cart Driver’s cart is loaded with crates marked with faded runes—symbols the party might recognize from Brynja’s stories about old trade routes. The Watcher’s presence is a quiet violation: his stance is too rigid to be natural, and his eyes flicker between the party and the Tollkeeper like a signal being passed. A faint scent of burnt sage lingers near the toll booth, a fragrance associated with Mathr’s rituals in whispered tales. The road ahead is shrouded in mist, but the party can’t shake the feeling that the fjord’s “natural” fog is being guided by unseen hands.

## Pressure  
The pressure manifests in layers:  
- **Low:** The Tollkeeper’s demands are clinical, his tone devoid of warmth. The Cart Driver’s nervous glances at the cliffs suggest he’s been warned about something—*someone*—on the road.  
- **Medium:** The Watcher’s subtle gestures to the Tollkeeper grow more frequent, and the Tollkeeper begins asking invasive questions about the party’s origins. The Cart Driver’s cart creaks ominously, as if the weight of his cargo is more than it should be.  
- **High:** The Tollkeeper accuses the party of carrying “forbidden items” and produces a rusted lockbox from his booth, its surface etched with the same smudged Mathr sigil seen on the toll receipt. The Cart Driver panics, dropping a crate that spills out shards of glass and something that glows faintly—*a shard of Brynja’s artefact, lost during the Törweg ambush*.

## Player Choice  
The encounter demands decisions that intertwine with the party’s holdings and secrets:  
- **Conceal:** Hide the Mathr token or Brynja’s artefact, risking discovery but avoiding immediate conflict.  
- **Expose:** Use the Tollkeeper’s scrutiny to subtly reveal the Watcher’s allegiance to Mathr, forcing a choice between confrontation or complicity.  
- **Leverage:** Use the Cart Driver’s distress to redirect attention, potentially uncovering the shard of Brynja’s artefact and its connection to her past.  
- **Negotiate:** Offer the Tollkeeper a bribe tied to the Mathr token’s value, risking a test of its authenticity.  
- **Investigate:** Search the toll booth for clues about the Watcher’s orders, possibly uncovering a hidden message linked to Dugweb’s upcoming visit.  
- **Retreat:** Attempt to leave without paying, triggering a cascade of escalating threats from the Watcher and Tollkeeper.

## Complication  
The Watcher’s presence is a double-edged blade: he is both an obstacle and a puzzle. His orders are vague, but his actions suggest he is testing the party’s willingness to comply with Mathr’s unspoken demands. If the party reveals the Mathr token, the Watcher’s loyalty to his handlers becomes clear—but his fear of retaliation keeps him from acting directly. Meanwhile, the Cart Driver’s accidental spill of the artefact shard creates a new variable: Brynja’s backstory is now inescapably tied to the encounter, and the shard’s glow may attract attention from factions beyond Mathr.

## Consequence  
The encounter’s resolution leaves scars:  
- **Success:** The party avoids direct conflict, but the Watcher’s report ensures Mathr’s network is now aware of their presence. The Cart Driver’s spilled shard becomes a lingering threat, potentially drawing Shadow Walkers or other factions to the road.  
- **Partial Success:** The party’s actions (e.g., bribery or negotiation) buy time but leave them marked as “untrustworthy” in Mathr’s eyes. The Watcher may later reappear with more direct demands.  
- **Failure:** The party’s defiance triggers a lockdown of the road, forcing them into a combat fallback with the Watcher and Tollkeeper’s allies. The Cart Driver’s shard is lost, and Brynja’s artefact’s connection to the fjord road is now a public secret.

## Fail-Forward Path  
If the party is detained or forced into combat, the encounter does not end cleanly. The Watcher escapes, leaving behind a cryptic message carved into the toll booth: *“The fjord remembers.”* This message becomes a clue for a later encounter, where the party must confront a Mathr-aligned faction that has been “watching” their journey since the Törweg ambush. The Cart Driver, if spared, becomes a reluctant ally, offering fragmented knowledge about the Watcher’s movements in exchange for protection from the “new road patrols.” The shard’s glow also draws the attention of a Lösweg housecarl, whose honour-bound duty to investigate the artefact creates a new thread of conflict.
