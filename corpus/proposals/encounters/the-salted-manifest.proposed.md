# Encounter: The Salted Manifest

## Encounter Type
Procedural Delay & Information Extraction

## Campaign Purpose
To subtly delay the party’s departure from Valweg, allowing Mathr agents to assess their cargo and contacts while creating a sense of unease and suspicion. It serves as a subtle warning about Mathr’s influence and a potential prelude to a more direct confrontation later.

## Cast
* **Harbormaster:** Noble (npc-bases/srd-2014/noble), Wants: To appear neutral, maintain order. Knows: The manifest is subtly altered. Must Not Know: The full extent of Mathr’s involvement.
* **Inspector 1:** Spy (npc-bases/srd-2014/spy), Wants: To identify illicit goods and potential allies/enemies within the party. Knows: The altered manifest details. Must Not Know: The Harbormaster’s awareness of the alteration.
* **Inspector 2:** Guard (npc-bases/srd-2014/guard), Wants: To follow orders and maintain a visible presence. Knows: The party is being scrutinized. Must Not Know: The Inspectors’ true motives.
* **Dockworker (Witness):** Commoner (npc-bases/srd-2014/commoner), Wants: To finish his shift. Knows: He saw Inspector 1 subtly adjust a ledger. Must Not Know: The significance of what he saw.

## Opening Beat
The party arrives at Valweg’s bustling docks, eager to depart on their vessel. However, the Harbormaster informs them there's a "slight discrepancy" in their manifest requiring further inspection. Two stern-looking inspectors, one sharp-eyed and the other imposing, are already present, blocking access to the ship. As the Harbormaster speaks, a crate marked "Non-Perishables" from the party’s cargo is inexplicably missing—**a detail the Harbormaster insists is unrelated to the inspection**.

## What the Opposition Wants
The Inspectors want to confirm the contents of the party’s cargo, identify any hidden items or individuals, and assess the party’s connections within Valweg. The delay is meant to observe and gather information, not to overtly accuse. **A hidden objective: Inspector 1 is subtly testing the party’s awareness of Mathr’s presence through seemingly innocuous questions.**

## What the Players Can Notice
* "The questions are too specific for a routine inspection." (DC 12 useful - Inspectors inquire about the weight of specific crates, the origin of a particular spice, and the name of the ship's carpenter.)
* "The seal on the document is the Mathr sigil — you have seen it before." (DC 15 deep suspicion - A faint, almost invisible Mathr sigil is pressed into the wax seal of the manifest, only visible under close scrutiny.)
* **"The missing crate is a trap."** (DC 13 investigation - The crate’s absence is a deliberate provocation. If the party mentions it, Inspector 1 becomes slightly more hostile.)
* Dockworker (Witness) can be observed (DC 10 obvious) looking nervous and glancing at Inspector 1.

## Pressure Ladder
**Low:** Harbormaster insists on a thorough manifest review, causing a 15-minute delay.  
**Medium:** Inspector 1 requests a full cargo inspection, requiring the party to unload and repack.  
**High:** Inspector 2 accuses the party of attempting to smuggle goods, threatening detention and a formal investigation.  
**Critical:** If the party mentions the missing crate, Inspector 1 **explicitly accuses them of "tampering with official records"** and demands the crate’s return, escalating the inspection to a full search of the ship.

## Checks and Mechanics
* **Insight (DC 13):** Success - Recognize the Inspectors' subtle tension and suspicion. Partial - Notice one Inspector seems more agitated than the other. Failure - Miss the underlying hostility.  
* **Investigation (DC 14):** Success - Discover the faint Mathr sigil on the manifest. Partial - Notice the Dockworker’s nervousness. Failure - Miss any subtle clues.  
* **Persuasion (DC 15):** Success - Convince the Harbormaster to expedite the process. Partial - Reduce the delay by 5 minutes. Failure - The Harbormaster remains firm.  
* **Deception (DC 12):** Success - Successfully mislead the Inspectors about a minor detail. Partial - Buy a few minutes of breathing room. Failure - Raise the Inspectors’ suspicion.  
* **Sleight of Hand (DC 16):** Success - Discreetly swap a document. Partial - Briefly distract an Inspector. Failure - Alert the Inspectors to the attempted deception.  
* **Perception (DC 14):** Success - Notice Inspector 1’s subtle hand movements (e.g., adjusting a ledger, fiddling with a pocket). Partial - Spot Inspector 1’s discomfort when asked about the missing crate. Failure - Miss all physical cues.

## Player Choices
* **Comply:** Accept the inspection and answer questions honestly.  
* **Challenge:** Confront the Harbormaster or Inspectors, demanding an explanation.  
* **Bribe:** Attempt to influence the Harbormaster or Inspectors with a discreet payment.  
* **Distraction:** Create a diversion to allow a party member to slip away or alter the manifest.  
* **Follow:** After the inspection, discreetly follow one of the inspectors to learn more.  
* **Reveal the Missing Crate:** Actively question the Harbormaster about the crate’s disappearance, risking escalation.

## Complication
**The crate’s absence is a test.** If the party investigates, they learn the crate was **never part of their cargo**—it was a decoy placed by Mathr to provoke a reaction. If the party insists on finding it, Inspector 1 **refuses to cooperate**, claiming the crate was "never registered." This forces the party to choose between escalating tensions or accepting the inspection’s outcome.

## Consequence
* **Clear Success:** The party quickly resolves the inspection, departing with minimal delay and no suspicion. (Requires excellent Persuasion or a clever distraction.)  
* **Partial Success:** The party departs, but with a significant delay and a lingering feeling of being watched. (Requires a mix of successful and failed checks.)  
* **Costly Success:** The party departs, but a valuable item is confiscated or a contact is exposed. (Requires a failed Deception check or a poorly executed bribe.)  
* **Failure:** The party is detained for questioning, delaying their departure significantly and attracting unwanted attention. (Requires multiple failed checks and a confrontational approach.)  
* **Fail-Forward Consequence:** Regardless of the outcome, the Dockworker remembers the party and their interactions, potentially becoming a source of information or a target for Mathr’s attention later. **If the party mentions the missing crate, Mathr’s interest in them escalates immediately—** a **Mathr Agent (spy)** is now monitoring the party’s movements through Valweg’s underworld networks.

## Combat Fallback
If challenged aggressively, Inspector 2 (Guard) will attempt to physically restrain the party. Combat is discouraged, but a brief scuffle is possible. Generic NPC bases apply.

## State Updates
* **Party Reputation:** Slightly decreased in Valweg.  
* **Mathr Awareness:** The party is now on Mathr’s radar. **If the missing crate was mentioned, Mathr’s interest is heightened.**  
* **New Contact:** The Dockworker might become a potential informant. **If the party failed an Investigation check, the Dockworker is now under Mathr’s surveillance.**

## Follow-up Hooks
1. The Dockworker approaches the party seeking assistance, claiming he was threatened by someone connected to the inspection.  
2. A package arrives at the party’s next destination, containing a cryptic message referencing the Valweg inspection.  
3. A known associate of the party is arrested on fabricated charges, seemingly linked to the events at the dock.  
4. **Mathr’s Interest:** A Mathr Agent (spy) appears in a future encounter, subtly manipulating events to test the party’s awareness of their presence.  
5. **The Missing Crate’s Origin:** A clue is left in the party’s ship (e.g., a hidden compartment with a Mathr sigil), suggesting the crate was never theirs but was planted to provoke a reaction.
