# Public Accusation

## Pattern Summary

A faction makes a move in public — an accusation of theft, fraud, law-breaking, or association with an enemy — in a space where the crowd can see and react. The goal is to force the party into a defensive position, create a pretext for detention, or damage their standing. The crowd's reaction is part of the pressure.

## Use When

- A faction needs a legal or social pretext to act against the party openly.
- The party's reputation or access in this location is important to their mission.
- The DM wants a social encounter where the crowd is part of the dynamic.

## Do Not Use When

- The party is anonymous and has nothing to lose publicly.
- The faction would rather act in private.

## Scene Structure

1. A public space — a market, a dock, a council hall, a street.
2. The accuser steps forward, formally or dramatically.
3. The accusation is specific enough to be plausible but difficult to refute immediately.
4. The crowd watches. Officials may be present to enforce.
5. The party responds — denial, counter-evidence, or a counter-question that reframes the scene.

## Pressure Ladder

**Low:** A pointed accusation from across the market. The accuser wants the crowd's attention.
**Medium:** Official witnesses present. The accusation is in a formal register.
**High:** Guards or officials move to enforce the accusation. Escape becomes a flight, not a retreat.

## Useful NPC Bases

- Primary accuser: **Noble** (Persuasion +5, Intimidation +5, Deception +5)
- Official backing the accusation: **Veteran** (AC 17, HP 58, legal authority)
- Crowd agents: **Commoner** (planted to react, DC 13 Insight to identify)
- Watching coordinator: **Spy** (Insight +4, signals to the accuser from the crowd)

## Common Checks

- **Persuasion DC 14** — address the crowd directly and undermine the accusation's framing
- **Deception DC 15** — deny convincingly in a high-attention context
- **Intimidation DC 15** — force the accuser to back down publicly
- **Insight DC 13** — identify the political interest behind the accusation
- **Performance DC 12** — project calm authority that reassures the crowd
- **History DC 13** — cite relevant law, precedent, or the accuser's own record

## Fail-forward Consequences

- The accusation is not fully resolved — the crowd is divided, the party is suspicious.
- The party refutes the accusation but the accuser's faction has marked them publicly.
- Partial success: the party escapes detention but the accusation is on record somewhere.

## Example Karsac Uses

- A Mathr intermediary publicly accuses the party of smuggling at the Valweg dock.
- A Lösweg council member accuses the party of entering the archives without authority.
- A faction plants evidence and calls a public official to witness the party's arrest.
