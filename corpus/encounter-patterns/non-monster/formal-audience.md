# Formal Audience

## Pattern Summary

The party is received in a formal setting — a hall, a council chamber, a private meeting — by a powerful NPC who holds something they need. The host will assess them, probe for information, and make a decision. Every detail of presentation and behaviour is observed.

## Use When

- The party needs permission, passage, alliance, or information that only this NPC can grant.
- A faction wants to take the party's measure before acting.
- Protocol and social hierarchy matter in this setting.

## Do Not Use When

- The meeting is already informal and trust is established.
- The party has no leverage and no reason to be received.

## Scene Structure

1. The party is announced and shown into the formal setting.
2. The host opens with pleasantries that are actually assessments.
3. The host asks direct or indirect questions about the party's purpose.
4. Negotiation: the party states what they want; the host states conditions.
5. Resolution: agreement, partial agreement, refusal, or a deferred answer.

## Pressure Ladder

**Low:** Polite formality. The host is measuring the party but not hostile.
**Medium:** Direct questions about purpose, allegiance, or what the party carries.
**High:** A condition or demand the party did not anticipate. An implicit threat if refused.

## Useful NPC Bases

- Host: **Noble** (Persuasion +5, Deception +5, AC 15)
- Advisor/observer: **Spy** (Insight +4, Deception +6)
- Guard presence: **Veteran** (AC 17, HP 58, Multiattack)
- Spiritual advisor: **Priest** (Spellcasting, Insight)

## Common Checks

- **Persuasion DC 13** — make a convincing case for the party's request
- **Insight DC 14** — read the host's real reaction beneath the formal manner
- **History DC 12** — recall relevant protocol or the host's known allegiances
- **Deception DC 15** — conceal the party's true purpose or affiliation
- **Performance DC 12** — maintain appropriate decorum under pressure
- **Perception DC 13** — notice the observer in the corner, the scribe recording everything

## Fail-forward Consequences

- The audience ends without agreement, but the party learns the host's price.
- The host gives nothing but the party's visit is now noted by the faction.
- Partial success: the host grants a lesser version of what was asked, with a condition.

## Example Karsac Uses

- Meeting with a Lösweg council member who holds a writ the party needs.
- Audience with a Valweg harbour master who controls dock access.
- Formal reception by a minor jarl whose goodwill controls the road through their territory.
