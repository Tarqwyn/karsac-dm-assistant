# False Hospitality

## Pattern Summary

A host — a noble, an innkeeper, a respected local — welcomes the party with genuine apparent warmth. The welcome is staged. The conversation is targeted. The comfortable room is an opportunity to access the party's belongings. The real goal is information extraction through a disarmed, social environment.

## Use When

- A faction is sophisticated enough to use social manipulation rather than force.
- The party needs rest or resources and cannot afford to refuse hospitality.
- The DM wants to reveal that a trusted-seeming environment is hostile.

## Do Not Use When

- The host is genuinely friendly.
- The party would not accept hospitality from a stranger in this context.

## Scene Structure

1. The party is welcomed — food, warmth, a safe place to sleep.
2. The host is attentive and asks questions that seem like friendly interest.
3. Servants or assistants have access to the party's rooms.
4. The host probes toward specific topics: cargo, companions, destination, recent events.
5. Resolution: the party detects the agenda, plays along, or reveals something costly.

## Pressure Ladder

**Low:** Warm welcome, good food, pointed but plausible questions.
**Medium:** Items in the party's rooms have been moved. Questions narrow to a specific topic.
**High:** The party is separated under a social pretext. Each member is questioned independently.

## Useful NPC Bases

- Host: **Noble** (Persuasion +5, Deception +5, Insight +4)
- Servant/watcher: **Spy** (Stealth +6, Perception +4, Sleight of Hand +4)
- Theological framing variant: **Priest** (uses spiritual conversation to probe for knowledge)
- Local cutout: **Commoner** (genuine warmth, but passing information to an unseen principal)

## Common Checks

- **Insight DC 14** — the host's questions are too specific for idle curiosity
- **Perception DC 13** — the servant's movements suggest access to your belongings
- **Investigation DC 14** — your pack has been opened and carefully repacked
- **Deception DC 14** — maintain a cover story through a long evening of conversation
- **Persuasion DC 12** — redirect the conversation away from the topic being probed
- **History DC 13** — recognise the host's family or faction allegiance from their crest or manner

## Fail-forward Consequences

- The party slept well but their cargo inventory is now known.
- The party detected it and left — but the host knows they suspected.
- The party played along and fed false information — the faction will act on it.

## Example Karsac Uses

- A Valweg merchant house offers lodging that is actually a Mathr observation point.
- A hospitable village elder is passing information to the Truthspeaker network.
- A minor noble hosting the party is under pressure from a faction to report on them.
