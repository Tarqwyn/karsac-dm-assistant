# Witness Pressure

## Pattern Summary

Someone saw something. A faction wants that person silent, scared, or controlled. The party either needs to protect the witness or the party themselves are the witness under pressure. The encounter is about who controls information and at what cost.

## Use When

- A witness exists whose knowledge threatens or supports a faction's position.
- The party cares whether the truth comes out.
- A faction is applying quiet pressure rather than open violence.

## Do Not Use When

- The witness's knowledge does not matter to the current story.
- The scene should be an overt confrontation or chase rather than a social encounter.

## Scene Structure

1. The party learns or discovers that a witness is under pressure.
2. They investigate: what does the witness know, and who wants it suppressed?
3. Contact with the witness — are they willing to talk, already scared, or already bought?
4. The faction's agents may intervene — or may have already acted.
5. Resolution: witness is protected, silenced, moved, or lost.

## Pressure Ladder

**Low:** The witness has been visited and warned — not threatened, just advised to be careful.
**Medium:** The witness is visibly frightened and reluctant to speak even in private.
**High:** The witness has disappeared, been arrested on a pretext, or received an explicit threat.

## Useful NPC Bases

- Faction agent applying pressure: **Spy** (Intimidation +4, Deception +6, Persuasion +6)
- Physical escort or minder: **Guard** (AC 16, HP 11, prevent unsupervised contact)
- Muscle for an explicit threat: **Thug** (HP 32, Multiattack, Heavy Crossbow)
- Senior overseer of the suppression: **Veteran** (AC 17, HP 58, present if the situation is important enough)

## Common Checks

- **Persuasion DC 12** — convince the witness that speaking is safer than silence
- **Intimidation DC 14** — convince the faction's agent to back off
- **Insight DC 13** — assess whether the witness is telling the whole truth
- **Investigation DC 13** — find evidence of what the witness originally saw
- **Athletics DC 12** — extract the witness from a controlled location
- **Perception DC 13** — spot the faction's watcher outside the witness's location

## Fail-forward Consequences

- The witness gives partial information before going silent again.
- The party moves the witness but the faction knows they are gone.
- The witness talks but only to confirm what the faction already knew.

## Example Karsac Uses

- A dock worker at the south dock who saw Mathr's token on a housecarl is being pressured to recant.
- A scribe who copied an authority document with the Mathr sigil has been warned to forget it.
- The party themselves are witnesses to a council vote and a faction wants them out of Lösweg.
