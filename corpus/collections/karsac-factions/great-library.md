# The Great Library of Vushhir

**Canon File ID:** `factions/great-library`

> Archive, university, observatory, court of debate, magical college, and hospital of learning; the institutional voice of the Karsac Sourcebook's in-world sections.

## Overview

The Great Library is not a single building. It is a district, an institution, a legal authority, and a spiritual proposition — though Vushhir would object to the last phrase. Its oldest halls descend below the present city into chambers whose foundations predate the current civic calendar. Its newest observatories climb above the eastern terraces. Between these extremes lie the public reading courts, sealed stacks, translation houses, anatomical theatres, spell-vaults, debating chambers, botanical roofs, cartographic galleries, and the Hall of Unresolved Questions — where certain matters are deliberately kept open until better evidence presents itself.

## Description

### Purpose and Method

The Library's purpose is not merely to collect knowledge but to preserve the conditions under which knowledge may be tested, challenged, corrected, and extended. A text is not considered sacred because it is old. A claim is not considered true because it is beautiful. A theory is not considered false because it is inconvenient.

This discipline has produced wonders: physicians who map the body with the seriousness other cultures reserve for holy scripture; astronomers who predict eclipses with unsettling accuracy; mages who do not distinguish sharply between experiment and spellcraft. To understand magic is not to make it less wondrous — it is to honour the wonder properly.

### The Sourcebook Voice

The Great Library's faculty are the authors of the Karsac Sourcebook's Parts I–III — the in-world voice that constitutes the player-safe layer of the canonical record. Their perspective is the frame through which Karsac knowledge reaches most readers, with all the biases and limits that implies. The faculty know their limitations: they have failed to fully understand Lösweg, they approach Yngondi with warranted caution, and Duzgin records remain largely inaccessible. These admissions are made honestly.

### The Hall of Unresolved Questions

The Library's most honest section. Matters are placed here when evidence is insufficient to determine — not when questions are unanswerable, but when answering them now would produce premature closure. The Old People of the Isle of Mist: in the Hall. The nature of the Skaldic abilities: in the Hall. The matter of Yngondi: definitely in the Hall.
