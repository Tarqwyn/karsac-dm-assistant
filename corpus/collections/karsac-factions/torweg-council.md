# Törweg Council

**Canon File ID:** `factions/torweg-council`
**Retrieval Summary:** Advisory body under the Housecarl-Captain in Törweg; carries more weight than normal because Vane has been distracted; Brynja's seat of operational authority.

> Advisory body under the Housecarl-Captain; formally weak, but in Törweg specifically carrying unusual weight because Vane has been distracted by Mathr's orders for two years.

## Overview

The Törweg council sits beneath the Housecarl-Captain in the formal Lösweg hierarchy — advisory, not governing. In most Lösweg towns this means the council is effectively powerless in contested situations. In Törweg, over the last two years, the council has accumulated unusual weight because Aldric Vane has been occupied carrying out orders from Valweg that he has not questioned. Brynja Thorgrimsdotter has held the city in that gap.

## Description

### Composition and Seat

The council's formal seat is beneath Vane's authority. In practice, Brynja chairs it. She has been head of the Törweg council since her thirties — longer than Vane has been Housecarl-Captain. The council's credibility in Törweg is based on Brynja's two years of watching, recording, and holding the city together while the Housecarl-Captain moved cargo south without asking why.

### The Archives

The council archives — old trade ledgers, Lösweg oral histories — are Brynja's particular domain. She keeps them current. The genealogical records that reveal Mathr's family's lack of antecedent are here. See `places/torweg/council-archives`.

### Current State

After the Truthspeaker escorts Vane away at the south dock, the council formally holds the Törweg authority — there is no sitting Housecarl-Captain. Brynja describes what the Truthspeaker's arrival means: Vane will not be questioned at council, will be thanked, honoured, and quietly retired. The council is not powerful enough to challenge Mathr directly. It is, however, powerful enough to be a witness.

## Player Knowledge

Players encounter the council primarily through Brynja — she is the council's effective voice in the session. The archives are accessible via Thread D. The council's formal authority becomes relevant at the session's close when Vane is gone and Brynja holds the city.

## DM-Only

The council is Dhurvaq's quiet operation at the institutional scale. The fact that an advisory body with no formal authority has held Törweg's continuity through two years of Vishara's smoothing is not accidental — it is the Holding working through the specific persistence of one determined woman and the structures she has maintained around her.
