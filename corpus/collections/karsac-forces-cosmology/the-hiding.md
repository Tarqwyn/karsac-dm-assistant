# The Hiding

**Canon File ID:** `forces/the-hiding`
**Retrieval Summary:** Dhurvaq's structural arrangement separating Karsac from Exandrian awareness; not a wall — a sustained condition that makes the attention required to act on knowledge of Karsac consistently redirect elsewhere.

> Dhurvaq's structural arrangement separating Karsac from Exandrian awareness; not a wall — a sustained condition.

## Overview

The Hiding is the name given to Dhurvaq's structural separation of Karsac from the awareness of the wider Exandrian world. It is not a wall. It is not a conspiracy. It is a sustained condition — making the attention required to act on knowledge of Karsac consistently and subtly redirect elsewhere. Ships that might have sailed northwest found reasons to sail elsewhere. Scholars who found fragments of the old charts found themselves drawn to other questions. The knowledge persisted, buried in places too obscure for casual discovery, waiting for a moment Dhurvaq could not predict and has never been able to control.

## Description

### Origin

Dhurvaq's logic was structural, not moral. It assessed what contact between the gods' peoples and Maharuq's location would produce, and the assessment found: *not yet*. Not because the outcome would be evil — Dhurvaq does not think in those terms — but because the outcome would be collapse. Either Maharuq would use the new minds to accelerate its waking, or the new minds would destroy Maharuq and break the balance from the other side. Either resolution was catastrophic in the structural sense.

So Dhurvaq did what it has always done: it maintained a boundary. This time between Exandria's growing awareness of the world and the knowledge that Karsac existed.

Crucially, Dhurvaq did not erase the knowledge — direct action of that kind is not within its nature. It ensured that the attention required to act on the knowledge was subtly and consistently redirected.

### The Divine Gate Layer

At the Divergence — the Calamity and the establishment of the Divine Gate — Dhurvaq had a moment of restructuring. The gods were withdrawing. The world was being remade. In the noise of every boundary being redrawn, one more boundary, drawn quietly, was easy to miss. The Gate sealed the Betrayer Gods. Dhurvaq used the moment of its establishment to add a second layer — an extension of the separation between the two worlds that the gods did not notice and did not intend.

The gods did not intend the Hiding's second layer. They do not know it exists. Dhurvaq used the intention the gods already had and extended it, slightly, in a direction they were not attending to.

### Current State

The Hiding's mechanism persists but is under pressure. Maharuq's waking is producing cracks. Serris of the Cobalt Soul has come closer than any scholar to connecting the fragments — the blank spaces on Exandrian maps, the archived field reports, the fragments that don't connect. The Captains predate the Hiding and have maintained Exandrian connections through channels too old for its mechanisms to close. The boundary is showing its first cracks.

## Player Knowledge

Players arriving from Exandria via Halvash have already crossed the Hiding's boundary — they are the unaccountable variable, the people the Hiding failed to turn aside. They may learn:

- The blank spaces on Exandrian maps are not ignorance — they are a residue of the gods' encounter with Maharuq and subsequent withdrawal.
- Halvash is an anomalous point of contact; what makes it an exception is not fully understood.
- Serris has been working on this question for three years.

The Hiding as a named, Dhurvaq-authored structural condition is DM-layer context — it arrives via the Session 4 cosmological fragment about the gods' encounter and withdrawal.

## DM-Only

### Mechanism

The Hiding does not command or erase. It conditions: the supporting structure for knowledge of Karsac does not exist in Exandrian consciousness. A thought that arises and finds no supporting structure, no other thought to connect to, no tradition that has prepared the mind to receive it — such a thought does not propagate. Ioun's church has fragments. They do not connect. Serris is the exception because she has been building the supporting structure, piece by piece, for years.

### The Cracks

With the Waking advancing, the Hiding's pressure is less consistent. The Captains' pre-Hiding channels to Exandria represent a permanent structural gap. The players themselves — arriving via the Halvash contact point, carrying the scroll case, carrying the bone disc — represent something the Hiding could not redirect. They are the moment Dhurvaq could not predict and has never been able to control.

## Ambiguities

- Whether Halvash is an intentional exception in the Hiding (Dhurvaq permits it as a controlled point) or a gap Dhurvaq cannot close is not specified in sources. The canon preserves this as unknown.
- The gods could, in principle, choose to end the Hiding if they understood what they were choosing. Whether they would is a question the Sourcebook's author cannot answer.
