# The Divine Gate

**Canon File ID:** `forces/the-divine-gate`
**Retrieval Summary:** The barrier raised at the Divergence to seal the Betrayer Gods; its second, unintended function was the deeper sealing of Karsac from Exandrian awareness — Dhurvaq's addition, made in the noise of the Gate's construction.

> The barrier raised at the Divergence to seal the Betrayer Gods; its second, unintended function was the deeper sealing of Karsac from Exandrian awareness.

## Overview

The Divine Gate was established at the Divergence — the great event following the Calamity — to seal the Betrayer Gods from direct action in the mortal world. This is its primary, intended function, and it is common knowledge across Exandrian theological traditions.

Its second function is not common knowledge. At the moment of the Gate's construction, Dhurvaq used the noise of every boundary being redrawn to add a second layer of separation between Exandria and Karsac — more thorough than anything it had previously been able to achieve. The gods did not intend this second function. They do not know it exists.

## Description

### The Intended Function

Established at the Divergence, the Divine Gate seals the Betrayer Gods from direct manifestation in the mortal world. The prime deities withdrew behind it simultaneously — not forced out, but withdrawing because the Gate's terms required it of both sides. Divine direct action in the mortal world ended with its construction.

This much is known across Exandria. It is standard theological history.

### Dhurvaq's Addition

What is not known outside DM-layer sources: Dhurvaq used the moment of the Gate's establishment to extend the Hiding's second layer. In the noise of every boundary being redrawn — the Betrayer Gods sealed, the Prime Deities withdrawing, the fundamental structure of the world being remade — one more boundary, drawn quietly, was easy to miss.

The Gate's structural work and Dhurvaq's structural work were layered at the same moment. The gods were not attending to Karsac during the Divergence. The Betrayer Gods' threat provided cover. Dhurvaq used the intention the gods already had and extended it, slightly, in a direction they were not watching.

### The Gods and the Hiding

The gods' relationship to the Hiding is therefore complex: they do not know the Hiding's second layer exists. They could, in principle, choose to end it — if they understood what they were choosing. Whether they would is a question the Sourcebook's author cannot answer.

The prime deities' blank spaces on Exandrian maps are not ignorance: they are the residue of the gods' encounter with Maharuq before the Hiding was fully established. The gods withdrew from that encounter without speaking of it. The Hiding then ensured that the memory of what they found could not propagate into action.

## Player Knowledge

Players can know:

- The Divine Gate was established at the Divergence to seal the Betrayer Gods.
- The prime deities withdrew behind it simultaneously.
- It is a foundational fact of Exandrian theological history.
- Karsac's separation from Exandrian awareness is connected to the events of the Divergence — the blank spaces on maps are a residue of something.

Players do not know the Dhurvaq addition in player mode. That is DM-layer context arriving in Session 4.

## DM-Only

The Dhurvaq addition: the Gate's second function was not intended by the gods and is not known to them. Dhurvaq used the gate's construction moment as cover. This is the most structurally elegant fact in the cosmology's Hiding section — the gods built the cage that sealed the world's most dangerous secret inside its most dangerous location, and they did not know they were doing it, because Dhurvaq arranged for them not to notice.

## Ambiguities

- The Sourcebook notes the gods could in principle choose to end the Hiding if they understood what they were choosing. Whether this is mechanically possible within the Gate's structure is not specified.
