# Ragnarholm

**Canon File ID:** `forces/ragnarholm`

> "The twilight of the powers." The foretold final battle of Lösweg theology; its outcome is known and not feared.

## Overview

Ragnarholm is the name given in Löswegiann to the great final battle that ends the current cycle of existence. It is translated approximately as "the twilight of the powers" or "the doom of the gods." Its outcome is foretold and accepted — the gods will die, the world will end, and then the world will be reborn.

## Description

### The Battle

The foretold events of Ragnarholm: Vorath, the All-Father, will be swallowed by the great wolf Fenrath. The Serpent of the World-Deep will destroy and be destroyed by the thunder-god Thormal. The dead will return. The fires will come.

These events are not feared by the Löswegiann in the way that eschatological traditions elsewhere treat the end of things. They are accepted as a structural feature of existence — what the universe requires, and what the gods have chosen to face.

### The Rebirth

What outside traditions consistently overlook or mischaracterise when encountering Lösweg theology is what comes after: a small number of gods and mortals will survive Ragnarholm. The earth will rise again from the sea. Green things will grow from the ashes. A new cycle will begin.

The Löswegiann are not nihilists. The Vushhiri faculty notes this clearly. The outcome of Ragnarholm is not oblivion but transformation — the end of this cycle and the beginning of the next.

### The Theological Implication

What the Löswegiann honour in their gods is not omnipotence or eternal perfection. They honour gods who know they will die and choose to fight anyway. This distinction shapes the culture in ways that ramify through everything — their attitude to courage, sacrifice, loss, and the proper conduct of one's life.

One fights not because one will win, but because fighting worthily is what the moment requires.

This is why the Löswegiann relationship to violence, death, and the Honoured Dead operates the way it does. A warrior who expects to continue the fight after death approaches battle differently than one who expects only oblivion. The courage of Löswegiann fighters is not the suppression of the fear of death — it is the genuine reconceptualisation of what death means.

### The Honoured Dead

Those gathered to the Hall of the Fallen feast and train between death and Ragnarholm — waiting for the call to fight alongside the gods in the last defence of the world. The Skalds maintain the tradition that connects the living to this eschatological structure. The silencing of Skalds — a Vishara symptom — is not merely cultural loss. In the Löswegiann frame, it is a severing of the living community from its proper relationship to the dead and to what comes after. See `concepts/honoured-dead-hall-of-fallen`.

## Atmosphere & Themes

Ragnarholm is the cosmological frame that makes Lösweg courage legible. A culture that believes its gods will die and has decided to find that not nihilistically devastating but somehow clarifying — such a culture has a relationship to the mortal condition that is genuinely different from cultures built on the premise of divine omnipotence and eternal protection.

The campaign's Session 6 victory — Ragnfrid speaking with the dead and being answered clearly — is a Ragnarholm-adjacent moment. The proper functioning of the dead-and-living connection, restored after Vishara's banishment, is the tradition working as it should. The final battle has not come. But the preparation for it — the tradition, the speaking, the specific dead who are still themselves when called — is intact.
