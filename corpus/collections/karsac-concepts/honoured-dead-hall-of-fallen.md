# The Honoured Dead and the Hall of the Fallen

**Canon File ID:** `concepts/honoured-dead-hall-of-fallen`
**Retrieval Summary:** Those gathered to Vorath's Hall feast and train between death and Ragnarholm; waiting for the final battle; the Skalds' ability to speak with them is the living connection to the dead.

> Vorath's great mead hall at the apex of all realms; the honoured dead feast and train; waiting for Ragnarholm.

## Overview

In Lösweg theology, those who die worthily are gathered to Vorath's Hall of the Fallen — the great mead hall at the apex of all realms. They feast there. They train. They wait for the call to fight alongside the gods at Ragnarholm.

This is not a metaphor. In Lösweg theology, the specific dead are there: particular people, still themselves, still training, still waiting. The Skalds maintain the tradition that connects the living to them — and the ability to speak across that boundary is part of what the skaldic tradition preserves.

## The Threshold Vishara Threatens

Vishara's dissipation threatens the dead as much as the living. If the specific self is so thoroughly diluted in life that not enough of it remains to cross the boundary at death, the particular dead do not reach the Hall. They are caught. They are not themselves when called.

The Session 6 victory is stated in these terms: Ragnfrid speaks with the dead and they answer clearly. The specific dead is still themselves. The Hall is intact. The tradition functioned.
