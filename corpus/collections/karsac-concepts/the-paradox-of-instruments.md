# The Paradox of the Instruments

**Canon File ID:** `concepts/the-paradox-of-instruments`
**Retrieval Summary:** The Yantravaq use Dhurvaq's tools to serve Maharuq; Ezrah uses apparent Maharuq tools to serve Dhurvaq; a full waking of Maharuq destroys the Yantravaq along with everything else.

> The Yantravaq use Dhurvaq's tools to serve Maharuq. Ezrah uses apparent Maharuq tools to serve Dhurvaq. Full waking destroys the Yantravaq along with everything else.

## The Core Paradox

The Yantravaq serve Maharuq — the principle of dissolution of all distinction. They do this using the tools of Dhurvaq's principle: planning, strategy, hierarchy, the maintenance of elaborate structures across time, the cultivation of loyal instruments. They build cages in service of a force that abhors cages.

A fully woken Maharuq would dissolve the Yantravaq along with everything else — because Maharuq consuming the world is Maharuq ending (nothing left to dissolve). The Yantravaq are working toward a victory that unmakes both the victor and themselves. The oldest of them knows this. They continue anyway.

## The Mirror

Ezrah uses the apparent logic of Maharuq's direction — dissolving constraints, freeing the contained — to produce Dhurvaq's outcome. People freed from false cages become more distinctly themselves, more particular, more irreducibly present.

Neither the Yantravaq nor Ezrah is aware of how completely they embody the paradox of the other.

## Individual Positions

- **Vishara** — the storyarch suggests Vishara may simply not think at this scale; it moves as water moves.
- **Qathar** — has decided the paradox is not its problem.
- **Sukaveth** — almost certainly knows. Has continued anyway. The question the cosmology author cannot answer.
