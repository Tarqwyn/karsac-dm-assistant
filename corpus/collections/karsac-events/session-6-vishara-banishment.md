# Session 6 — The Banishment of Vishara

**Canon File ID:** `events/session-6-vishara-banishment`
**Retrieval Summary:** The Campaign 1 ending event; scroll case used to open the binding; Vishara is banished; the dead cross; Ragnfrid speaks with the dead and they answer clearly; the world reasserts its particularity.

> Campaign 1 ending; the scroll case opens the binding; Vishara is banished; the dead cross; the victory stated in the tradition's own terms.

## Overview

The Campaign 1 ending event. In Session 6, after the binding artefact is found (Session 5) and the connection between the scroll case and the binding is made, the case is used to open the binding. Vishara is banished — not killed. The Principle of Dissipation remains as entropy remains. But it is no longer here.

**The moment of banishment:**  
The crowded quality of the air releases. The dead cross. The world reasserts its particularity. A tree is a tree and you know it is alive because you can feel that it is alive. Ragnfrid speaks with the dead and they answer clearly for the first time in the campaign. The victory stated in the tradition's own terms.

## What They Carry Forward

After Session 6: the players know what Vishara was; they know Mathr was shaped, not possessed; they carry the question of who placed the scroll case in Salsvault; Korvann understands what his settlement was protecting; the Pryzi key is either recovered or in the Yantravaq's hands; Sukaveth's name exists at the edge of awareness.
