# Sygna

> Twenty-six; eleven years with Floki; translator; her sister in Eskvor has been silent for six weeks.

## Overview

Sygna has sailed with Floki for eleven years. She is Lösweg-born, from a fishing village three valleys north of Törweg. Her Common is rougher than Edvar's — she acquired it functionally, not scholastically. She reads Floki better than most people read their own family. Some things will be lost in translation. Some of those things will matter.

## Description

Younger than Edvar and harder to read. She stands at the gangplank and watches the company come up the boards, one by one, before the cargo reveal. She translates without being asked.

Her translations are honest. She also chooses, sometimes, which version of the truth to give. The storyarch notes that partway through the Whale-Road crossing, a player who makes a passive Insight check at DC 14 notices that Floki said something with weight and Sygna gave them something gentler. She is not lying. She is choosing.

Her sister is in Eskvor — a village outside Törweg — and has been silent for six weeks. She has not mentioned it to Floki. She will mention it to a player who treats her as a person rather than a function.

## Voice & Bearing

Younger than she sometimes sounds. Pauses mid-translation, searching for the word. Sometimes uses the right word and looks unsure she should have. She translates honestly but with editorial discretion — the same way a person who loves someone will soften the hardest edges of their words without falsifying them.

## Lines to Inhabit

- *"He says, sorry, he says: stay aboard until I tell you."*
- *"He says, he is asking if you want to see the chart. I think he is asking. The word is the same."*
- *(Quietly, between translations, to a player who has been kind:)* *"My sister is in Eskvor. I have not heard from her."*
- *(On her own Common:)* *"My father sailed with western captains for fifteen years. I learned. I am not very good. I am better than no one."*

## Motivations

**What she wants:** to do her job. To survive the cargo. To find her sister, or at least to know what happened in Eskvor.

**What she hides:** her fear about her sister. And the fact that she has started softening some of Floki's lines — not dishonestly, but protectively. She knows he is more frightened than he sounds and she does not want to make the company more frightened than is useful.
