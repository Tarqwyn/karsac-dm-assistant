# The Truthspeaker

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/the-truthspeaker`
**Retrieval Summary:** Mathr's Truthspeaker; carries the formal Lösweg council authority; the black Ashvein pin; a real Skald whose tradition has been thinned; arrives to remove the consequences from the south dock confrontation.

> Mathr's Truthspeaker; black Ashvein pin; a real Skald whose tradition has been thinned; Ragnfrid feels it before he can name it.

## Overview

The Truthspeaker is Mathr's formal council voice — in Lösweg, the Truthspeaker is a real council role, a Skald who speaks with the verbal authority of their Jarl when present. This one has held the role for Mathr for twenty years. He arrived in Törweg the day before the company, sent because Mathr's intelligence network was warned the Greyback was coming back with strangers.

He arrives at the south dock shortly after the confrontation with Vane resolves. Not running. Walking. He has the discipline of someone for whom hurrying would be unbecoming.

## Description

Forties. Dark coat. Small black pin at his throat — Ashvein, the rare glass-metal mined only beneath Halvash. The same material as the Shadow-Walkers' throwing spikes from the alley in Halvash. The same material as Xyrrathh's phial.

He is a real Skald. His formal cadence is precise — the old metres, the Skald register, the voice used in recorded halls. Ragnfrid hears it immediately. And hears, in the same moment, that something underneath the precision is wrong. The form is intact. The weight is not. A Skald whose tradition has been thinned does not feel this to themselves. Ragnfrid can feel it from outside.

## Voice & Bearing

Composed. Correct. Unhurried. Speaks Lösweg with precise formal cadence — the Skald register. When he switches to Common for the players, he slows; the Common is a courtesy. The Lösweg is who he is, or what remains of who he was.

He does not raise his voice. He does not threaten. He arrives, assesses, removes the problem, and leaves. Efficient. Without drama.

## Lines to Inhabit

- *(To Vane, in Lösweg:)* *"Aldric. You have done what was asked of you. You will come with me. The Jarl wishes to thank you personally."*
- *(To the players:)* *"This matter is council business now. Your involvement is concluded. I would not advise involving yourselves further."*
- *"You have served Lösweg today without knowing you were doing so. The Jarl is grateful."*
- *"Brynja is the council voice in Törweg. Speak to her about anything you require. Do not speak to anyone else about this. I will know if you do."*
- *(To Ragnfrid, in formal Lösweg cadence, one Skald to another:)* *"Brother. You have walked far. The road ahead is longer. We will speak when the road meets the city. Until then, care for your verses."*

## Motivations

**What he wants:** to remove Vane before Vane finishes asking the question forming on the south dock. To minimise the players' continuing involvement. To leave Törweg without anyone understanding what has just been done.

**What he hides:** he is Mathr's man. He believes he is acting on the council's authority. He does not experience his own thinning — the dissipation of the weight beneath his tradition's form has happened so gradually that the absence does not register. He uses the formal cadences correctly. He cannot feel that they no longer carry the weight they should.

## Connections

- Arrived in Törweg the day before the company — someone in Mathr's network warned him. Brynja knows he has been there. She does not acknowledge him publicly.
- The Ashvein pin is the equipment tell that connects him to the Shadow-Walker network and to Xyrrathh's phial. Players with relevant Investigation checks or knowledge of Ashvein make this connection.
- Ragnfrid is the player character most likely to register what is wrong. Brynja's hook for Ragnfrid says: *"your tradition will hear what he is doing before he can tell you."* The same applies here.

## DM-Only

The Truthspeaker has been Mathr's voice for twenty years. Twenty years of sustained proximity to Mathr's shaped beliefs and Vishara's operational methodology has thinned his tradition in the way any prolonged exposure thins: not by removing it, but by removing the weight from beneath it. He is the struggling-speaking Skald at an advanced stage. He cannot reach the dead clearly. He uses the verses. They land wrong.

The polite escort of Vane is the thing Brynja flinches at. She knows what it means. She says: *"He will not be questioned at council. He will be thanked, honoured, and quietly retired to an estate in the north and not seen again. That is how it is done."* She means: Vane will be killed quietly.

## Ambiguities

- Whether the Truthspeaker has any personal awareness of his thinning is not specified in sources. The DM-layer characterisation is that he does not — the form remains intact and the absence of weight does not register.
