# Aldric Vane

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/aldric-vane`
**Retrieval Summary:** Housecarl-Captain of Törweg, fifteen years; loyal Lösweg housecarl serving Jarl Mathr without question; not a villain — exactly what he appears to be; on the south dock, beginning to ask.

> Housecarl-Captain of Törweg, fifteen years; loyal; not a villain — exactly what he appears to be; the trail goes north, not through him.

## Overview

Aldric Vane is thirty-three. He has been Housecarl-Captain of Törweg for fifteen years. His father held the post before him for thirty. Three generations of Vane have served Mathr's family without question. Aldric has never needed to look closely at anything — loyalty was what the role required, and loyalty is what he gave. He has done what he was told. He has not asked why.

He is not a villain. He is a loyal man who has been used.

## Description

He looks like what he is: a good Lösweg housecarl, weathered by sea and cold, not aging unnaturally, not unblinking, not wrong in any way the players can pin down. The dissonance the players have been building toward all session is not a confrontation with a villain. It is a confrontation with a man who has been doing what he was asked to do.

On the south dock, he is at the gangplank with a notebook, making a mark every time a crate goes aboard. Methodical. Not rushed. He sees the players the moment they enter the dock approach. He does not stop.

## Voice & Bearing

Working-man's directness. Speaks Lösweg first; broken Common when he has to. The voice of a man who does not raise it because he has not needed to. He uses people's names once he learns them. He is polite in the precise Lösweg way that is not warmth but is not cold. He pays his round at the Saltbone when he drinks. He remembers names.

## Lines to Inhabit

- *"I was told you might come. My orders did not include you. Please tell me what you want here so I can decide what to do about it."*
- *"My lord Mathr instructed me. The cargo goes to Bruithwr. The artefacts are objects of cultural significance to Lösweg. They are to be properly held. I have done what I was told."*
- *(After hearing about the boy, stillness, the pen goes down:)* *"You are telling me they killed a boy."*
- *"My lord asked me to arrange quiet transit for three passengers. I arranged it. I did not meet them. I did not ask."*
- *(If the arithmetic is raised — his father's thirty years of service, his own fifteen, Mathr looks the same:)* *"My father served him for thirty years. Until his death. I have served fifteen."* (pause) *"That is forty-five years. He does not look forty-five years older than he did when my father—"* (stops)
- *(Captured or cornered, calm, beginning to wonder:)* *"My name is Aldric Vane. I am the Housecarl-Captain of Törweg. I serve my lord Jarl Mathr. The destination is Bruithwr. The recipient is Captain Kohlmar."*

## Motivations

**What he wants:** to discharge his duty. To get the cargo aboard. To leave the dock with the work done.

**What he hides:** nothing — he is the only major NPC in Törweg who has no layer. He is concealing nothing because he has nothing to conceal. He has done what he was asked and believed he was serving Lösweg.

**What is moving in him:** on the south dock, he is beginning to ask. The question he has not needed to ask for fifteen years is arriving uninvited. The Truthspeaker is on his way precisely to prevent the question from becoming an answer.

## Connections

- Serves `npcs/jarl-mathr` — three generations of Vane service; the service predates Aldric's personal memory.
- His housecarls wear `items/mathr-token` — not Vane's mark but Mathr's. The operation belongs to Mathr.
- `npcs/the-truthspeaker` arrives to escort him away with council authority before he finishes asking.
- Brynja knows what the polite escort means. She does not say so aloud.

## DM-Only

Vane is going to be silenced. He has begun to ask questions and Mathr cannot have a Housecarl-Captain who asks questions. Whether he is killed outright, memory-adjusted, or genuinely retired to an estate in the north is DM discretion. Brynja's read: the estate is real, the retirement is not voluntary, and he will not be seen again.

The most recent letter of authority — the one with Mathr's seal but not Mathr's handwriting — is the tell that Vane himself has not processed. If the players recover it from his body or from the warehouse, it establishes that someone else is now writing Mathr's orders. Vane has been carrying orders whose authorship he has not examined.
