# Floki

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/floki`
**Retrieval Summary:** Crew chief of the Greyback; carries his grandmother's forty-year-old prophecy; the seventh object is the pierced river-stone in his coat; he has told no one.

> Crew chief of the Greyback; has carried his grandmother's prophecy for forty years; the first half came true the night the Shadow-Walkers came to Halvash.

## Overview

Floki is not young and not old. He has sailed the Whale-Road his whole life. He had a grandmother who was a sea-reader — not a Skald, a sea-reader — who told him two things when he was twelve. He has carried both of them since. The night the Shadow-Walkers came to Halvash, the first half of the prophecy became true. He is still working out what to do with the second half.

He has the seventh object — the pierced river-stone — apart from the others in his coat. He has not told anyone.

## Description

He has the bearing of a man who has made decisions in weather and does not need to announce them. He speaks almost no Common. He translates through Sygna. Short declarations. The angle of his shoulders tells as much as his sentences.

When he is afraid, he goes still in a specific way — standing at the foredeck, not moving, not looking at anyone. He was still in that way when the Greyback came into Törweg harbour and he looked at the three figures on the wharf.

He has known Brynja for twenty years. When she met them at the dock, he did not embrace her. They did not need to.

## Voice & Bearing

Almost no Common. Translates through Sygna. Short declarations. The angle of his shoulders is the grammar of what he says. He uses fewer words than necessary because more words would require him to be less precise.

## Lines to Inhabit

- *(Through Sygna, about the boy:)* *"He says he is sorry about the boy. He says the boy was not his and the boy was not yours and the boy died because of a thing that does not yet have a name in this room. He says: he has decided he will not apologise. He will say what is true instead."*
- *(Through Sygna, the prophecy's first half:)* *"He says she told him: when they come from the wrong direction, the thing sleeping under the Yngondi Wastes will be close to waking. He says he has waited forty years for that to be a sentence he hears with his own ears. He says he heard it tonight."*
- *(Through Sygna, the second half withheld:)* *"He says she told him a second thing that day. He says he will not tell you what it was. Not because he does not trust you. He says you will hear it when it is the right time."*
- *(Through Sygna, after the south dock:)* *"He says: I have lived on the Whale-Road my whole life. He says: I did not know it had been crossed by people who would kill a boy."*
- *(Through Sygna, about the Truthspeaker:)* *"He says: the Truthspeaker is the Jarl's voice. He says: the Jarl is Mathr. He says: we should not be in Törweg when morning comes."*
- *(Through Sygna, about the cargo:)* *"He says: his crew do not sleep well."*

## Motivations

**What he wants:** to get the cargo to Törweg. To find out what happened to his captain. To understand whether the people he has brought aboard are the answer or the question.

**What he hides:** the seventh object — the pierced river-stone, set apart from the others, in his coat. He has not told Sygna. He has not told Brynja. He set it apart the moment the cargo was unwrapped. He does not explain why. The explanation is the second half of the prophecy, which he is not ready to give.

## DM-Only

The seventh object is the pierced river-stone. It is the binding artefact's most significant component — or one of them. Floki recognised it from the oldest layer of Karsac oral tradition and felt, the moment he held it, that it should travel separately. He does not know why in any articulable sense. His grandmother's second half tells him what is found at the end of the road. He has spent forty years convincing himself she was old and wrong. Tonight she is not wrong. He cannot afford to be wrong about the second half too.

The second half of the prophecy (for the DM only): *"And those who come from the wrong direction will stand at the place where the sleeping thing has been bound, and they will be asked to choose. And the choice will not be the one they came expecting."* — Suggested text only; adjust when the players reach Session 5 or the Mathr confrontation.

## Ambiguities

- The exact nature of Floki's grandmother's gift (sea-reader vs Skald tradition) is deliberately left vague in sources. She is described as a sea-reader, not a Skald, as a distinct category. What that category entails is not specified.
