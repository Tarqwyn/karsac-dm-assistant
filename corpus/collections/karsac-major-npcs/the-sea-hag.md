# The Sea Hag of the Whale-Road

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/the-sea-hag`
**Retrieval Summary:** The Hag of the Whale-Road; drawn by the cargo; three Drowned Thralls including one in Lösweg clothing Floki recognises; pre-initiative scene on the Whale-Road crossing.

> The Hag of the Whale-Road; drawn by the cargo; the Deepwhale intervenes; one thrall wears Lösweg clothing Floki's father knew.

## Overview

The Sea Hag encounter on the Whale-Road is the Chapter 2 crossing's set-piece combat — the threat drawn by the cargo the Greyback is carrying, the same thing the Deepwhale registered on the previous leg of the voyage. The Hag perceives what is beneath the ship and withdraws when the Deepwhale arrives. The encounter ends before it becomes existential.

This entity is not yet known to the players.

## Description

The encounter layers: fog first, arriving wrong (against the wind, the wrong colour, with a quality of depth rather than diffusion); then the figure in the water calling out from forty yards, voice carrying too clearly across open ocean in fog; then the drop — fog comes down to twenty feet in every direction, something in the water climbs.

Three Drowned Thralls, one of them wearing old Lösweg clothing in the patterns Floki's father wore. Floki was a child the last time he saw it. He does not explain this during combat. He fights. Afterward he stands at the rail looking at the water where the body went down. The crew make the flat-hand gesture — it means something different here than it did for the Deepwhale.

The hag withdraws when the Deepwhale rises beneath the ship. Not from defeat — from perception. She perceives what the cargo is, and what follows the cargo, and makes the choice to disengage.

## DM-Only

The hag and her thralls are Vishara's willing servants caught at the threshold — the Threshold-Caught, those whose specific particularity was dissipated enough in life that they could not make the passage at death. They bear the rune. The thrall in Lösweg clothing is from a vessel Floki's father knew — a specific, particular dead person, now generalised. Floki's face does something complicated when he recognises the clothing.

This encounter is the first time the players see Vishara's methodology at its most literal: people who served it in life, unable to cross, still at the task they were given.
