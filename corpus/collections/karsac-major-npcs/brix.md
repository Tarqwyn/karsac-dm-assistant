# Brix

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/brix`
**Retrieval Summary:** First Mate of the Coldfront; sailed twelve years with Tor Ashfen; working through a bottle at the Saltbone every evening; afraid he was complicit by inaction.

> First Mate of the Coldfront; twelve years with Tor Ashfen; working through a bottle every evening at the Saltbone; afraid he should have noticed earlier.

## Overview

Brix has been First Mate of the Coldfront for twelve years under Captain Tor Ashfen. Three weeks ago Ashfen came back from a voyage asking different questions, interested in different things — *"gentler than he had been before. That was what frightened me most."* Then Vane's men took him. Brix has been at the Saltbone every evening since.

## Description

Mid-forties. Short, deliberate, weathered. He needs patience, not pressure. After three drinks the precision slips and something underneath comes up.

He has been working through a bottle every evening not because he is grieving in any simple way but because the alternative is sitting still with the specific thought: that he was complicit by inaction. He noticed. He did not act. And then it was too late to act.

## Voice & Bearing

Quiet. Slow. Deliberate. He is a man who chooses words carefully and still sometimes can't find the right one. He will not speak to a stranger who arrives loudly. The hook for Korvann is explicitly patience, not pressure. Sit with him. Let the conversation arrive on his schedule.

## Lines to Inhabit

- *"He came back asking different questions. Interested in different things."*
- *"He was gentler than he had been before. That was what frightened me most."*
- *"They were polite when they took him. That should not have been allowed."*
- *(After time, trust established:)* *"The day they took him he had been at the warehouse on the south dock. Two of them came for him. One was Aldric's man. I knew the face. The other I had never seen. The other had a black pin at his throat. I had never seen the metal before."*

## Motivations

**What he wants:** to know what happened to Ashfen. To stop feeling that he should have noticed earlier and done something about it.

**What he hides:** the guilt of inaction. He saw that Ashfen was changed. He did nothing. He is not hiding this exactly — he is avoiding the direct statement of it. A player who sits with him long enough and treats him as a person will hear it underneath everything else he says.

## Connections

- The black pin at the unknown figure's throat — Ashvein, the same metal as the Truthspeaker's pin at the south dock. This is the Ashvein cross-reference that connects the Halvash alley spikes to the Lösweg inner council supply chain.
