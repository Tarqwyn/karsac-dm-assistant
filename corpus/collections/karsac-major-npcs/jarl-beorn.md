# Jarl Beorn

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/jarl-beorn`

## Overview

Jarl Beorn leads the Lösweg inner council at Valweg. He has a deep personal relationship with King Dugweb, built across decades of annual visits. He has trusted Mathr for sixty years and has no reason not to. He is, in Brynja's assessment: *"not corrupted. Deceived. If you can show him what you have, he will act."*

This entity is not yet known to the players — only as the name Brynja gives them as their route forward.

## Description

Beorn has led the council and managed Dugweb's relationship with Lösweg with what appears to be genuine competence and loyalty. The distinction that matters: he has been deceived by Mathr, not reached by Vishara. He is not shaped. His convictions are his own. This means he can be turned.

He is the happy path to Dugweb — the person who, if shown the evidence (House Mathr's structural impossibility, the artefact movements, the Shadow-Walker connection), will act. Acting means taking it to Dugweb. Taking it to Dugweb means Dugweb sees the country Mathr has kept him from seeing for sixty years.

## DM-Only

Beorn is the Session 3–4 objective framing: the road north, reaching Valweg before Dugweb's visit, making the case to Beorn. Whether Brynja's assessment is correct — that Beorn will act when shown the evidence — is the uncertainty that drives Session 3. The answer is yes, but the players don't know that yet.

The road to Valweg is not safe. Brynja says so. Floki says so. The Truthspeaker's arrival in Törweg is Mathr's first response to the company's presence. By the time they are on the road, Mathr will know they are coming.
