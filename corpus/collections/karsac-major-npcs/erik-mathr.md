# Erik Mathr

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/erik-mathr`
**Retrieval Summary:** Former companion; cleric of Ezrah; working inside Mathr's network from within; left the note that keeps Pell Duvash in place; is 'the Cleric' in the coded message; not yet revealed by name.

> Former companion; cleric of Ezrah; working inside Vishara's political cage from within; left the note keeping Pell Duvash in place; is "the Cleric" in the coded message.

## Overview

Erik is a former companion — referred to in the Sourcebook's personal correspondence fragments as someone who walked toward Vushhir five years ago and did not come back. He is a cleric of Ezrah. He has been working, for five years, with the patience of a man who understands that you cannot open a cage by pulling on the bars.

He is "the Cleric" in Erwing's coded message: *"Get word to the Cleric. I am discovered, trust no one, continue the work."* Serris does not yet know who the Cleric is. The players may piece it together.

This entity is not yet revealed by name in play.

## Description

He saw the cage Mathr has built in Lösweg immediately — the way a priest of Ezrah sees everything. He has been identifying who can be reached, keeping threads alive, creating conditions for the moment when someone arrives from outside the cage.

He adopted the name Mathr as a strategic identity — the house is constructed, not a blood lineage, and using the name gives him access he could not otherwise have. He is not Mathr's descendant in any meaningful sense. He is an operative inside an instrument, working against its purpose from within.

He left the note for Pell Duvash: *"Stay, and tell the truth to whoever comes."* He knew Duvash could still be reached — could still, given permission, disclose. The note was the permission.

## Motivations

**What he wants:** to create the conditions under which enough people name their fears that when the door opens, someone is on the other side who understands what they are walking into.

**What he hides:** his identity and location. His discovery — referenced in the coded message — means he has been found by Mathr's network. He has gone further to ground.

## DM-Only

### The Dhurvaq Echo

Erik does not know his god may be Dhurvaq's echo. He does not know the cosmological architecture. He simply does what his theology requires. The Sourcebook's author notes: *"the fact that a man who trained as a cleric is doing the right thing, in the right place, at the right time, for reasons he would recognise without needing to understand all of this"* is the most moving thing in the entire account.

Dhurvaq, if it has something like a feeling about anything, probably finds this almost pleasing.

### Location

Erik's location in Session 2 is not specified in available sources. He is further underground following his discovery. He surfaces in Sessions 3–4 or later, depending on what the players do with the threads he has left.
