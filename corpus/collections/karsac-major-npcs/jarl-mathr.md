# Jarl Mathr

**Canon File ID:** `npcs/jarl-mathr`

> Lösweg inner council; Vishara's primary political instrument; not possessed — shaped; he still thinks he acts from conviction.

## Overview

Jarl Mathr sits on the Lösweg inner council in Valweg. His family appeared sixty years ago with no antecedent in any Lösweg oral history. He does not look like a man of his presumed age. He has managed King Dugweb's annual visit for sixty years, maintaining a carefully constructed silence between Dugweb and the country Mathr has been working to smooth.

He is Vishara's primary political instrument in Lösweg. Not possessed — shaped. His beliefs have been bent over sixty years: duty hardened into severity, caution into fear of weakness, pragmatism into willingness to make extreme choices. He still thinks he acts from conviction. That is what makes him dangerous.

This entity is not yet known to the players as Vishara's instrument — only as the jarl whose name is on the token and whose family has no antecedent.

## Description

The arithmetic that Vane cannot bring himself to finish on the south dock: Mathr appears in records sixty years ago as a man in his forties. He does not appear to have aged. Vane's father served him for thirty years. Vane himself has served fifteen. Forty-five years of Vane service and Mathr looks the same.

No one in Lösweg has done this arithmetic. That absence is Vishara's work at the political scale.

## Motivations

**What he wants:** to maintain the constructed silence around Lösweg's political structure. To ensure the artefacts move south. To ensure Dugweb's next visit proceeds as all previous visits have proceeded.

**What he hides:** from himself — the nature of the force whose direction he serves. He believes he acts from conviction and duty. The conviction and duty are real. They have been shaped into the service of something he would not choose if he could see it clearly.

## The Two Paths — Session 4

**Path A (The Theoden Moment):** The players reach what is left of the real Mathr — the man beneath sixty years of bent belief. The confrontation is then with Vishara's senior Shadow-Walker asset, whose job is to stop that information leaving the room. Mathr can, if reached, point them toward the binding directly.

**Path B (The Residual Clue):** Mathr dies or cannot be reached. The Shadow-Walker departs believing the secret died with him — it hasn't anticipated the other clue, something the old Mathr could not quite extinguish. Either path delivers a thread to Session 5.

## DM-Only

Mathr knows where the binding is. This is the critical DM-layer fact. Everything else about him in the campaign — the Truthspeaker, the token, the artefact movements — is operational groundwork. The binding's location is the payload.

The mechanism of his apparent agelessness is not specified in available sources. The storyarch notes "Mathr does not look like a man of his presumed age" without explaining how. The canon preserves this as a known-unknown — the DM notes the arithmetic but does not explain it in play.
