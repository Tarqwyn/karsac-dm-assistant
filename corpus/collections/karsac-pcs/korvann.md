# Korvann

**Canon File ID:** `pcs/korvann`
**Retrieval Summary:** Air Ashari Ranger; has been following Vishara's operational signature for ten years without knowing what it was; his settlement was destroyed to prevent access to something; the bone disc is the original of what he has been chasing.

> Air Ashari Ranger; ten years hunting a rune he cannot identify; the bone disc is the original.

## Overview

Korvann is a high elf ranger raised among the Air Ashari of Zephrah. Not a druid or ritual guardian — he was the scout, the boundary-walker, the tracker. His unit was drawn out into the passes by a malevolent intelligence moving behind the dead. He read the trail well enough to persuade the others to follow. While they followed, the real attack fell on the settlement behind them. His sister Caeli was among the dead.

He left the Air Ashari soon after. He could not live with their mercy. He has been on guild work, bounties, and escort contracts for ten years — useful cover, and occasionally useful information. The rune keeps appearing. Dark, geometric, angular, with a central point and smaller strokes. Always corrupted. Always slightly different. As if many hands copied it from the same wrong source.

*Languages: Common, Elvish.*

## Background

The rune first appeared in the ruins of his settlement, left on bodies, broken timber, and scorched stone. Corrupted geometry — rough-cut, blackened, touched by necrotic magic, as if it were an imitation of something older and cleaner. He has spent ten years following its appearances across Exandria, across Wildemount, into the northern reaches.

His path crossed Rowan's several times through similar dangerous work. Not close companions — both loners, both shaped by loss, both on roads most people would rather not look at directly.

## Voice & Presence

The quiet elf who takes grim jobs, asks strange questions afterwards, and disappears before anyone can ask too much in return. He does not tell people about the rune. He records it. He compares. He is a tracker who has been tracking something he cannot name.

Brynja's hook for him goes directly to Thread A: *"The hunter. Saltbone Inn this evening. Ask for Brix. First mate of the Coldfront. He has been drinking alone. He knows what happened to his captain. He will not say it to a stranger who arrives loudly. Sit with him."* She reads him immediately.

## Player Knowledge

Korvann knows: his unit was drawn out; the attack fell on the settlement; Caeli died; the rune has appeared in corrupted form for ten years. He does not know: the rune's origin (the oldest Vishara symbol from the proto-Yngondi cultures), what the intelligence behind the dead was, or what the Air Ashari hold that the attack was designed to prevent access to.

The bone disc in the company's possession is the original of what he has been following for ten years. Discovering this — comparing the geometry — is the player-facing revelation. He may have already made that recognition, or be close to it.

## DM-Only

### The Air Ashari Thread

All three Instruments have an interest in what the Air Ashari hold, guard, carry, or are. The attack on Korvann's settlement was Shadow-Walker work, targeting the Air Ashari's outer boundary to create a gap. Whatever the Air Ashari hold is relevant to the Yngondi Wastes, which is where the Captains are moving artefacts.

The figure in old armour at the edge of sight — the malevolent intelligence his unit followed — was one of Vishara's willing servants, caught at the threshold, still at the task it was given. Bearing the rune. Still at work.

### Session 6 Revelation

When Vishara is banished in Session 6, the picture assembles for Korvann. He understands what his settlement was protecting and why it was targeted. He does not know what the Air Ashari hold — that is his thread into Campaign 2.

### The Ashvein Tell

At the south dock, a passive Investigation DC 12 lets Korvann recall the Truthspeaker's pin material in detail. On success: the pin is the same black metal as the Shadow-Walkers' throwing spikes from the Halvash alley. The cross-reference is player-accessible — it connects Mathr's network and the Shadow-Walkers to a shared supply chain.
