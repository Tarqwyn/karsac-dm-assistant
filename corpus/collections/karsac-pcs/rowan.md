# Rowan Thornevale

**Canon File ID:** `pcs/rowan`
**Retrieval Summary:** Human Fighter/Sorcerer; Noble Exile / Mercenary; carries a name he does not know he shares with a dead king; the Crow is a promise; magic erupted late and violently.

> Human Fighter/Sorcerer; youngest son of an old Tal'Dorei sorcerous house; the Crow watches; carries a name that exists in Karsac history books.

## Overview

Rowan Thornevale was born the youngest son of House Thornevale — an old Tal'Dorei sorcerous family whose ancestral lands lie near the Verdant Expanse borderlands. A family built around inherited arcane power, with a crest that bears a raven. Rowan's magic did not awaken with his siblings. It erupted at sixteen, traumatically, driven outward by rage and grief. His father chose the elder son and exile over the truth. Rowan has been on the road ever since.

## Background

His years wandering as a mercenary brought him north and east — pulled by something he couldn't name and didn't examine too closely. His path crossed Korvann's several times through similar dangerous work. They were not close friends then, but each recognised the other as someone shaped by loss and hard roads.

André, the stablemaster who trained him in secret, is not from this region and never said much about where he came from. The style he taught felt almost familiar — as if some part of Rowan already knew it. Alice, André's daughter, is the person most bound to Rowan's hope for a life beyond the manor. He has not gone back for her yet. He does not yet have the stability to.

## Voice & Presence

Rowan is the watcher — Brynja reads him that way immediately and hooks him to Pell Duvash. He observes before he acts. The magic that erupted violently at sixteen has not erupted that way again, which means he has not yet lost control again, which means he has been very careful about what he lets himself feel.

The Crow appears at thresholds. Stepping onto Karsac soil, it was there on a beam above the eastern cliff face in Törweg, watching him, and then was gone without the beat of wings you would expect. It does not tell him anything. It does not speak. It chooses a direction.

## Player Knowledge

Rowan knows: his family's sorcerous bloodline; his exile; André's training; Alice; the Crow's appearances. He does not know why the Crow appears, what it represents, or that his family name appears in Karsac history.

## DM-Only

### The Name in Karsac History Books

Rowan carries a name he does not know he shares with a dead king. The name Rowahn — or a recognisably close cognate — exists in the oldest stratum of Karsac political history. The storyarch note: *"Rowan Thornevale at the edge of a name he does not know he shares with a dead king."* A city called Zaksabaz. The connection between House Thornevale's sorcerous bloodline and whatever Rowahn the king was is not specified in available sources. The name in an old document is the player-facing tell — Thread D in the council archives is the first place it could appear. Session 4 portent register: *"Rowan: a name in an old document. A city called Zaksabaz."*

### The Crow

The Crow is a promise. It is not Thornevale family surveillance — Rowan's instinct to resent it as that is wrong. The Crow is something older, attached to the bloodline, possibly to the name itself. It chose the Council Archives in Törweg. It has been pointing toward discovery since before Rowan had language for what it was tracking.

### André

André is not from Tal'Dorei originally and never said where he was from. The combat style he taught felt familiar to Rowan because it is, in some sense, Rowan's style — the body memory of a lineage rather than a teacher's instruction. Alice's middle name is not from any tradition Rowan has placed. The two facts about André and Alice are seeds for later investigation.
