# Xyrrathh

**Canon File ID:** `pcs/xyrrathh`
**Retrieval Summary:** Dragonborn Sorcerer; Exile / Ruin-Seeker; carries the dark vessel; his blood is not purely draconic; the whispers keep promising answers; the Threshold-Bound Echo treated him differently.

> Dragonborn Sorcerer; Exile / Ruin-Seeker; carries the dark vessel; at the edge of whatever he carries.

## Overview

Xyrrathh was hatched on the Obsidian Coast, where he grew up mapping sea caves rather than fearing them, reading tides for patterns no one else named, and carrying a disposition toward knowledge that functions as armour. His people exiled him — not for the acid blast that saved the harbour during the pirate raid, but for what the acid implied about his blood. Something alien had entered the line. The elders had no name for what he was. That absence of a name was, to them, reason enough.

He left not as one fleeing exile but because the whispers had begun to sound less like madness and more like directions.

*Languages: Common, Draconic.*

## Background

He has since roamed forgotten ruins, cursed laboratories, drowned vaults, and chambers sealed for centuries, recording glyphs while the whispers rose around him into something almost coherent. One object stayed with him: the dark vessel — a small, dense, cold vessel made of something neither quite glass nor quite metal, volcanic in colour, containing acid suspending a black shifting substance that does not dissolve, does not settle, and sometimes becomes still near certain old marks.

He does not know whether the acid is a weapon, a prison, or preservation. He knows the vessel is evidence.

## Voice & Presence

Xyrrathh does not destroy knowledge. Watching the Salsvault phials and laboratory destroyed — necessary as it was — touched something primal. His companions chose safety over understanding, and he cannot be sure they will not do it again. He acknowledged people with gestures rather than words for some time afterward. Not punishment. Deliberate quiet, carrying that fear.

The whispers keep promising answers. Xyrrathh keeps demanding proof.

The Threshold-Bound Echo in the Maw treated him differently than the other PCs — it tested him more and withdrew less. Something in what he carries was recognised. He may or may not have said anything about this aloud.

## Player Knowledge

Xyrrathh knows: his blood is not purely draconic; the dark vessel and its contents; the whispers; the Salsvault experience. He does not know: the vessel is Ashvein (and what that implies about its origin), or the nature of the black substance.

## DM-Only

### The Dark Vessel — Ashvein

The vessel is made of Ashvein — the rare glass-metal mined only beneath Halvash, associated with pre-Calamity trade routes. Xyrrathh does not know this name or provenance. He will recognise the material when he sees the Truthspeaker's pin on the south dock — it is the same material — but may choose not to say so aloud. If he does make the connection, it raises the question: who made the vessel, from Halvash-sourced material, and why?

### The Black Substance — Two Interpretations (Both Open)

The black shifting substance in the vessel is somehow linked to Maharuq. Two interpretations are in play, neither yet confirmed:

**1. Remnant of a Living Weapon.** Something engineered or summoned during a pre-Calamity conflict — never fully spent, now reduced to a dense, shifting residue. The acid may not be what Xyrrathh is studying; it may be what is containing it. He thinks he carries evidence. He may be carrying a prisoner.

**2. An Entity Born of Conflict.** Something that accreted out of the violence and loss — not made, not summoned, but emerged. Like Ezrah is one kind of emergence from the cosmological situation, the substance may be another kind. The two need not have met. They are simply what Maharuq left behind.

These are not mutually exclusive. A living weapon sentient enough to survive as a kind of entity sits between both. The question remains open until the campaign is ready for it. For now: the substance responds to things it should not, and Xyrrathh does not fully understand why.

### Blood

His blood is not purely draconic. Something alien entered the line — a parasitic essence, in the elders' characterisation, feeding on fear, corrosion, and the places where certainty fails. The exact nature of that alien element is not specified in available sources. The dark vessel may be related. This is the deepest open thread in his background.

### The Echo

The Threshold-Bound Echo treated Xyrrathh differently at the Maw. The performance doc specifies: it tests him more, withdraws less. Whatever he carries was recognised by something that guards the oldest geometry in the Stormwatch foothills. This is not explained in Session 2. It is a plant.

## Ambiguities

- The alien element in Xyrrathh's blood is not specified — the background describes it as a "parasitic essence" in the elders' framing, which may be accurate or may be the elders' fear rather than fact.
- The substance's exact nature (Remnant / Entity / both) is deliberately left open.
