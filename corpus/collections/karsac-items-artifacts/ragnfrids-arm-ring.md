# Ragnfrid's Arm-Ring — The Oath-Ring of Veyr

**Canon File ID:** `items/ragnfrids-arm-ring`
**Retrieval Summary:** Old silver and whale-bone arm-ring; belonged to Floki's grandmother; given to Ragnfrid before Törweg; the Oath-Ring of Veyr; inscription: 'Hold what the sea returns. Name what the grave refuses.'

> Floki's grandmother's arm-ring; given before Törweg; old silver and whale-bone; inscription in old rune forms.

## Overview

An arm-ring of old silver, darkened almost black in the grooves, with whale-bone worked between the metal bands and small runes cut along the inner curve, worn smooth by age and hands. Not beautiful in the way treasure is — beautiful in the way old tools are beautiful, the way things become when they have outlived everyone who first understood them.

Floki gave it to Ragnfrid the morning Törweg appeared in the mist, before landing. It belonged to his grandmother. She wore it when she was old, kept it wrapped when she was older. The night before arriving, he dreamed of her. She told him he had carried it as far as he was meant to carry it. She said it must go to the one who knows when a story has stopped being a story.

It was colder than the air when Ragnfrid took it.

## The Inscription

The runes on the inner curve are older than common hall-runes. Older than the marks carved on drinking horns. But close enough to the deep forms of Lösweg verse that the meaning comes through:

*Hold what the sea returns.*  
*Name what the grave refuses.*  
*When the third silence breaks,*  
*let the witness speak.*

The words are clear. Their meaning is not. The third silence is not yet explained. Let the phrase sit in the campaign.

## Resonance with the Truthspeaker

Ragnfrid's tradition resonates wrongly with the Truthspeaker's tradition when they meet on the south dock. The arm-ring amplifies this tell — its sensitivity to the old oath-forms means Ragnfrid can hear the discrepancy between the Truthspeaker's correct formal cadence and the wrong weight beneath it.
