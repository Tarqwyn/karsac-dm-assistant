# The Twelve Artefacts

**Canon File ID:** `items/the-twelve-artefacts`
**Retrieval Summary:** Twelve objects from the sealed Stormwatch vault; components of the structure that anchors Vishara to material existence; seven were diverted to Halvash, five remain in and around Törweg.

> Components of the binding that anchors Vishara to material existence; twelve total; seven diverted to Halvash; five remain in Törweg.

## Overview

Three months before the Chapter 2 events, a sealed location in the Stormwatch Mountains above Törweg opened itself. Twelve objects were recovered from inside. They are the components of the structure that holds Vishara to material existence on this plane. That structure was moved out of Karsac centuries ago — so quietly that not even the Yantravaq noticed — and has since been separated and moved.

## Description

### The Seven on the Greyback

Seven objects are currently aboard the Greyback, diverted from their intended destination (Törweg) because the captain who was supposed to receive them went silent. They arrived in Halvash instead and have now come back with the company. Floki unwrapped six in the cabin:

- A dark stone the size of a child's head, perfectly cylindrical, warm to the touch.
- Two iron rings as wide as a man's wrist, joined by a chain of black metal that does not catch light correctly.
- A bronze disc, palm-sized, blank on both faces and yet not blank. Serris found the markings later, in firelight — they match the bone disc carving and continue past it.
- Three smaller things wrapped in oiled cloth that Floki does not open in this company.
- And the seventh: the pierced river-stone, which Floki set apart from the others and has not explained. See `items/the-pierced-river-stone`.

### The Five in Törweg

Five objects remain in and around Törweg. Vane has been moving the most significant of the five south to Bruithwr on Mathr's orders. One was hidden by Brynja behind her hearthstone. See `items/brynjas-hidden-artefact`.

### The Pattern

The bronze disc's markings match the bone disc and the scroll case geometry. The pattern is the binding. Each artefact is a face of the same structure. Together they form the map of what holds Vishara to material existence on this plane.

When the recovered artefacts came near the remaining Törweg objects and the scroll case at the south dock, something happened — a low hum at the edge of hearing; the scroll case opened once on its own; the warmth in Floki's coat increased briefly. The pattern recognises itself across the components.

## DM-Only

The twelve objects together constitute the binding mechanism. Their dispersal is what makes them trackable — their components are warm, they respond to each other, and the pattern on the bronze disc gives Serris enough to start connecting the geometry. This is the chain from Session 2 through Session 5: the artefacts lead the players toward the binding's location.

The fact that Vane has been moving them south on Mathr's orders without knowing what they are is the most politically significant operational fact: Mathr knows what the artefacts are and wants them removed from Lösweg. He does not want the binding accessible. This is either because he is genuinely loyal to Vishara's direction (keep the binding dispersed) or because the binding's location, if found, would point back to him.

## Ambiguities

- The specific identities of the three smaller objects Floki does not open are not described in sources.
- The total distribution of the twelve (how many with Kohlmar in Bruithwr, how many Vane still has, how many Brynja has secured) is not precisely specified beyond "seven on the Greyback, five in Törweg."
