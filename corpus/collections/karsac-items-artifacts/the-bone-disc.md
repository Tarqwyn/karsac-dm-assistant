# The Bone Disc

**Canon File ID:** `items/the-bone-disc`
**Retrieval Summary:** Vishara's oldest symbol; recovered from the Shadow-Walker killed in the Halvash alley; warm since Halvash; its pattern continues on the Maw walls and in the Carver's whittlings.

> Vishara's oldest symbol; recovered from the Shadow-Walker in the Halvash alley; warm since Halvash; the pattern continues on the Maw.

## Overview

The bone disc was recovered from the Shadow-Walker killed in the Halvash alley — pulled from the body after the fight. It has been warm since. Nobody has mentioned the warmth, though several people have noticed it.

When Floki first saw it, he said one word: "impossible." He did not explain.

## Description

A disc of bone — hand-sized, smooth, carved with a precise geometric pattern. The pattern is the oldest stratum of Vishara's symbol, from the encounter with the proto-Yngondi cultures, before Karsac was named. The Shadow-Walkers carry it because their tradition carries it. They do not know what it is at its deepest root.

The Maw above Törweg: its walls are scored with the same pattern, continued past the point where the disc's carving ends. The pattern continues the binding. The disc is one face. The scroll case is a third. There are more.

The Carver at the Cooperage reproduces a fragment of this geometry every afternoon, without knowing why.

## Player Knowledge

Players have been carrying this since Session 1. They know: it was taken from a Shadow-Walker; Floki called it impossible; it has been warm. In the Maw, they see the connection to the wall geometry. The pattern continues past the disc's edge.

They do not know: what the rune is at its origin; its connection to Vishara's oldest operations; what the full pattern means.

## DM-Only

### The Rune's Full Significance

The rune appears on the Threshold-Caught — Vishara's willing servants who could not cross at death. It appears on the Maw walls. It appears in corrupted copies wherever Vishara's operations have touched across Exandria. Korvann has been following a corrupted copy of this geometry for ten years. The original is in the party's pocket.

The bone disc is one face of the binding pattern. Its warmth is the binding pattern recognising the scroll case the players are also carrying. The two faces of the same object, carried by the same people, in proximity — the warmth is the pattern responding.

### The Shadow-Walker's Source

The bone disc's tradition traces to the proto-Yngondi cultures — the oldest stratum of Karsac's continental memory. The Shadow-Walkers carry it because their tradition carries it, across generations, without understanding its origin. This is how Vishara's oldest operational mark persists: not through intention but through tradition. The geometry outlasts the understanding.
