# Movement and Position

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/movement-and-position`

**Retrieval Summary:** On your turn, you can move up to your speed, split that movement around actions, and pay extra movement for terrain and special movement modes.

## Rule Summary

Combat movement is measured against speed. You can break up movement, switch movement modes, and pay extra movement for obstacles or awkward positions.

## Procedure

1. Start with your available speed for the turn.
2. Spend movement in any order around your action.
3. Pay extra movement for difficult terrain, crawling, climbing, swimming, squeezing, or standing from prone.
4. Stop if your speed becomes 0 or you run out of movement.
5. If you leave a hostile creature's reach, check for opportunity attacks.

## At the Table

Use this when adjudicating speed, prone movement, movement through creatures, flying falls, or movement split around attacks.

## Common Questions

### Can I move before and after my action?

Yes. You can split movement around your action and even between attacks from the same Attack action.

### Can I end my turn in another creature's space?

No. You can move through some creatures' spaces, but you cannot willingly end your move there.
