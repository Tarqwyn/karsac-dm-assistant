# Area of Effect

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/area-of-effect`

**Retrieval Summary:** Areas of effect originate from a point of origin and extend in straight lines that are blocked by total cover.

## Rule Summary

Spells can affect cones, cubes, cylinders, lines, or spheres. The point of origin and any blocked lines determine which spaces are actually included.

## Procedure

1. Identify the spell's area shape and size.
2. Place the point of origin according to the spell.
3. Trace the area outward from that point.
4. Exclude any location that does not have an unblocked straight line from the point of origin.
5. Apply the spell's saving throw, attack, or effect to creatures in the included area.

## At the Table

Use this when placing blast spells, checking corners and walls, or deciding whether cover blocks part of an area.

## Common Questions

### What blocks an area of effect?

Total cover blocks the imaginary straight lines from the point of origin.

### Does every spell use the same shape?

No. The spell description tells you whether it uses a cone, cube, cylinder, line, or sphere.
