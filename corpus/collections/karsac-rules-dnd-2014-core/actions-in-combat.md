# Actions in Combat

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/actions-in-combat`

**Retrieval Summary:** On your turn, you usually move and take one action chosen from the combat options, class features, or an improvised action.

## Rule Summary

Your action is your main combat choice on your turn. The SRD lists common actions, but class features and improvised actions can also use that slot.

## Procedure

1. Start your turn and decide whether to move before or after your action.
2. Choose one action available to you.
3. Resolve that action using its own rule.
4. If you want something not listed, describe it and the DM decides whether it is possible and what roll it needs.

## At the Table

Use this when a player asks what they can do with their action or proposes something improvised.

## Common Questions

### Is casting a spell always an action?

No. A spell uses whatever casting time its description gives.

### When do I need Use an Object?

Use it when an object's description says it takes an action, or when you need more object interaction than the free interaction on your turn.
