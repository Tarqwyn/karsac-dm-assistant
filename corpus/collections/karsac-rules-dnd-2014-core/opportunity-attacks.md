# Opportunity Attacks

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/opportunity-attacks`

**Retrieval Summary:** A creature can use its reaction to make one melee attack when a hostile creature it can see moves out of its reach.

## Rule Summary

Leaving an enemy's reach can trigger a reaction attack, but only when the movement uses the mover's own movement, action, or reaction.

## Procedure

1. Check whether a hostile creature that can see the mover is being left behind.
2. If the mover is leaving that creature's reach, the creature can spend its reaction.
3. The reaction makes one melee attack.
4. The attack happens right before the mover leaves reach.
5. Do not trigger this if the mover Disengaged, teleported, fell, or was moved without using its own movement, action, or reaction.

## At the Table

Use this when someone backs away, runs past, or is pushed to the edge of an enemy's reach.

## Common Questions

### Does forced movement trigger it?

No. If something else moves you without using your own movement, action, or reaction, you do not provoke.

### Does Disengage protect the whole turn?

Yes. If you take the Disengage action, your movement does not provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of that turn.
