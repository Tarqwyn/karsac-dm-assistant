# Spellcasting Basics

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/spellcasting-basics`

**Retrieval Summary:** Spellcasting uses spell slots, casting times, range, components, duration, and target rules that apply regardless of class.

## Rule Summary

Most spells spend a spell slot, follow a casting time, require components, and define their own range, duration, and targets.

## Procedure

1. Confirm the spell is known or prepared and that a slot or special casting method is available.
2. Check the casting time and use the required action, bonus action, reaction, or longer casting process.
3. Check range, targets, and whether there is a clear path.
4. Provide the required verbal, somatic, and material components.
5. Resolve the spell's attack roll, saving throw, or automatic effect.
6. Track duration, including concentration if required.

## At the Table

Use this for general spell questions: what a caster needs to cast, whether they can reach the target, how rituals work, and what a spell slot does.

## Common Questions

### What do higher-level slots do?

A spell cast with a higher-level slot counts as that higher level for the casting, and some spells gain stronger effects.

### How do bonus-action spells constrain the rest of the turn?

If you cast a spell as a bonus action, the only other spell you can cast that turn is a cantrip with a casting time of 1 action.
