# Advantage and Disadvantage

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/advantage-disadvantage`

**Retrieval Summary:** Advantage and disadvantage change a d20 roll by letting you roll twice and use the higher or lower result.

## Rule Summary

Advantage means roll two d20s and use the higher. Disadvantage means roll two d20s and use the lower.

## Procedure

1. Determine whether the roll has advantage, disadvantage, both, or neither.
2. If it has advantage, roll two d20s and keep the higher.
3. If it has disadvantage, roll two d20s and keep the lower.
4. If it has both, they cancel and you roll one d20.
5. Add normal modifiers after choosing the die result.

## At the Table

Use this for attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws when a rule or circumstance says so.

## Common Questions

### Do multiple sources stack?

No. Multiple sources of advantage still mean only one extra d20, and the same is true for disadvantage.

### What if a feature lets me reroll?

If you have advantage or disadvantage and can reroll the d20, you reroll only one of the dice.
