# Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Ready

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/dash-disengage-dodge-help-hide-ready`

**Retrieval Summary:** Dash adds movement, Disengage prevents opportunity attacks, Dodge defends, Help grants advantage, Hide starts a Stealth attempt, and Ready prepares a reaction.

## Rule Summary

These are common combat actions that trade offense for movement, defense, setup, or timing control.

## Procedure

1. Choose the action that matches the intended effect.
2. Apply its immediate effect:
3. `Dash`: gain extra movement equal to your speed for the turn.
4. `Disengage`: your movement does not provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn.
5. `Dodge`: attacks against you have disadvantage if you can see the attacker, and you have advantage on Dexterity saves until your next turn.
6. `Help`: give an ally advantage on the next relevant check, or on the first attack against a creature within 5 feet of you before your next turn.
7. `Hide`: make a Dexterity (Stealth) check to become hidden if the circumstances allow it.
8. `Ready`: name a perceivable trigger and the action or movement you will use with your reaction before your next turn.

## At the Table

Use this when a player wants to reposition safely, hold an action, support an ally, or disappear into cover instead of attacking.

## Common Questions

### What is the main restriction on Ready?

You spend your action now and your reaction later. A readied spell must have a casting time of 1 action and requires concentration while held.

### Does Hide guarantee invisibility?

No. Hide starts a Stealth attempt. The DM still decides whether the situation allows hiding and whether observers notice you.
