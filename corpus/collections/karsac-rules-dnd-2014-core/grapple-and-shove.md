# Grapple and Shove

**Rule ID:** `rules/core/grapple-and-shove`

**Retrieval Summary:** Grapple and shove are special melee attacks that replace one attack from the Attack action and use contested checks instead of attack rolls.

## Rule Summary

Grappling grabs a target and usually sets its speed to 0. Shoving pushes a target 5 feet or knocks it prone.

## Procedure

1. Use the Attack action and replace one attack with a grapple or shove.
2. The target must be within reach and no more than one size larger than the attacker.
3. Roll Strength (Athletics) for the attacker.
4. The target contests with Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics).
5. On a grapple success, the target gains the grappled condition.
6. On a shove success, the attacker pushes the target 5 feet or knocks it prone.

## At the Table

Use this for wrestling, pinning movement, knocking someone down, or moving a fight toward hazards or positioning.

## Common Questions

### How does a grapple end?

The grappler can release the target at any time, the grappler becoming incapacitated ends it, and effects that move the target out of reach also end it.

### Can I move a grappled creature?

Yes. You can drag or carry it, but your speed is halved unless it is two or more sizes smaller than you.
