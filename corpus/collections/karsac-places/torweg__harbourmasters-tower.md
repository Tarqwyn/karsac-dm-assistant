# Harbourmaster's Tower, Törweg

**Canon File ID:** `places/torweg/harbourmasters-tower`
**Retrieval Summary:** The harbourmaster's tower; the half-mast flag is visible from inbound vessels and nobody knows for whom.

> The harbourmaster's tower; the half-mast flag; nobody on the inbound vessel knows for whom.

## Overview

The harbourmaster's tower rises at the end of the main wharf. Its flag is at half-mast when the Greyback comes in. The flag is the first specific wrong thing the players can point to — not a feeling, not an impression, but a flag at the wrong position and no explanation for it.

## Description

The tower itself is not entered in Chapter 2 — it functions as an environmental detail rather than a scene location. Its significance is entirely in the flag: someone in Törweg is in mourning, or marking a death, and nobody on an inbound vessel can know who.

This is the Thinning of the Particular in its most distilled form: something that should have a specific name has been made general by the absence of information. The flag marks someone. The company arrives not knowing who, and nobody on the wharf offers.
