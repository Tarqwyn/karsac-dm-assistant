# Zörsdkog

**Canon File ID:** `places/zorsdkog`
**Retrieval Summary:** The disciplined northern realm; vassal-lord of Lösweg by mutual recognition of merit; seat of King Dugweb; Chapter 3 of the Sourcebook forthcoming.

> The disciplined northern realm; vassal-lord of Lösweg by mutual recognition of merit, not conquest.

## Overview

Zörsdkog is the political and military hinge of the central north — the realm whose laws, military orders, and sustained competence have made vassalage to it a relationship of admiration rather than fear. Lösweg is formally its vassal, but this vassalage was not made under duress. It was made under admiration. A people who honour their gods precisely because those gods chose to face an unwinnable war with dignity find in Dugweb something they understand without translation.

## Description

The Sourcebook's Chapter 3 is marked *forthcoming* with outline sections only. What is currently known from Sourcebook cross-references and the Lösweg chapter:

- Zörsdkog is the realm to which Lösweg owes vassalage — a relationship of ships, sailors, and fighting capacity exchanged for trade access and political stability.
- King Dugweb is Zörsdkog's hereditary head; figure of legendary personal authority; ancient; Kurogane-bearing; one of the five who stopped the Shade of Qadim al-Sharr.
- Zörsdkog's statecraft is not well described by conventional monarchy theory — Dugweb's personal authority shapes it in ways the available frameworks are inadequate to describe.
- Relations with Vushhir are respectful but cautious — Vushhir admires Zörsdkogi discipline and statecraft; it is less comfortable with heroic kingship and the political force of personal legend.
- Dugweb visits Lösweg once a year. Jarl Mathr has managed every visit for sixty years.
