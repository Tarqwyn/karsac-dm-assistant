# Council Archives, Törweg

**Canon File ID:** `places/torweg/council-archives`
**Retrieval Summary:** Old trade ledgers and Lösweg oral histories Brynja keeps for the council; site of the Mathr–Vane genealogical reveal.

> Old trade ledgers and Lösweg oral histories; site of the discovery that Mathr's family appeared fully formed sixty years ago with no antecedent.

## Overview

The council archives are where Brynja keeps the records she maintains for the Törweg council — trade ledgers, Lösweg oral histories, older documents. Thread D leads players here to find what Brynja has known for two years: that something is structurally wrong with the Mathr family's genealogy.

## Description

Two discoveries in the archive, for players who look:

**Mathr's family.** Appears in records sixty years ago, fully formed — already wealthy, already on the inner council. No antecedent in any Lösweg oral history before that date. This is not how Lösweg families work.

**Vane's family.** Properly antecedented — a Lösweg fishing family from a valley three days south. Elevated to housecarl service sixty years ago in a single instrument signed by Mathr's grandfather. Three generations. Hereditary. The Vanes are real. Mathr's family are not.

Serris, if present: *"Mathr's family has no antecedent. Vane's family has antecedent, but their elevation is contemporaneous with Mathr's family's appearance. The Vanes are real. Mathr's family are not. This is not how Lösweg families work."*

### The Arithmetic

Mathr appears in records sixty years ago as a man in his forties. He does not appear to have aged. Vane's father served Mathr for thirty years until his death; Aldric has served fifteen more. Forty-five years of Vane service and Mathr looks the same. Vane has never done this arithmetic. Players may.

## Player Knowledge

Players learn: Mathr's family appeared sixty years ago fully formed with no antecedent. The Vane family is real and traceable. The elevation was contemporaneous. Something is structurally wrong with Mathr.

## DM-Only

The arithmetic points toward Mathr being shaped over sixty years — not born into this position but constructed into it.
