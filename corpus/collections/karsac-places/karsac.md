# Karsac

**Canon File ID:** `places/karsac`
**Retrieval Summary:** The old continent in the northwest of Exandria, sealed from wider Exandrian awareness by the Hiding; the campaign setting.

> The old continent in the northwest of Exandria, sealed from wider Exandrian awareness by the Hiding; the canvas on which the campaign sits.

## Overview

Karsac is the continent occupying the extreme northwest of the Exandrian world. It is old beyond the comfort of tidy chronology. Its peoples have not preserved themselves for the convenience of later scholars — some keep records in stone, some in song, some in bloodline, some in oath, some in silences so deliberate that the silence itself must be treated as evidence. It is also, by virtue of the Hiding, essentially unknown to the wider Exandrian world. The blank spaces on Exandrian maps are not ignorance. They are the residue of the gods' encounter with what lies beneath Karsac, and their withdrawal.

## Description

### Geography and Peoples

Karsac is a continent of distinct regions, each with its own culture, temperament, and relationship to the wider world. Lösweg occupies the extreme northwest, sealed from the south by the Stormwatch Mountains. Vushhir lies near the heart of Karsac, where roads, rivers, trade winds, and argument all eventually converge. Zörsdkog, to whom Lösweg owes a vassalage of honour, anchors the northern-central reaches. Prizober lies in the applied-craft region to the south. Halvash, the anomalous port, sits on the Karsac coast as the one known point of contact with Exandria.

Beyond these are the Eastern Reaches — Barbytura, the long roads, and the Yngondi Wastes, which Vushhir records with warnings and sealed files.

### Why It Matters

Karsac is not the centre of the Exandrian world as Exandrians understand it. It is the place the gods looked at and turned away from — the place where Maharuq's pressure is most concentrated, where the Hiding has sealed the continent from Exandrian awareness for centuries, and where the Waking is now advancing. See `forces/maharuq`, `forces/the-hiding`, `forces/the-waking`.

The campaign unfolds in Karsac because the scroll case was found here, because the sealed location in the Stormwatch Mountains opened here, and because Vishara has been working here — in Lösweg specifically — for sixty years. Karsac is not a backdrop. It is the reason.

### The Name

The name Karsac resists easy scholarly treatment. The Sourcebook makes no attempt to derive it. It is simply the name — old, pre-scholarship, carried in oral traditions across the continent with the weight of a thing that named itself.

## Atmosphere & Themes

Karsac is the world meaning something. It is a continent of specificity — cultures that preserve the particular against the pressure of dissolution, peoples who keep memory in forms that resist the easy recording that would make it general. Lösweg is the most concentrated expression of this, which is why Vishara began there. But the principle is continental: Karsac is what the specific looks like when it has been left largely alone to become itself.

The Waking is the threat to all of that at once.

## Player Knowledge

Players know Karsac as their current location — the destination they sailed to from Halvash. They know it is off every map they have seen. They do not yet know why.

## DM-Only

The cosmological significance: Karsac exists because the gods' perception fixed Maharuq's location. The continent formed not because the gods willed it, but because the world learned from the gods that where things are most concentrated is a place. Karsac is, in its deepest structural sense, the place where Maharuq has a *here*. Everything else on the continent — its peoples, its cultures, its oral traditions, its silence on Exandrian maps — is the world organised around that fact.
