# Lösweg

**Canon File ID:** `places/losweg`

> The storm-born nation of the extreme northwest; sealed from southern Karsac by the Stormwatch Mountains; bound by oral tradition; the primary campaign region.

## Overview

Lösweg occupies the extreme northwest of Karsac — a land of savage beauty and unrelenting hardship that has, over the long centuries, forged its people into something that sits uncomfortably between the mortal and the mythic. To approach Lösweg from the south is to understand why its people are as they are: the Stormwatch Mountains rise like a wall of judgement across the approaches, their peaks lost in cloud, their passes narrow, treacherous, and in the deeper months entirely closed. Lösweg is not merely distant — it is sealed, as though the land itself wishes to be left alone.

## Description

### Geography

The great Bay of Whales dominates the northern coastline, its grey waters churning with currents that would swallow lesser sailors without thought. It is from these waters that Lösweg derives its lifeblood: fish, trade, and the profound spiritual relationship with the sea that underpins virtually every aspect of Lösweg culture. The bay is not merely an economic resource — it is, in the most literal theological sense, the body of one of their gods.

The seat of government is Valweg, nestled deep within the fjords of the Bay of Whales. Törweg, the second city, occupies a more exposed position further along the fjords — harder, colder, home to those Löswegiann who regard even Valweg as somewhat soft. Kroshonok lies at the uttermost north, where the sea freezes and weeks pass without meaningful daylight.

### The Löswegiann People

The Löswegiann are not a people given to easy generality. Their histories are guarded in oral tradition, ritual formula, lineage recitation, seasonal performance, and specialised forms of memory that many Vushhiri scholars are poorly trained to receive. A Löswegiann skald does not "tell a story." They preserve law, theology, ancestry, warning, grief, and prophecy in forms whose structure matters as much as their content. To translate such material into prose is already to wound it.

The name itself resists translation. Most plausible derivations yield *the land that endures* or *the place of waiting*. A minority scholarly view — Elder Maren of the Vushhiri faculty — holds it descends from an older root meaning *the wound that does not close*. All three are recorded without adjudication.

### Why Lösweg is First

Vishara chose Lösweg first because Lösweg is the culture that most completely embodies what Vishara opposes. The specific name. The exact verse. The irreducible individual who goes to the Hall of the Fallen and waits and is still themselves when called. The tradition that says: to name something correctly is to participate in its existence. See `forces/vishara` and `concepts/the-thinning-of-the-particular`.

### The Skalds

Lösweg's warrior-mystics occupy a role with no clean equivalent elsewhere in Karsac. They are warriors, keepers of memory, and practitioners of capacities the Vushhiri faculty has not been able to classify — including, by credible testimony, speaking with the dead. Their silencing — communities where more than one skald has gone quiet — is a Vishara symptom and one of the clearest early warnings that something is wrong. See `factions/skalds` and `concepts/skald-tradition`.

### Theology

The cosmological tradition centres on Veldrasil — the World-Tree, not metaphor but literal structural fact. The gods will die at Ragnarholm. This is accepted, not feared. What other traditions miss: the world will be reborn afterward. The Löswegiann honour gods who know they will die and choose to fight anyway. See `forces/veldrasil` and `forces/ragnarholm`.

## Atmosphere & Themes

Lösweg is what Vishara is working against — and what the campaign is ultimately defending. The specific name. The exact verse. The irreducible individual. The tradition that refuses to let the dead become general. The fighters who know they will lose and fight anyway because fighting worthily is what the moment requires.

The Waking threatens all of it at once. The question *what is left when the specific is gone?* is, first and most urgently, a question about Lösweg.
