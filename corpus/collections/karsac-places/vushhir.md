# Vushhir

> The civic-scholarly heart of Karsac; seat of the Great Library; a civilisation that chose wonder over certainty.

## Overview

Vushhir lies near the heart of Karsac, where roads, rivers, trade winds, and argument all eventually find their way. It is not the oldest civilisation on the continent, though its scholars would caution that *oldest* is often used too confidently by those with insufficient records. Nor is it the largest, richest, or most militarily feared. What Vushhir possesses instead is something more difficult to measure: continuity. Where other kingdoms rose around bloodlines, gods, fortresses, or conquest, Vushhir rose around inquiry.

## Description

### The Great Library

At the centre of Vugasir, the capital, stands the Great Library — though to call it merely a library is to diminish it. It is archive, university, observatory, court of debate, magical college, diplomatic forum, museum, hospital of learning, and occasional prison for ideas too dangerous to destroy and too valuable to release. Its purpose is not to collect knowledge but to preserve the conditions under which knowledge may be tested, challenged, corrected, and extended. A text is not sacred because it is old. A claim is not true because it is beautiful.

### The Vushhiri Character

The central assumption of Vushhiri civilisation: the world is full of wondrous variety. This is not a slogan — it is a civic instinct. The Vushhiri expect the world to contain contradiction, beauty, terror, intelligence, absurdity, pattern, accident, and things not yet named. They are therefore less disturbed than many peoples when confronted with the unfamiliar. Whatever is strange is met first not with belief or disbelief, but with questions: *what did you observe? Who else saw it? Can it be repeated? What did it cost?*

Vushhir has no state gods. Its public institutions do not deny the existence of divine or divine-seeming beings — such denial would be, by Vushhiri standards, intellectually premature. But it does not accept that power proves divinity, nor that mystery requires worship.

### The Great Cities

**Vugasir** — the capital; seat of the Great Library; city of domes, courts, and botanical roofs. Cosmopolitan to an exhausting degree. Prizes refinement, precision, and disciplined argument; occasionally mistakes polish for wisdom.

**Orkath** — the port that chose Vushhir; salt, trade, rope, law, tide, and argument. Louder than Vugasir, quicker to laugh, less patient with theory that cannot survive weather.

**Uglarsis** — the great crossroads; where roads from Hastovarn, Prizober, Barbytura, and the dangerous southeastern route all converge. More immediate than Vugasir; knows how to be a sluice-gate, not a dam.

**Dulvashir** — the old capital, in the Vush Mountains; quieter, more symbolic; conservative in the proper sense — distrusts haste, not variety. Even rebellion is expected to cite precedent.

**Iambarsir** — the eastern frontier city; faces toward Barbytura and the long roads beyond; first to hear what is coming from the east.

### Vushhir and Karsac

Vushhir is the voice of the Sourcebook's Parts I–III — its in-world scholarly perspective is the frame through which much Karsac knowledge reaches readers. This means Vushhir's biases and limits are built into the available record. The faculty knows it has failed to fully understand Lösweg, that it approaches Yngondi with warranted caution, and that Duzgin records remain largely inaccessible. These admissions are made honestly in the Sourcebook's preface, which is itself authored in the Vushhiri scholarly voice.
