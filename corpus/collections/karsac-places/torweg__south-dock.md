# South Dock, Törweg

**Canon File ID:** `places/torweg/south-dock`
**Retrieval Summary:** The south dock where Aldric Vane's housecarls collect cargo; site of the session's central confrontation and the Truthspeaker's arrival.

> The south dock; Vane's cargo operation; the Mathr token; the Truthspeaker's arrival with council authority.

## Overview

The south dock is where Aldric Vane supervises the loading of artefacts onto a Bruithwr coastal vessel. Scene 5 — the session's central confrontation — takes place here. The mic-drop is not the confrontation with Vane. The mic-drop is the token at the housecarls' collars, bearing not Vane's mark but Jarl Mathr's.

## Description

Quieter than a working dock should be at this hour. The fishing crews have been told to take their nets elsewhere. The dockworkers handling Vane's cargo are not Törweg locals. They wear similar coats and do not speak.

Six housecarls visible: two at the approaches, four on the dock itself. Each wears a small bronze token at the collar — flat, palm-sized, marked with a sigil that does not match the Vane house mark on the warehouse door across the dock. The sigil is Mathr's.

Vane at the head of the gangplank: working clothes, holding a notebook, making a mark every time a crate goes aboard. Methodical. Not rushed. He sees the players the moment they enter the dock approach. He does not stop.

The coastal vessel: small, Bruithwr lines, narrower and sleeker than Lösweg work, made for speed in coastal waters. The flag at the stern is not Lösweg.

### The Confrontation

Vane will speak if approached without immediate violence. He gives Mathr's name without ceremony — it is his lord's name and he is not in the habit of hiding it. When the players raise the Shadow-Walkers, the boy killed in Halvash:

*"My lord asked me to arrange quiet transit for three passengers. I arranged it. I did not meet them. I did not ask. I did what my lord asked."* (Beat.) *"You are telling me they killed a boy."*

He sets the pen down on the notebook. He looks at the vessel. He looks back. Something is moving in him that he has not given language to yet.

### The Mic-Drop

The token at the housecarls' collars is not Vane's house mark. Players who look across the dock at the warehouse door see Vane's house mark there. The token on the housecarls is different. Serris, on a passive Investigation, names it: Mathr's mark. The operation belongs to Mathr. The trail goes north.

## Player Knowledge

Players reach this location in Scene 5 after navigating the threads and the clock. What they find here determines whether they understand who the operation actually belongs to before the Truthspeaker arrives to remove the consequences.

## DM-Only

Vane is on this dock beginning to ask why. He has served Mathr faithfully for fifteen years. He has never needed to look closely at anything. The players have just given him reason to look. The Truthspeaker arrives precisely to prevent the question from becoming a conversation. He has been on his way since Mathr's intelligence confirmed the players were in Törweg.

If the players get five quiet minutes with Vane before the Truthspeaker arrives, Vane will begin to ask aloud. This is Path A to Mathr (the Theoden moment's precursor). Hold it open.
