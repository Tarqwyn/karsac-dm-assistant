# The Cooperage, Törweg

**Canon File ID:** `places/torweg/the-cooperage`
**Retrieval Summary:** The Spiral Carver works here; he carves the same spiral every afternoon and does not know why; his brother went into the Maw and came out wrong.

> Where the Spiral Carver works every afternoon; he carves the same pattern and does not know why.

## Overview

The Cooperage is a working barrel-making shop in Törweg. It is also where the Carver — whose name is not given — spends every afternoon carving the same spiral into scraps of wood, over and over, without being able to say why. His brother Hjalmar went into the Maw three months ago and came out wrong.

## Description

*"We went up. Two of us. Hjalmar went in. I waited at the slot. He came out—he came out wrong. He looked the same. He walked the same. He did not—he did not—"*

He forgets the sentence and begins again on the spiral.

The spiral he carves is structurally identical to a fragment of the bone disc pattern. Players who have examined the bone disc or who have Serris with them recognise this. The geometry continues the bone disc past the point where the disc's carving ends.

See `items/the-spiral-carvers-whittlings` and `npcs/the-carver`.

## Player Knowledge

Players can see: the Carver carves the same spiral every afternoon. He cannot say why. His brother went into the Maw and came out changed. The pattern he carves matches the bone disc geometry.

## DM-Only

Hjalmar went into the Maw fracture and became the Threshold-Bound Echo — or encountered it, or was absorbed into its function in some way the sources do not fully specify. The Carver's compulsive spiral-carving is Vishara's work at the individual scale — specifically, the Threshold-Caught adjacent effect, where someone in proximity to deep Vishara influence picks up compulsive repetition without understanding it. The Carver has not been reached the way Vishara's willing servants are reached. He has simply been eroded to the point where the pattern leaks through.

## Ambiguities

- The precise mechanism by which Hjalmar became the Threshold-Bound Echo is not specified in available sources.
