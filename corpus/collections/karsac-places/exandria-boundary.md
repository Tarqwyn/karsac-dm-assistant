# The Exandrian Boundary

**Canon File ID:** `places/exandria-boundary`
**Retrieval Summary:** The metaphysical separation between Karsac and Exandrian awareness; the residue of the gods' withdrawal arranged by Dhurvaq; not a wall but a sustained condition.

> The metaphysical separation between Karsac and Exandrian awareness; not a wall — a sustained condition that makes knowledge of Karsac fail to find purchase in Exandrian consciousness.

## Overview

The Exandrian Boundary is not a geographical feature. It has no cliff, no gate, no marker. It is the condition by which Karsac is absent from Exandrian maps, Exandrian consciousness, and Exandrian intention — the residue of the gods' encounter with Maharuq and their withdrawal, formalised by Dhurvaq into a sustained structural arrangement.

## Description

Karsac is reachable. Ships can sail there. Halvash exists as a known contact point. The boundary does not prevent arrival; it prevents the *attention* required to purposefully seek arrival. A thought about Karsac that arises in Exandrian consciousness finds no supporting structure — no other thoughts, no traditions that have prepared the mind to receive it, no network of knowledge in which it can propagate.

The blank spaces on Exandrian maps are not cartographic ignorance. They are the residue of the gods' encounter with Maharuq — the moment when the gods extended their perception toward the northwest, found what was there, and withdrew. Dhurvaq arranged the withdrawal. The gods felt it as their own decision. The blank spaces are what that feels like at the cartographic level.

Halvash represents the anomalous exception: a known controlled point of contact. Whether this exception is intentional in the Hiding's structure or a gap Dhurvaq cannot close is not specified in available sources.

## Player Knowledge

Players who have come from Exandria already know the boundary in practice — they could not find Karsac on any Exandrian map and had to reach Halvash via specific knowledge (Serris, the Cobalt Soul archive, the chain of introductions that led them here). They do not yet know why this is the case.

## DM-Only

The full Hiding mechanism — Dhurvaq's structural arrangement, the Divine Gate layer, the gods' unknowing participation — is DM-layer context. See `forces/the-hiding` for full detail.

The boundary is showing cracks under the Waking's pressure. Serris has come closer than any Cobalt Soul scholar to connecting the fragments. The players themselves are the boundary's failure — they crossed, arrived, and are now inside. Dhurvaq cannot account for them.
