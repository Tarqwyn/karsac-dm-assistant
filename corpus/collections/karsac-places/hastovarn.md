> Northern culture grouped with Lösweg in Vushhiri scholarly difficulty; memory held locally and practically, with deep suspicion of outside simplification.

## Overview

Hastovarn is mentioned in the Sourcebook alongside Lösweg as presenting similar challenges of scholarly access, though of a different kind. Where Löswegiann history is guarded in oral tradition and skaldic form, Hastovarn holds memory locally, practically, and with deep suspicion of outside simplification.

## Description

The Sourcebook's Preface characterises Hastovarn briefly: names may shift according to season, valley, oath, or feud. A hill recorded by one traveller as a boundary may be described by another as a witness, by a third as a grave, and by a fourth as a thing best not named aloud after dusk. All four may be correct.

This is the full extent of the current sourcebook treatment. Hastovarn appears only in the Preface and is not given its own chapter. No geographic, political, or cultural detail beyond the above is available in the current corpus.
