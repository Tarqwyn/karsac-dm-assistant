# The Maw

**Canon File ID:** `places/the-maw`
**Retrieval Summary:** The site of stillness in the Stormwatch foothills above Törweg; the fracture that opened three months ago; the geometry continues the bone disc; the Threshold-Bound Echo guards the interior.

> The site of stillness in the foothills above Törweg; the fracture that opened itself three months ago; something was taken; the taking left the pattern unfinished.

## Overview

The Maw is the fracture in the Stormwatch foothills above Törweg that opened itself three months before the Chapter 2 events. Twelve objects were recovered from inside. Vane's housecarls missed the Aeorian note. The Threshold-Bound Echo guards what remains. The geometry of the walls continues the bone disc pattern the players have been carrying since the end of Session 1.

## Description

The shaft tilts down into the rock approximately thirty feet. The walls are scored with the same pattern as the bone disc — deeper, and in places fresh. An iron sconce has been driven into the rock at chest height; the metal is new, the fittings not Lösweg work. Vane's housecarls' recent presence is visible in the equipment left behind.

### The Aeorian Note

Inside the fracture, missed by Vane's people: an Aeorian-period note on a fragment of worked stone. The same hand that wrote the scroll case margins. After two thousand years.

*"It is not a weapon. It is a condition. Available to be woken or to be held."*

Serris, reading it, goes very quiet.

### The Geometry

The spiral pattern on the Maw's walls is structurally identical to the bone disc pattern — and continues past the point where the disc's carving ends. The pattern is the binding. The disc is one face. The bone disc is another. The scroll case is a third. There are more.

The Carver's whittlings, produced every afternoon at the Cooperage without his understanding why, replicate a fragment of this same geometry. See `items/the-spiral-carvers-whittlings` and `npcs/the-carver`.

### What Was Taken

Something was removed from the Maw before Vane's people arrived — or perhaps by them, without knowing what they were taking. The removal left the pattern unfinished. This is visible to anyone examining the geometry carefully: the pattern has a gap. Something was here and is now gone.

## Connections

- `forces/sukaveth` — the Maw is possibly Sukaveth-adjacent, per the Sourcebook's Appendix C. The stillness at its centre is consistent with Sukaveth's gravitational presence. **This link is explicitly marked suggestive only in the Sourcebook.**
- `items/the-bone-disc` — the geometry continues the bone disc and resolves the disc's incomplete pattern.
- `npcs/threshold-bound-echo` — guards the interior fracture; tester not finisher; does not pursue beyond the Maw.
- `events/stormwatch-vault-opening` — the event that opened the Maw; began the Waking's current phase.

## Atmosphere & Themes

The Maw is where the cosmological substrate shows through. The warmth carried by the bone disc, the geometry that the Carver cannot stop drawing, the Aeorian note connecting to the scroll case's margin — all of these resolve here. Players should feel, in the Maw, that the world has a deeper structure than the Törweg wharf suggested, and that they have been touching that structure since Session 1 without knowing what it was.

The stillness is not peaceful. It is the stillness of something very old that has been patient for a very long time.

## Player Knowledge

Players reach the Maw via Thread B. They find: the Aeorian note (its connection to the scroll case is Serris's recognition); the bone disc geometry continuing on the walls; evidence that something was taken; the Threshold-Bound Echo in the depths.

They do not receive: the Sukaveth connection; the full significance of what the pattern means; what specifically was removed.

## DM-Only

The Maw is the closest surface expression of Maharuq's concentrated presence in the Stormwatch range. The Sukaveth connection is the Sourcebook's most cautious cross-reference — the link is suggestive only. Do not confirm it in play until a future source document specifies it.

The Waking's Part IV §VII fragment is suggested for discovery here — players encountering the Maw find the text that explains the Waking is not caused, it is occurring. This is the player-facing cosmological revelation of Thread B.

### The Threshold-Bound Echo and Hjalmar

Hjalmar — the Carver's brother — went into the Maw and came out wrong. The current state of Hjalmar's relationship to the Threshold-Bound Echo is not fully specified in sources. The Echo tests, does not finish, and does not pursue. Hjalmar became something adjacent to the Echo's function — or became the Echo. The exact mechanism is not canon-confirmed. See `npcs/threshold-bound-echo` and flagged ambiguity in `places/torweg/the-cooperage`.
