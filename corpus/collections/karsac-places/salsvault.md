# Salsvault

**Canon File ID:** `places/salsvault`
**Retrieval Summary:** Aeorian pre-Calamity weapons facility under the ice of Eiselcross; where the scroll case was hidden as ordinary stock; where the campaign began.

> Aeorian pre-Calamity weapons facility sealed beneath the ice of Eiselcross; where the scroll case was stored as ordinary stock and waited.

## Overview

Salsvault is an Aeorian weapons research facility sealed under the glacier of Eiselcross — a bioweapons laboratory from the pre-Calamity age. The company entered it not for the campaign's purposes but for an entirely ordinary one: someone was sick and the antidote was there. They found the scroll case among ordinary stock, priced at fifteen gold.

## Description

### The Location

Salsvault is one of the known Aeorian facilities beneath Eiselcross — the kind of site that appears in Cobalt Soul records with appropriate warnings. It is not a library or an archive. It is a weapons laboratory, which is precisely why the scroll case was placed there: a weapons laboratory is not the first place you look.

The Sourcebook's Part IV §IX addresses Salsvault specifically in the context of the scroll case — noting that Dhurvaq could not suppress the Aeorian research fragment inside without direct intervention in a sealed location. So it was left. The calculation was presumably that a weapons laboratory under a glacier was unlikely to be found by anyone who would know what to do with it.

### The Scroll Case

Someone, long ago, understood both the scroll case and the binding simultaneously — the binding and the key — and separated them deliberately. The case was placed in Salsvault not as a hiding place exactly, but as the best available approximation of invisibility: stored as ordinary stock, filed as ordinary inventory, priced at fifteen gold. See `items/the-scroll-case`.

It waited. The campaign began when four people went looking for an antidote.

### The Frigid Woe Phials

The company destroyed the Frigid Woe phials during the Salsvault events — a detail noted in the storyarch as one of the things they did before the campaign proper began. The phials were what brought them to Salsvault. Their destruction was incidental. The scroll case was what they left with.

## Player Knowledge

Players know: Salsvault is where they found the scroll case, priced fifteen gold among ordinary stock. They know it is a pre-Calamity Aeorian facility. They know someone was sick, and the antidote was there, and they went.

## DM-Only

The Sourcebook's cosmology author's assessment: no intelligence arranged this. No plan produced it. It is the purest accident in a chain of events that has otherwise been shaped at every point by someone's intention. The Captains cannot account for it. Dhurvaq cannot account for it. This may be exactly why the company can do what they need to do. They are the unaccountable variable.

The Aeorian fragment inside Salsvault — not the scroll case itself, but a research note about the nature of the thing the case relates to — was not suppressed by Dhurvaq because suppression would have required direct intervention in a sealed location. So it was left. "It is not a weapon. It is a condition. Available to be woken or to be held." The same hand that wrote the Maw's Aeorian fragment wrote this one.
